**My read on the concept:**

This is strong. Here's why it works structurally:

La Avispa is one of the most dramatically loaded characters in the whole project — former FSB, burned, defected through four countries, rose inside a Mexican cartel through sheer competence, and her dynamic with Spook is one of the few genuinely unresolved emotional threads ("Ask me again."). A DLC built around her origin and her world fills a gap nothing else does: it's the *underworld layer* of the conspiracy — the money, the trafficking infrastructure, the dark money clearing houses — told from inside rather than from Spook's external vantage point.

It also provides connective tissue for:
- The Montoya Farm / Shadow Underground architecture
- Los Sietes' role in the Goldberg extraction
- The Bisutri / El Tiburón organizational structure
- The favor La Avispa owes El Tiburón (terms undisclosed — perfect DLC thread to pull)

**One flag:** Yelena is Russian playing a Mexican cartel identity. Her story is fundamentally about *who she became* after the FSB burned her — not about cartel life per se. The DLC needs to hold that tension. It shouldn't feel like a narco story wearing a Project Warriors skin. It should feel like a spy story that happens to live inside a cartel.

---

**On the title — "Money or Lead" vs *Plata o Plomo*:**

Your instinct is right. *Plata o Plomo* is so thoroughly Escobar-coded (especially post-*Narcos*) that using it in Spanish as a DLC title would almost certainly read as a direct Escobar reference, which you don't want. It would set expectations the story can't and shouldn't meet.

**"Money or Lead"** in English works better because:
- It carries the meaning without the cultural baggage
- It's slightly defamiliarized — you have to think for a second, which is good
- It fits a character who is herself translated: Russian woman, Spanish cover, English title

**My honest take:** "Money or Lead" is clean and evocative, but it might read slightly generic on a game shelf. It doesn't signal *spy thriller inside a cartel* — it signals *crime game*. You might want to push one step further.

A possible alternative worth considering: **"Project Warriors: The Wasp"** — her callsign, her identity, her blade. Simple. Unambiguous protagonist framing. And "The Wasp" has its own menace without any Escobar association. Though I understand if you want the *Plata o Plomo* echo in the title — it does signal the moral world she operates in.

Could also be a subtitle: **"Project Warriors: Money or Lead — A La Avispa Story"** if you want both.
