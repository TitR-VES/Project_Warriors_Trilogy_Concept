SECTION 1 — BUILD SPECIFICATION
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Build name:     ¡HASTA LA VISTA!
                (always with Spanish punctuation and all caps when rendered
                 as the build name)
Manufacturer:   DynaCal Industries, Vallejo, CA, United States (fictional;
                fictionalized Remington)
Model:          DynaCal Remington 870 Tac-14 TACS II Custom
                (TACS = Tactical Attachment and Configuration System)
                Fictionalized shortened 870 Tac-14 platform.
                Visual base / real-world analog: Specna Arms SA-VGS2 gas
                shotgun replica, Half-Tan colorway, M-LOK fire grip + shuttle.
Caliber:        12 gauge
Capacity:       4+1 shells
Operation:      Pump-action
Fire mode:      Single
Color:          Half-Tan

ATTACHMENTS:
  Foregrip:     Black AFG-style aluminum angled low-profile foregrip with
                rear hand stop, mounted on the M-LOK along the bottom of the
                shortened shuttle's grip. Facilitates handling in follow-up
                shots. Attached by default; can be upgraded, or removed at
                the cost of some follow-up-shot handling.
  Iron sights:  Black Daniel Defense-pattern folding iron sights (front and
                rear), mounted at the ends of the top rail. Absolute
                co-witness with the XPM-5 RAGE when flipped up.
  Optic:        XpertMark XPM-5 RAGE — Compact Reflex Sight. (LOCKED)
                In-universe shorthand / alternate name: RAGE C1.
                Fictional maker; name derived from "Expert Marksman"
                (old company name). Rectangular window, reinforced 6061
                aluminum housing.
                Real-world analog: Aim-O FRENZY-style compact reflex.
                NOTE: replaces rejected "X-Mark" (real Remington trigger
                brand) and "ExMark / XM-5" (Exmark mowers; SIG XM5
                military designation). PURGE those forms on sight.
  Light:        SureFire (real brand, per series convention — GRAY GHOST
                precedent), model TBD (Scout-family candidate). Black,

                handguard, positioned at 1 o'clock roughly flush with the
                barrel. Wired pressure switch on the LEFT side of the
                machined-aluminum M-LOK handguard, directly above the
                shuttle. See Section 2.2 for the deliberate ergonomics note.
                NOTE: replaces rejected fictional "AccuShot" (real
                Leapers/UTG trademark) and "TruAim" (TRU/SIGHT internal

  Hardware:     2x QD sling eyelets — one at the front of the handguard

SECTION 2 — NARRATIVE PROVENANCE & DESIGN NOTES
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.1  PROVENANCE (LOCKED, Stefan 2026-07-18):
     The weapon was Frank Manola's personal shotgun. Frank — a huge fan of
     80s/90s Hollywood action movies, Terminator included — wrote the motto
     on the receiver himself; the build name is his T2 nod. After Frank's
     death in the Phalanx armored run, Chris is contacted by someone from
     Frank's circle (working idea: a buddy Frank knew from the shooting
     range) who gives Chris the gun to honor Frank's wishes — Frank had
     wanted Chris to have it at some point, "and that time was as good as
     ever now that he's gone."

     Motif note: this is the series' inherited-weapon motif in a second
     color. DON'T TREAD (Dale Pruett → Romeo) is a LIVING gift — an act of
     choosing a side. ¡HASTA LA VISTA! is a POSTHUMOUS gift — an act of
     remembrance. Keep the two emotionally distinct.

     Handwriting seed (WORKING LOCK, Stefan 2026-07-18 — variant choice
     open): seed Frank's marker handwriting ONCE before the handoff, via a
     hand-written note kept in the Rhino 6 van during the fateful run.
     Two variants:
       (a) a note visible in the van — e.g., a phone number for a
           technical specialist Frank knows at Phalanx;
       (b) PREFERRED: a note Frank hands to the player during an informal
           or personal conversation in a quieter part of the Rhino 6 run
           mission.
     The note contains a name, address, and phone number in Frank's
     handwriting. It persists as an examinable artifact the player can
     revisit later — visually indicating that Chris is, to some degree,
     still processing what happened that day with Frank and the attack.
     Dual function: emotional callback for Chris, AND a means for the
     player to compare the handwriting and recognize that the marker
     inscription on the gun matches Frank's hand. Radical minimalism —
     felt, not stated; no dialogue explains the match.
     OPEN (suggestion, not Stefan's): the name on the note could be the
     range-buddy intermediary of Section 4.4 — one artifact seeding both
     the handwriting and the person who later delivers the gun.

2.2  PRESSURE SWITCH ERGONOMICS (deliberate design, adjudicated):
     On a pump gun the support hand rides the moving shuttle, so the
     switch on the fixed handguard is reachable only when the shuttle is
     forward — i.e., in battery, ready to fire, which is exactly when
     illumination is wanted. Spec as intentional: "switch indexed to the
     support-hand thumb with shuttle forward." Wire routing stays clear of
     the shuttle stroke. Sling routing keeps clear of pump path and light
     beam under normal operation.

2.3  PLACEMENT & REWARD LOGIC (LOCKED direction):
     The Collapse DLC, mid-campaign story/objective completion reward —
     NOT a menu-ping progression unlock. A weapon carrying a previous
     owner's handwriting wants a narrative beat (the handoff scene). If
     the handoff lands while St. Louis's disintegration is accelerating,
     the ordinariness of the gesture — a man keeping a friend's promise —
     gains weight against the backdrop.

     Roster note: fills the shotgun gap in The Collapse build roster
     (LAST RESORT — M16A4+M203; SAVING GRACE — P226; MCX Rattler PDW).
