PURPOSE
Define the trilogy-level thematic progression and standardize how it is used in story development, campaign framing, franchise identity, and promotional language.

CANONICAL LINE
FRACTURE. RESISTANCE. RESTORATION.

CORE MEANING
The old order breaks.
People organize against that break.
A damaged future becomes possible again.

FRACTURE
Fracture is the breaking of systems once assumed stable: states, institutions, command structures, public trust, private certainty, and the ordinary expectation that tomorrow will resemble today.
It includes physical destruction and geopolitical shock, but also moral rupture, institutional compromise, hidden-system exposure, and psychological breakage.
Fracture is the collapse of the lie of normalcy.

RESISTANCE
Resistance is the organized answer to fracture.
It includes insurgency, refusal, clandestine action, preservation of people and knowledge, civic defiance, survival under occupation, mutual reliance, and active opposition to collapse or enemy control.
It is not only armed struggle. It is the point at which characters stop merely suffering collapse and begin pushing back against it.

RESTORATION
Restoration is the contested effort to rebuild legitimacy, continuity, civic life, trust, and hope after systemic ruin.
It is not a clean return, restoration of innocence, or reinstatement of the old order unchanged.
It does not mean everything lost can be repaired.
It means hope becomes credible again and the future can once more be fought for, governed, and lived in honestly.

INTERPRETATION RULE
These terms are directional emphases, not rigid one-game-only compartments.
- fracture remains present after the opening movement;
- resistance begins before the middle movement in some campaign lines;
- restoration in the final movement is a beginning, not completion.

CHARACTER-LINE FIT
- Romeo carries the civic, territorial, and national dimension.
- Ducky carries the institutional, alliance, military, and systems dimension.
- Spook carries the clandestine, systemic, and moral dimension.
The line must remain true across all three leads.

TONE RULE
Use the line with restraint, seriousness, and confidence.
- Fracture: severe, destabilizing, revelatory.
- Resistance: active, durable, morally stubborn.
- Restoration: hard-won, incomplete, credible.
Do not present Restoration as sentimental, triumphalist, utopian, or falsely conclusive.

STORY ALIGNMENT TEST
Aligned material:
- reveals breakage beyond surface spectacle;
- shows endurance, refusal, organization, preservation, or active opposition under pressure;
- points toward rebuilding, continuity, legitimacy, or credible future-making without overstating closure.

Misaligned material:
- treats collapse as spectacle only;
- treats resistance as firefights only;
- treats restoration as neat reset, full recovery, or uncomplicated victory.

PROMOTIONAL USE
Use FRACTURE. RESISTANCE. RESTORATION. as a load-bearing trilogy signature, not constant slogan wallpaper.
Appropriate uses include franchise overview language, story bibles, internal pitch framing, key-art strategy, trailer-card planning, long-form promotional copy, and late-cycle trilogy branding.
Before Game 3 is public, use the full line selectively. Earlier marketing may foreground Fracture or Resistance, or echo the structure indirectly, so Restoration lands with greater force later.

APPROVED SHORT DESCRIPTION
FRACTURE. RESISTANCE. RESTORATION. is the trilogy-level thematic arc of Project Warriors: the world breaks, people push back, and a damaged future becomes possible again.

APPROVED LANGUAGE BANK
Primary:
FRACTURE. RESISTANCE. RESTORATION.

Secondary:
A world broken. A people unbowed. A future fought back into being.
The old order fractured. Resistance followed. Restoration begins where survival stops being enough.
The war is not only for victory. It is for what can still be rebuilt after it.

Soft foreshadow:
Born in fracture. Forged in resistance.
Resistance is not the end of the story.
Survival wins time. What comes after must still be built.

PROHIBITED READINGS
Do not imply:
- utopian ending or total recovery;
- restoration of innocence;
- restoration of the old order unchanged;
- resistance as military action only;
- fracture as disaster imagery without civic, moral, institutional, or psychological consequence.

HOUSE STANDARD
Treat FRACTURE. RESISTANCE. RESTORATION. as the cleanest high-level thematic signature of the trilogy.
It should feel severe, clear, memorable, and earned.
Use it sparingly enough that it retains weight.
