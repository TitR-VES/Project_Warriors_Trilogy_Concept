ANGEL — PRONUNCIATION GUIDE
Project Warriors — Game 2 — Romeo Campaign
Cast / VO Support Document

DOCUMENT PURPOSE
This guide supports consistent pronunciation during recording for the ANGEL sequence.
It focuses on:
- character names
- callsigns
- place names
- radio / military terms that appear in the scene
- recommended read style for potentially ambiguous items

STYLE NOTE
This is a fictional military-action setting anchored in the United States.
Most reads should feel natural and contemporary rather than over-militarized.
Avoid exaggerated "movie radio voice" delivery.

========================================================================
PRIMARY NAMES AND TERMS
========================================================================

ALDRIDGE
Preferred spelling: A-L-D-R-I-D-G-E
Recommended pronunciation: AWL-dridge
Simple guide: "ALL-dridge"
Notes:
- Keep consistent with the locked spelling.
- Do not drift into "Aldrich."
- The second half should be a quick "dridge," not over-enunciated.

ROMEO
Recommended pronunciation: ROH-mee-oh
Notes:
- Standard English pronunciation.
- In radio context, can carry slight emphasis on first syllable.

SPECTRE FOUR ONE
Recommended pronunciation: SPEK-ter four one
Notes:
- "Spectre" with a clean "spek" opening.
- Avoid over-stylizing.
- "Four One" should usually be read as separate digits, not "forty-one."

AMERICAN FREEDOM
Recommended pronunciation: uh-MER-ih-kən FREE-dəm
Notes:
- Straight, sincere, understated.
- Do not turn this into a slogan read.

========================================================================
VEHICLES / WEAPONS / PLATFORM TERMS
========================================================================

F-150
Recommended pronunciation: F one-fifty
Alternate acceptable: F one hundred fifty
Preferred in-scene read: F one-fifty
Notes:
- Keep casual and natural.

M240B
Recommended pronunciation: em two-forty bravo
Notes:
- Standard spoken pattern.
- "Bravo" should be clean and professional, not exaggerated.

UAZ
Recommended pronunciation: oo-AHZ
Simple guide: "oo-AHZ"
Notes:
- Stress the second syllable.
- Do not spell out U-A-Z unless a specific line is rewritten that way.
- If a performer struggles, "truck" or "vehicle" can often replace it in live dialogue, but the preferred guide remains oo-AHZ.

AC-130
Recommended pronunciation: ay-see one-thirty
Notes:
- Standard and natural.
- Do not over-punch the letters.

GUNSHIP
Recommended pronunciation: GUN-ship
Notes:
- Straight read.

========================================================================
RADIO / MILITARY / AVIATION TERMS
========================================================================

AUTHENTICATE
Recommended pronunciation: aw-THEN-tih-kayt
Notes:
- Stress on "then."
- Keep clipped in radio use.

SIERRA-TWO-KILO
Recommended pronunciation: see-AIR-uh two KEE-loh
Notes:
- NATO alphabet read.
- "Sierra" natural American English.
- "Kilo" = KEE-loh, not KILL-oh.

ANGELS TWO
Recommended pronunciation: AYN-jəlz two
Notes:
- Aviation altitude shorthand.
- In this script, read naturally, not like a lecture.
- "Angels two" should sound like the speaker uses the term routinely.

ROVER
Recommended pronunciation: ROH-ver
Notes:
- Straight read.
- In military context, still just "ROH-ver."

PUSH FREQUENCY
Recommended pronunciation: push FREE-kwən-see
Notes:
- Natural technical phrase.
- No need to over-accent "push."

BANDITS
Recommended pronunciation: BAN-dits
Notes:
- Standard air combat shorthand for hostile aircraft or hostile contacts in stylized military dialogue.
- In this scene, deliver matter-of-factly.

EGRESSING
Recommended pronunciation: ee-GRESS-ing
Notes:
- Stress the second syllable.
- Keep smooth and professional.

TWELVE O'CLOCK
Recommended pronunciation: twelve oh-CLOCK
Notes:
- Standard directional call.
- Keep natural.

ON STATION
Recommended pronunciation: on STAY-shən
Notes:
- Standard military / aviation phrase.
- Underplay slightly.

========================================================================
DELIVERY NOTES FOR POSSIBLY TRICKY LINES
========================================================================

"ROMEO element, SPECTRE FOUR ONE, we are at your push frequency. Authenticate."
Notes:
- "ROMEO element" should be clear and slightly separated from the callsign.
- Brief pause after "SPECTRE FOUR ONE" is acceptable.
- "Authenticate" should sound procedural, not aggressive.

"SPECTRE FOUR ONE, this is ROMEO element. Authenticate Sierra-Two-Kilo."
Notes:
- Read the code group clearly.
- Avoid swallowing "Sierra."
- "Two" and "Kilo" should sound clean and distinct.

"ROMEO element, copy. SPECTRE FOUR ONE is at angels two, four minutes out. We have your position on ROVER. Stand by, over."
Notes:
- This line carries several technical terms in one sentence.
- Best approach is measured calm, with slight phrase breaks:
  1. "ROMEO element, copy."
  2. "SPECTRE FOUR ONE is at angels two, four minutes out."
  3. "We have your position on ROVER."
  4. "Stand by, over."

"ROMEO element, SPECTRE FOUR ONE. One minute. We are in your twelve o'clock. You should have us shortly."
Notes:
- "One minute" should be reassuring through certainty, not excitement.
- "Twelve o'clock" should stay natural.

"ROMEO element, SPECTRE FOUR ONE. BANDITS are egressing. You are clear. What else do you need, over?"
Notes:
- "Bandits" and "egressing" should not become flashy.
- The power of the line is in calm control.

"Don't mention it. American Freedom, over."
Notes:
- This is the signature line.
- Underplay it.
- The speaker should sound like he means it, not like he has been waiting all day to say it.

========================================================================
COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID
========================================================================

1. Do not say "Aldrich."
Preferred: "Aldridge."

2. Do not say "forty-one" for the callsign.
Preferred: "Four One."

3. Do not turn SPECTRE FOUR ONE into a movie trailer voice.
Keep him calm and real.

4. Do not overdo Southern accents for the truck crew.
Local flavor is fine. Caricature is not.

5. Do not over-pronounce every radio term.
Natural competence is better than forced crispness.
