ANGEL — DESIGNER-FACING TRIGGER SHEET
Project Warriors — Game 2 — Romeo Campaign
Late Act 2 / Exfil Chase Set Piece

PURPOSE
This document translates the locked ANGEL sequence into implementation-facing mission logic.
It is structured around states, trigger IDs, conditions, bark priority, fallback behavior, and protected beats.

CANON LOCK
- Aldridge spelling locked
- Vehicle: current-generation Ford F-150, quad cab
- Mounted weapon: M240B on improvised bed ring / pintle
- Player position: Romeo on gun
- Total ammo state: two cans total at chase start
- Enemy: three UAZ-type pursuers, at least one mounted
- Air support: AC-130 Spectre gunship, callsign SPECTRE FOUR ONE
- Protected closing line: "Don't mention it. American Freedom, over."

======================================================================

STATE A   Exfil_Start

Recommended playable duration: 5.5 to 7 minutes.

======================================================================
2. ACTOR MAP
======================================================================

PLAYER VEHICLE OCCUPANTS
01. Romeo - mounted gun, player-controlled
02. Driver
03. Front Passenger / Radio
04. Aldridge - rear left
05. Ammo Teammate - rear right / handoff role

ENEMY VEHICLES
E1. Lead gun truck - highest early threat
E2. Pressure truck - medium distance harasser
E3. Offset truck - wider angle / closure pressure

AIR ASSET
A1. AC-130 Spectre gunship - SPECTRE FOUR ONE

======================================================================
3. ROAD SEGMENT MAP
======================================================================

SEGMENT R1 - Settling straight
Purpose: initial orientation and first target acquisition.

SEGMENT R2 - Fenced bend section
Purpose: increase sway, introduce line-of-sight breakups.

SEGMENT R3 - Dip / rise
Purpose: ideal location for first belt depletion and reload fear.

SEGMENT R4 - Longer rearward lane
Purpose: lets player re-engage and understand the pursuit geometry.

SEGMENT R5 - Offset pressure section
Purpose: enemy stagger behavior becomes clearer.

SEGMENT R6 - Rough downhill approach
Purpose: maximum instability, strongest trough.

SEGMENT R7 - Open horizon corridor
Purpose: protected reveal space for AC-130.

======================================================================
4. GLOBAL RULES
======================================================================

GR-01
Do not show all three pursuers in full, clearly readable composition at the exact first visual moment.
Preferred cadence: dust -> one vehicle -> two -> three.

GR-02
Ammo scarcity is narrative and should not be softened by hidden reserve.

GR-03
Player should retain control during the reveal. Avoid hard-cut loss of agency.

GR-04
The AC-130 must be heard before it is clearly seen.

GR-05
Aftermath must be restrained. No cheering, no victory whoops, no tonal break.

GR-06
Do not checkpoint after the reveal. Protect run-up tension.

======================================================================
5. STATE-BY-STATE LOGIC
======================================================================

----------------------------------------------------------------------
STATE A - Exfil_Start
----------------------------------------------------------------------

TR_A_01_ControlGain
WHEN:
- player control is handed to Romeo on mounted gun
- truck speed already above chase threshold
- enemy fire audio already active

DO:
- enable mounted gun controls
- start vehicle shake profile A
- queue Bark Set A

BARK SET A - PRIORITY ORDER
A1 Driver: "Contact rear! Rear, rear, rear!"
A2 Front Passenger: "They stayed on us!"
A3 Ammo Teammate: "Romeo, on the gun!"

FAILSAFE
If A1 is blocked by overlapping SFX, force A2 within 1.0 sec and A3 within 2.0 sec.

TR_A_02_FirstVisual
WHEN:
- player yaws within rear acquisition arc
- E1 enters camera-eligible lane

DO:
- expose E1 clearly
- hold E2 for 1.0-1.8 sec
- hold E3 for 2.0-3.5 sec

BARK SET B - PRIORITY ORDER
B1 Front Passenger: "One truck--"
B2 Aldridge: "No. Three. Three total."
B3 Driver: "Lead one's mounted!"

FALLBACK
If player never yaws far enough rear within allowed window, auto-slew camera assist 10-15 degrees and fire B2/B3 anyway.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
STATE B - First_Engagement
----------------------------------------------------------------------

TR_B_01_AmmoCall
WHEN:
- player has aimed at a pursuing vehicle for 3-6 sec cumulative
- current state still B

DO:
- queue ammo scarcity line
- reveal ammo UI state if using diegetic/minimal HUD

BARK
B4 Ammo Teammate: "Romeo! Two cans total!"

FOLLOW-UP PRIORITY
B5 Driver: "Then make 'em hurt!"

FALLBACK
If B4 cannot fire due to combat overlap, force within 5 sec of first sustained trigger pull.

TR_B_02_LeadThreat
WHEN:
- E1 lead gun has valid suppressive lane
- player has not yet significantly damaged E1

DO:
- increase tracers from E1
- flag E1 as tutorialized high-priority threat

BARK SET C
C1 Driver: "Lead gun truck! Kill that gun!"
C2 Aldridge: "Don't chase all three. Break the one that can kill us."
C3 Ammo Teammate: "Short bursts!"

DESIGN INTENT
Player should feel effective here, but not dominant.
One meaningful disable is acceptable. Total encounter cleanup is not.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
STATE C - Reload_One_Vulnerability
----------------------------------------------------------------------

TR_C_01_RoadGetsUgly
WHEN:
- vehicle enters R2 or R3
- player has been firing at least one engagement window

DO:
- switch to shake profile B
- increase mounted sway
- enable harsher chassis bounce audio

OPTIONAL BARKS
C4 Driver: "Hold on!"
C5 Aldridge: "Road drops here!"

TR_C_02_BeltOneLow
WHEN:
- first belt under low threshold

DO:
- light ammo-low signal
- prep reload choreography

OPTIONAL BARK
C6 Ammo Teammate: "You're running it down!"

TR_C_03_BeltOneEmpty
WHEN:
- first belt reaches zero

DO:
- hard dry-fire event
- force reload state
- temporarily increase enemy closure pressure
- disable instant, gamey reload snap

MANDATORY BARK PRIORITY
C7 Romeo or Ammo Teammate: "Reloading!"
C8 Driver: "Make it fast!"
C9 Aldridge: "They're gaining!"

RELOAD ONE TIMING
Target feel: 4.5 to 6.0 sec
Not all of that must be literal animation lock; some may be recovery vulnerability.

RELOAD ONE CHOREOGRAPHY
RLD1. Romeo ducks
RLD2. feed cover open
RLD3. can handoff enters frame
RLD4. bump interrupts seat
RLD5. reseat belt
RLD6. close cover
RLD7. charge
RLD8. restore fire

FALLBACK
If animation desync occurs, preserve audio order and do not skip the vulnerable beat.

CHECKPOINT
CP_01_ReloadOneComplete
Set only when the weapon is live again.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
STATE D - Mid_Chase_Triage
----------------------------------------------------------------------

TR_D_01_BackOnGun
WHEN:
- reload one complete
- Romeo regains sustained fire

DO:
- return agency
- push E2 and E3 into stronger tactical behaviors
- reduce chance of E1 instantly dying unless already critically damaged

BARK SET D
D1 Front Passenger: "Command says SPECTRE may be on call."
D2 Driver: "May?"
D3 Front Passenger: "That's what I got."

DESIGN INTENT
This is the realization state:
player now understands firepower will not fully solve the chase.

ENEMY BEHAVIOR RULES
E1 - hold center when possible
E2 - medium-distance suppress and punish tunnel vision
E3 - seek offset lane / alternate pressure angle

FALLBACK
If one enemy is already destroyed early, preserve three-axis pressure by making the surviving offset vehicle more aggressive in angle selection.

TR_D_02_TriageBarks
WHEN:
- player has split fire across multiple targets
OR
- E3 acquires strong angle
OR
- truck damage threshold hits mid band

BARK PRIORITY
D4 Aldridge: "Only shoot what matters!"
D5 Driver: "Pick one and break it!"

----------------------------------------------------------------------
STATE E - Last_Can_Commit
----------------------------------------------------------------------

TR_E_01_BeltTwoLow
WHEN:
- second belt under low threshold

OPTIONAL BARKS
E1 Driver: "How much you got left?"
E2 Ammo Teammate: "Not enough!"

TR_E_02_LastCanLine
WHEN:
- final handoff moment begins
OR
- if represented abstractly, at emotional commit point for last usable belt

MANDATORY BARK
E3 Ammo Teammate: "That's the last can. Make it count."

RULE
Do not allow any other high-value line to overlap E3.

DESIGN INTENT
This is the emotional pivot.
Silence around the line helps it land.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
STATE F - Final_Trough
----------------------------------------------------------------------

TR_F_01_NoMargin
WHEN:
- final belt nearly spent
OR
- scripted survival timer reached
OR
- truck damage enters high but non-fail band

DO:
- escalate chassis damage audio
- apply visible damage package if not already active
- increase perceived pressure while keeping survival window fair

VISIBLE DAMAGE PACKAGE
F1 cracked windshield centerline
F2 tailgate and rear body peppering
F3 one missing or shattered mirror/light assembly
F4 ring mount rattle layer

BARK SET F
F5 Driver: "We are not doing this much longer!"
F6 Aldridge: "Stay on the road!"
F7 Front Passenger: "Wait. Wait one--"

MIX RULE
At this point, enemy engines and impacts should briefly dominate over hope.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
STATE G - Spectre_Authenticate
----------------------------------------------------------------------

TR_G_01_SpectreContact
WHEN:
- state F established
- player has survived minimum trough duration
- not before emotional low point

MANDATORY RADIO ORDER
G1 SPECTRE FOUR ONE:
   "ROMEO element, SPECTRE FOUR ONE, we are at your push frequency. Authenticate."

G2 Front Passenger:
   "SPECTRE FOUR ONE, this is ROMEO element. Authenticate Sierra-Two-Kilo."

G3 SPECTRE FOUR ONE:
   "ROMEO element, copy. SPECTRE FOUR ONE is at angels two, four minutes out. We have your position on ROVER. Stand by, over."

RULE
Truck must still be under active threat during G1-G3.
Do not stage as safety.

CHECKPOINT
CP_02_SpectreConfirmed
Set after G3 completes.

FALLBACK
If radio line G2 is blocked by combat VO, temporarily duck non-essential barks and replay within 1.5 sec.

TR_G_02_OneMinuteOut
WHEN:
- 20 to 35 sec after authentication
- reveal corridor not yet entered

BARKS
G4 SPECTRE FOUR ONE:
   "ROMEO element, SPECTRE FOUR ONE. One minute. We are in your twelve o'clock. You should have us shortly."

G5 Aldridge or Driver:
   "You hear that?"

RULE
G5 should feel quiet and instinctive.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
STATE H - Reveal_Run
----------------------------------------------------------------------

TR_H_01_RoadOpensForward
WHEN:
- vehicle enters R7 open horizon corridor

DO:
- reduce forward clutter
- preserve skyline readability
- keep rear pressure audible
- start AC-130 pre-visual audio bed

AUDIO RULE
The turboprop body-rumble must begin before visual acquisition.

PLAYER AGENCY RULE
Retain full control. At most, use gentle aim damping or subtle visual bias.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
STATE I - Angel_Reveal
----------------------------------------------------------------------

TR_I_01_AngelReveal
WHEN:
- player camera enters forward reveal lane
OR
- fail-safe if player refuses to look forward for too long

VISUAL ORDER
I1 empty horizon
I2 aircraft mass rises over ridgeline
I3 silhouette resolves to AC-130
I4 maximum visual presence
I5 flare deployment
I6 angel-wing smoke pattern
I7 aircraft pushes through smoke

PROTECTED RULE
Do not cut away to a detached camera unless absolutely necessary.
Primary power of sequence comes from seeing it from Romeo's live position.

AUDIO DUCKING
For 2-3 sec around I5/I6, reduce perceived dominance of road combat mix.

DESIGN NOTE
Player can technically keep shooting, but almost nobody will.
That is desired behavior, not a problem.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
STATE J - Spectre_Intervention
----------------------------------------------------------------------

TR_J_01_GunshipEngages
WHEN:
- 1 to 3 sec after reveal peak

ENEMY OUTCOME SCRIPT
J1 one pursuer destroyed outright
J2 one pursuer disabled / crash / spin-off
J3 one pursuer breaks formation / aborts close pursuit

MANDATORY RADIO ORDER
J4 SPECTRE FOUR ONE:
   "ROMEO element, SPECTRE FOUR ONE. BANDITS are egressing. You are clear. What else do you need, over?"

J5 Front Passenger or Romeo:
   "SPECTRE FOUR ONE, I think we're good. How copy?"

J6 SPECTRE FOUR ONE:
   "ROMEO element, copy good. SPECTRE FOUR ONE will remain on station. You call, we answer. Over."

J7 Front Passenger or Romeo:
   "Thank you, SPECTRE FOUR ONE."

J8 SPECTRE FOUR ONE:
   "Don't mention it. American Freedom, over."

PROTECTED RULE
No one talks over J8.

FALLBACK
If a surviving enemy vehicle persists too long due to gameplay variance, force disengage behavior after J4 so that the emotional release is not delayed.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
STATE K - Aftermath_Exfil
----------------------------------------------------------------------

TR_K_01_ChaseBroken
WHEN:
- enemy close pursuit state collapsed
- truck still mobile

DO:
- reduce combat intensity
- keep truck moving
- preserve AC-130 orbit visibility if angle permits

BARKS
K1 Driver: "Everybody still here?"
K2 Aldridge: "Yeah."
K3 Aldridge: "Hell of a thing."

RULE
Keep space between K2 and K3.
Do not over-write the aftermath.

OBJECTIVE UPDATE
"Reach exfil route"

MISSION END OPTIONS
Preferred: truck turns onto concealed secondary route under tree cover.
Alternate: brief playable handoff, then fade on motion.

7. CHECKPOINTS
======================================================================

CP_00_GunControl
After mounted control stabilizes and first target is valid.

CP_01_ReloadOneComplete
After first vulnerable reload completes.

CP_02_SpectreConfirmed
After G3 radio line completes, before reveal run.

Do not place a checkpoint after the reveal.

======================================================================
8. FAILURE CONDITIONS
======================================================================

HARD FAIL
- Romeo killed
- truck disabled beyond scripted continuation threshold
- driver killed before intervention window
- catastrophic rollover / route loss

SOFT FAILURE PRESSURE
- poor target prioritization
- wasted ammo
- excessive exposure during reload windows
- allowing lead gun truck uninterrupted fire too long

Encounter should remain survivable without perfection, but not without discipline.

======================================================================
9. DIFFICULTY SCALING
======================================================================

EASY
- enemy gun accuracy reduced
- sway slightly softened
- reload one slightly shorter
- suppression more effective

NORMAL
- reference tuning

HARD
- lead gun truck punishes delay faster
- offset truck chooses better angles
- truck durability margin lower
- final trough tighter

RULE
Do not add extra ammo by difficulty.
Scarcity belongs to story logic.

======================================================================
11. IMPLEMENTATION NOTES
======================================================================

AI
Enemy vehicles should feel aggressive but road-constrained, not arcade-suicidal.

ANIMATION
Reload must feel physically awkward and exposed. Preserve vibration and handoff friction.

CAMERA
Most of the scene should live from Romeo's playable viewpoint.
Use assist, not takeover.

SOUND
The AC-130 should feel like mass entering the scene, not just another vehicle cue.

NARRATIVE
This sequence works because the player buys time rather than wins outright.
Do not accidentally tune it into a clean turret-victory chase.
