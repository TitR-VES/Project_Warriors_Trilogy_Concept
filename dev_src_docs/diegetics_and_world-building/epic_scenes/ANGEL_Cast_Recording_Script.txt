ANGEL — CAST RECORDING SCRIPT
Project Warriors — Game 2 — Romeo Campaign
Late Act 2 / Exfil Chase Set Piece

DOCUMENT PURPOSE
This is the cast-facing recording script for the ANGEL sequence.
It is organized by character for efficient studio use and includes:
- grouped lines by speaker
- line IDs
- priority markers
- delivery notes
- context notes
- light alternates where useful

CANON LOCK
- Aldridge spelling locked
- Vehicle: current-generation Ford F-150, quad cab
- Mount: improvised bed-mounted ring / pintle
- Weapon: M240B
- Ammo state: two cans total
- Player: Romeo on mounted gun
- Enemy: three UAZ-type pursuers, at least one mounted
- Air support: SPECTRE FOUR ONE, AC-130
- Protected closing line: "Don't mention it. American Freedom, over."

KEY
P1 = must fire / protected
P2 = strongly preferred
P3 = flexible
P4 = optional
CALM = controlled, composed
SHARP = urgent, clipped
STRAINED = under pressure but functional
LOW = quiet, instinctive
AWE = briefly unguarded / involuntary human reaction

GLOBAL PERFORMANCE NOTES
This scene should not sound like a joke-heavy action movie.
Nobody is quipping.
Nobody is performing for the audience.
The pressure is real, but the team is competent.

The truck crew should sound like people already in motion, already in trouble,
and already used to operating under stress.

SPECTRE FOUR ONE should sound like a sharp contrast to the truck interior:
calm, professional, procedural, but recognizably human.

========================================================================
CHARACTER: DRIVER
========================================================================

ROLE SUMMARY
The Driver is keeping the truck alive under terrible conditions. He is practical,
fast, and stretched near his limit, but he does not panic. He should sound like
someone whose attention is split between speed, traction, incoming fire, and
not killing everyone in the truck by losing the road.

READ STYLE
- urgent
- economical
- never theatrical
- Kentucky / local flavor is fine if subtle, but do not overplay accent

- functional

Kentucky-specific rather than generically military.

- emotionally contained
- not showy, not speechifying

CHARACTER: ROMEO
========================================================================

ROLE SUMMARY
Romeo is sparse here. If voiced at all during the chase, he should be functional and
economical. He is too occupied to narrate his feelings. Use him sparingly so that when
he does speak, it counts.

READ STYLE

- low-emotion surface, high focus underneath
- no chatter

========================================================================
CHARACTER: SPECTRE FOUR ONE
========================================================================

ROLE SUMMARY
SPECTRE FOUR ONE is the tonal counterweight to the truck chaos. He must sound like an
experienced American crewman: calm, clipped, reliable, professional. Not robotic, not
swaggering, not movie-cool. His confidence comes from competence, not performance.

READ STYLE
- calm under pressure
- crisp procedural delivery
- steady pacing
- slight warmth permitted after the strike, but stay disciplined

SESSION NOTES FOR DIRECTOR
========================================================================

1. Keep the truck interior voices grounded and non-performative.
2. Protect the quiet around:
   - "Romeo! Two cans total!"
   - "That's the last can. Make it count."
   - first SPECTRE contact
   - "Don't mention it. American Freedom, over."
3. Aldridge should sound like a real teammate, not a tagged-on plot passenger.
4. SPECTRE FOUR ONE should not sound like a one-liner machine. Underplay him.
5. Final aftermath lines should feel smaller than the reveal, not larger.
