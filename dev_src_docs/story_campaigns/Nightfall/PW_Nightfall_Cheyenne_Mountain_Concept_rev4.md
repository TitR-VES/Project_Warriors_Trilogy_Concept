## 0. POSITIONING

The Cheyenne Mountain / NORAD raid is the **final mission of Spook's Nightfall campaign** and the **shared culmination event of the entire Nightfall story**. Ducky and Lupin's campaign converges on the same final operation from its own perspective (see §8). This document covers Spook's version as primary, with the synchronization architecture sketched for both.

Era: long after U.S. federal collapse. Tone lock (repo): **rare heavy violence; expedition / stealth / survival-intelligence pressure** — this applies to *all* perspectives, including Ducky and Lupin's. No loud-assault version exists.

## 1. WORLD STATE & STAKES (per handoff — locked frame)

- The former U.S. is fragmented: territorial blocs, independent successor territories, owner-class enclaves, Federal Loyalist holdouts.
- The legitimate continuity government survives in exile under **Ainsley Taylor** in the United Kingdom, supported by scattered loyalist military and intelligence remnants.
- The antagonist is not an army but the **owner-class / Sovereign Lattice / Network State**: private cities, elite compounds, data centers, security-AI systems, old blackmail networks, continuity-regime infrastructure — attempting to rebuild a KINGMAKER-style shadow-power system by recovering buried archives, credentials, blackmail material, shell-finance access, and coercive infrastructure.
- Spook's campaign spine: **buried information** — what should be preserved, what exposed, what destroyed so it can never be used again. Triggered by a dead connection tied to Tripper and Montoya Farm beneath Dawnstar; traced through Deluvia/Damhaven → Sazan Island → Prague/Overlander routes → Scorched Crescent/Dubai "dead finances" → **Cheyenne Mountain**.

## 2. THE MOUNTAIN'S CONDITION — a four-layer target (locked frame)

Cheyenne Mountain has not been conquered; it has been **colonized from the inside**. Owner-class security-AI companies and continuity-regime interests are woven into its access control, surveillance, decision-support, and security architecture.

1. **The physical layer** — a hardened Cold War bunker complex, still doing its job.
2. **The human layer** — captive professionals: technical and military staff maintaining critical systems under a corporate regime they do not morally control. Loyalty-vetted, pretending compliance, keeping their heads down. Not ideological collaborators; not allies either — *witnesses*.
3. **The corporate security-AI control layer** — the true garrison. It watches, gates, and escalates. **[LOCKED 2026-07-07]** The contractor is **PHALANX** — introduced in The Collapse DLC as a secure transit company (Chris Haywood, that DLC's protagonist, initially works for them). By the Nightfall era PHALANX has grown government-entangled and holds the facility contract: it "hired" the skeleton crew of former Air Force staff who man the mountain. That employment relationship is the captivity — and why the staff's quiet loyalty runs to the Federal Loyalists, not their employer.
4. **The strategic node** — surviving command infrastructure the owner class intends to make the heart of a private post-American regime.

The mission is **not "storm the bunker."** It is: restore enough lawful access, bypass the private control layer, and deny the mountain to private sovereignty.

## 3. MISSION PURPOSE & MORAL ENDPOINT (locked frame)

Objective: use Cheyenne Mountain's surviving strategic infrastructure **against** the Sovereign Lattice — launch/enable **conventional strategic strikes (explicitly NOT nuclear)** against major fictionalized owner-class command-processing, data-center, surveillance, and coercive infrastructure nodes.

**Why conventional [rationale LOCKED 2026-07-07]:** the targets are overwhelmingly on the soil of the former United States — you do not nuke your own country, even a fragmented one. And conventional is *sufficient*: ground-penetrating munitions irreparably kill hardened data centers; coastal and shallow-underwater nodes can be structurally wrecked; most surface nodes sit inside the former US. The restraint is both moral and practical — which is exactly the register the finale wants. (Do NOT use a "nuclear capability is dead/decommissioned" beat; that framing is retired.)

**The strike package [ADDED 2026-07-07]:** the launches are executed by the team with loyalist help, firing **MRC (Mid-Range Capability, Typhon-type) ground-based launchers** — conventional Tomahawk/SM-6-class missiles — from **several dispersed emplacement sites** across the former US. The batteries were placed pre-collapse; their original units are long defunct. **Custodian [LOCKED 2026-07-07]:** the **Sovereign Lattice itself maintains them as assets** — inertial upkeep plays a part, but control runs through the Lattice's strategic AI systems and integrated government data platforms. Implication: the finale's launch is a **seizure** — restored lawful command authority, threaded through Soraya's access path, flips the batteries' allegiance, and the Lattice is destroyed with the arsenal it kept for itself. Cheyenne Mountain fires nothing; it remembers where the guns are buried and holds the legacy authority the Lattice's platforms never fully purged. Primary targets: the owner-class data centers powering the Network State's compute. (Remaining texture flag: whether some batteries fail to answer the wake-up — partial failure would make the launch feel earned rather than push-button.)

Moral endpoint, all five clauses protected:
- preserve enough truth and evidence for the future;
- deny the owner class KINGMAKER-style blackmail and credentials;
- destroy the infrastructure that lets private power become sovereign;
- restore a limited but meaningful line of lawful continuity via Federal Loyalist / Taylor exile channels;
- **no restoration fantasy.** The Union is not fixed. The private continuity regime is decisively wounded, nothing more.

Thematic core (protected, verbatim from handoff): *The old state failed. The private replacement is worse. The only future worth preserving requires destroying the machinery of coercion without destroying the possibility of lawful memory.*

## 5. INFILTRATION ROUTE (locked frame + design development)

Normal access points are out — too heavily monitored by the AI layer. Sakhalin's old files + Deluvian fragments imply an **old cave / reservoir-adjacent route** connected indirectly to the complex's spring-fed freshwater supply. Old, partially flooded, officially "blocked or impassable" — which is exactly why the defenders trust it. **Fictionalized; never a real-world exploit** (design guardrail: keep geology and breaching at story-level detail, no procedure).

**Design development (new):**
- **Three movements in the approach:** (a) dry cave — navigation, cold, Sakhalin reading rock like text; (b) flooded galleries — limited air logic, marker lights as breadcrumbs, the route "worse than expected" beat; (c) the man-made threshold — the moment natural rock gives way to poured concrete and a Cold War-era intake, and the rules change from *survive the mountain* to *don't wake the machine*.
- **The grate set piece:** Sakhalin's sealed breaching kit pays off exactly once. Play it as tension-and-patience, not action — a long, silent, procedural beat where the player holds position and watches a professional work. KYSC audio grammar applies: **silence is the tell.** Regulator breathing as the only rhythm; when Sakhalin stops working, that's the cue something's wrong.
- **Reservoir arrival:** surfacing inside the football-field freshwater reservoir chamber — black water, service lights, the maintenance rowboat, and (kept from rev1, still true and still humanizing) the plastic decoy ducks, decades old now. First sight of the "living" mountain from underneath.

## 6. MISSION STRUCTURE — the eight beats, developed for play

1. **Planning / briefing (Deluvia or loyalist forward site).** Establishes route, uncertainty, stakes; Sakhalin's intelligence reveal; Soraya frames the AI layer; loyalists frame legitimacy. Player choice surface: what preparation to weight (route kit vs. access credentials vs. evidence-handling) — a light loadout-of-intent choice that colors later beats rather than gating them.
2. **Cave / flooded approach.** As §5. Pure expedition pressure. No enemies — the mountain itself is the antagonist here. (Absence principle: the scariest stretch of the finale has nothing in it.)
3. **Reservoir / utility threshold.** Transition into human-built infrastructure. Teach the new stealth grammar: the threat is not patrols but *instrumentation* — flow sensors, door logs, camera sight-lines, badge gates. The AI layer notices *anomalies*, not silhouettes.
4. **Internal compromise reveal.** Traverse the spring-mounted building grid (kept from rev1: the 18-inch gaps, spring forest, gangplanks as Spook's traversal layer). The player *sees* the four-layer truth: captive staff at consoles; compliance badges; corporate signage bolted over USAF stencils; a break room where technicians eat in silence under a camera. **Witness mechanic (new proposal):** captive staff are neither hostile nor friendly. They will not fight; if the AI interrogates them, they *will* report — coerced. Spook can pass unseen, or expose himself to individuals judged (via observation intel) safe to trust. Small, reversible trust gambles; no morality meter, just consequences in beats 5–6.
5. **Cyber / access fight.** Soraya + Spook vs. the AI layer. Objective is narrow: **create one lawful access path** and keep the corporate layer from locking the mountain down. Mechanically: an escalation ladder, not an alarm bar — the AI moves through containment postures (observe → restrict → seal → purge), each visibly changing the map's grammar (doors, lighting, staff behavior). Full stealth keeps it at "observe." Mistakes cost geography, not just difficulty. (This is rev1's deniability system, correctly re-aimed: the thing you're denying is the *machine's certainty*.)
6. **Command restoration / strike authorization.** The mission's heart, and the synchronization node (§8). Lawful command reassertion is deliberately **physical and human**: surviving two-man-rule hardware, credentials that need living, willing loyalist officers present, chain-of-custody as gameplay. While Spook and Soraya open the path from inside, Ducky and Lupin's element escorts and protects the loyalist command group converging on the battle deck. The strike package is assembled on the old tiered command deck — the room doing the one thing it was built for, aimed at data centers instead of missiles.
7. **Final moral choice / execution.** During strike preparation, Spook reaches the recovered KINGMAKER-legacy archive the Lattice was assembling in the mountain. Three-way choice, resolving the campaign theme **[LOCKED as proposed, 2026-07-07]**:
   - **Burn it all** — coercion machinery *and* evidence die together; the future is safe from it and blind to it.
   - **Seal it** — destroy the credentials/blackmail instruments, preserve the evidentiary record under loyalist/Taylor custody: lawful memory without live ammunition. (The "intended" ending, if any — it is the only one that satisfies all five moral-endpoint clauses.)
   - **Expose it** — broadcast everything, now, unredacted; truth as a weapon with no custodian. Cheapest, loudest, and the epilogue quietly shows the cost.
   The strikes launch regardless; the choice governs *what is left behind* — exactly the handoff's framing.
8. **Exfil — the mountain answers back [LOCKED 2026-07-07, revised].** As the strike launch executes, the PHALANX AI partially regains control, breaking through Soraya's countermeasures, and initiates containment: **localized containment first** — compartment-by-compartment seals cutting the building grid into cells — **escalating to the full-complex button-up**, the big seal at the end. Security drones and armed PHALANX personnel converge under protocol. The team exfils **upward, to the surface**, through the main complex and out the curved access tunnel — the mission's first and only traversal of the mountain's iconic front geography. In through the mountain's veins, out through its mouth. The final blast-door close is the countdown clock; door control is **contested** — the loyalists' partial lawful access from beat 6 is the only reason there is a countdown at all instead of an instant seal (this also answers the "why can't the AI just close everything" question at story level; manual/mechanical interlocks as backup texture). **Sakhalin holds the rear** at a containment choke so the team can reach the tunnel, is overwhelmed, and ends on the wrong side of the final door — his last words arrive through comms as the 25-ton door seals, the "coffin lid" audio beat landing on him. **Final line [LOCKED 2026-07-07]:** *"We're almost out of time. Move! See you on the other side."* Nothing follows — no death sound, only the seal (KYSC grammar). The guide who found the secret way in dies holding the front door open. Everyone else makes it out into open air. **[Synchronization proposal]** In Ducky & Lupin's version, their element is holding the north portal mouth from outside — the two lanes meet, for the only time, at the tunnel exit.
9. **End state.** The Sovereign Lattice loses its command/control advantage. The mountain is denied, not "saved." The lawful thread survives. Epilogue textures vary by beat-7 choice. No sunrise-over-the-flag shot; per the Nightfall rulebook, restraint is the register (Long Shadows discipline applies — the finale earns one quiet human image, once).

## 7. ATMOSPHERE & ENVIRONMENTAL STORYTELLING (salvaged from rev1, era-shifted)

- **Real facility as skeleton:** curved access tunnel, 25-ton blast doors, three main chambers + four cross-tunnels, 15 spring-mounted steel buildings, reservoirs, diesel level with locomotive-sized generators. Compress distances; keep every iconic beat legible. Authenticity of feel over authenticity of survey.
- **Colonized, not ruined:** the horror is renovation. Biometric turnstiles set into 1960s blast corridors; corporate wellness-compliance posters over USAF safety stencils; a "vetting renewal" notice board in staff quarters; ration tokens next to family photos. The mountain is *maintained* — that's what's wrong with it.
- **Absence as statement:** the battle deck runs on a skeleton crew for a facility this size; the AI needs few humans. Half the building grid is dark, springs still faithfully holding up buildings nobody enters. The most fortified place on the continent is quiet — because the war for it was won without a shot, years ago, in access-control software.
- **Lighting/sound:** institutional flat light in AI-controlled zones (hostile to stealth — the player makes darkness, doesn't find it); the water route pitch-black and breathing-scored; blast-door hydraulics reserved as the finale's percussion. If the AI reaches "seal" posture, the button-up becomes the climax's clock — doors closing on Ducky and Lupin's element while Spook fights to hold one path open.

## 8. THREE-PERSPECTIVE ARCHITECTURE — synchronized, not staggered

**Correction from rev1:** not RE2-style staggered timelines. Both campaign versions depict the **same operation, same hours, simultaneously** — two lanes of one synchronized plan, each complete on its own, interlocking in the player's head.

- **Spook's version (this doc):** route, systems, evidence, judgment. Underwater in; undercity traversal; the AI fight; the archive choice. Combat rare and costly.
- **Ducky & Lupin's version:** the protection lane — moving the loyalist command element to the mountain and onto the battle deck alive; ground-level survival pressure; the human cost of holding a corridor open while someone else's clock runs. Still Nightfall-toned: sparse, heavy-when-it-happens violence; never a blitz.
- **Synchronization touchpoints (proposals):** shared radio crosstalk heard from opposite sides; Spook watching Ducky/Lupin's approach on surveillance monitors he dare not touch; a door that opens for them "by itself" (Spook, unseen, on the other side); the strike authorization scene playable from both rooms; the button-up threatening one lane in each version. Persistent world-state flags carry across versions (staff trusted/exposed, AI posture reached, archive choice) so the second playthrough — either order — reads as revelation, not repetition.
- **Order-agnostic design rule:** neither version spoils the other's reveal. Spook's version withholds the protection lane's losses; Ducky/Lupin's version never explains *why* the doors opened or what was in the archive. Only the player who finishes both holds the whole night.
