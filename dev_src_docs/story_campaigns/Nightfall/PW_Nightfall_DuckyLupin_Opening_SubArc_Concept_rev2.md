## 0. POSITIONING

This is the **opening sub-arc of Ducky & Lupin's Nightfall campaign** — the front door to the campaign whose final act is the protection lane of the Cheyenne Mountain operation (Cheyenne rev4 §8).

**Campaign thesis [LOCKED]:** the campaign opens protecting the *idea* of lawful continuity — the exile government's legitimacy at the summit — and closes protecting its *last physical instrument* — the loyalist command element on the Cheyenne battle deck. Spook's Nightfall campaign is about custody of information. Ducky and Lupin's is about custody of people.

Tone lock (repo, governs all Nightfall perspectives): **rare heavy violence; expedition / stealth / survival-intelligence pressure.** This sub-arc is tradecraft versus tradecraft. No loud-assault version exists.

## 1. WORLD STATE — WHAT THE SUMMIT IS

- The legitimate US continuity government survives in exile under **Ainsley Taylor** in the United Kingdom (Cheyenne rev4 §1).
- The **Euro-American Cooperation Summit** convenes at the **Queen Elizabeth II Centre, Westminster, London** — European governments and the Taylor exile government negotiating recognition, aid, trade, and security cooperation.
- **[LOCKED]** Successor-territory observers are present — **as attendees only, not official guests.** (A diplomatic distinction the territorial delegations resent, and texture for the floor: the people who inherited the country, seated below the government that lost it.)
- The summit is the political embodiment of transatlantic legitimacy. A successful attack does not merely kill delegates — it kills European appetite for backing the exile government, in one morning, on camera.
- **Flag:** whether Ainsley Taylor attends in person or is represented — TBD.

## 2. THE NIGHTFALL-ERA NAF [LOCKED]

Following the territorial split-up of the lower 48 after the federal Collapse and the Fragmentation Years, the **New American Frontier** became less of a frontline force and more of an **operationally clandestine organization**:

- **Internal ideology:** ensuring an equal balance of power and non-domination between the new territorial entities.
- **External goal:** eliminating the US federal government in exile — the last remnant of the NAF's ideological enemy no. 1.

**Design note:** this NAF is a *rival theory of what comes after America*, not a militia remnant. Treat NAF operatives with tradecraft competence — cells, cutouts, legends, counter-surveillance — never woods-militia coded.

### 2.1 Leadership arc [LOCKED]

- **Sunny** ordered the NAF stand-down during the Shreveport armed insurrection (Game 4 endgame) → captured, tried, and imprisoned by federal authorities → **released after the federal Collapse**, resumed her NAF role. Her vindication is structural: the state that jailed her ceased to exist.
- **Pacer** was killed midway through the Fragmentation Years in a **loyalist missile strike on his security convoy**. The loyalist forces involved included many former **American Freedom Corps** fighters.
- **[LOCKED — DRAMATIC LANDMINE, handle deliberately]:** **BG Ramón Álvarez was part of the chain of command that authorized the strike on Pacer.** Reveal placement and mechanism TBD — this thread must be detonated deliberately or not at all. Implications: (a) the QEII plot Ducky prevents is retribution inside a cycle Romeo's chain helped start; (b) NAF holds maximum personal motive against Álvarez specifically, live ordnance for the Cheyenne finale where Romeo is the remote authorizing figure and Ducky/Lupin protect the loyalist command element; (c) Ducky's relationship to Romeo carries silent weight once the player knows.
- **Sunny and Hazzard** are the only two surviving original leaders. They are joined by **Steven Holger "Thorn" Sorenson**, a younger adept who made his name **finding and destroying multiple hidden loyalist cells** since the early Fragmentation Years.
- The trio shifted NAF to clandestine operations built on Thorn's expertise. **This covert, low-profile NAF runs the European overseas cell that plots the QEII Centre bombing.**
- **[LOCKED]** **Duke Montgomery** becomes a Steve Bannon-like figure and **runs propaganda production at the NAF** — the movement's media strategist and narrative engine. (His machinery is what converts the failed bombing into a partial ideological win — see §7.)
- **Flag:** River and Static fates — implied dead by "only two surviving original leaders"; unspecified.

### 2.2 Thorn Sorenson [LOCKED]

- Remote coordinator, based in the former US for most of the sub-arc, directing the European cell through cutouts.
- **Near-miss encounter [LOCKED]:** Thorn briefly visits the cell's safe house — **an apartment in Barmbek, Hamburg, Germany** — while Ducky and Lupin have it under surveillance (§5, Mission 2). See §2.4 near-miss grammar.
- The counterintelligence predator inverted: the man who found hidden cells now builds them. Every cutout, legend, and counter-surveillance habit the European cell uses is his signature.
- **[LOCKED]** **Thorn survives the sub-arc.** The cell falls; the architect does not. He is the campaign's recurring human antagonist face beneath the faceless Lattice.
- **[LOCKED in principle]** The Sovereign Lattice quietly enabled parts of Thorn's cell-hunting legend — the owner class also benefits from the elimination of the federal government in exile, and eliminating hidden loyalist cells served both agendas. Some of his early finds were fed by Lattice-adjacent data. **The man who prides himself on finding hidden things never found the hand feeding him.** Mechanics, extent, and whether/when this is ever revealed — TBD.

### 2.3 Sunny's sanction [LOCKED]

Sunny sanctions the summit strike as **eye-for-an-eye retribution** for the loyalist decapitation strike on Pacer, and as an opportunity for the NAF to win a **major ideological and strategic victory**.

**Design note — the blind spot:** the symmetry Sunny sees is false. Pacer's convoy was a military-adjacent target; the summit is full of European delegates, staff, and civilians on allied foreign soil. Whether anyone inside the organization sees this — and acts on it — is the seam the sub-arc exploits (§5, the doubter).

### 2.4 The near-miss grammar [LOCKED as approach]

The Barmbek near-miss is understood **only retroactively**. During surveillance, Thorn is a face without a name — an unremarked visitor photographed through a window or logged in passing, gone within hours. Identification comes later, from campaign-core intelligence work. The player who replays the mission realizes they were meters from the architect and let him walk. No chase, no confrontation, no musical sting at the time. Nightfall grammar: the significance is silent when it happens.

## 3. THE PLOT — OUTSOURCED DENIABILITY

The bombers are genuine believers: NAF clandestine operatives and radicalized members of the **American diaspora in Europe** — refugees of the Collapse, displaced and bitter, who see the exile government as the traitors who fled while the country burned. (The post-collapse American exile community in Europe — churches, aid queues, expat bars, remittance networks — is the water the cell swims in.)

But the **enablement** — financing corridors, hard-to-source components, access and vetting anomalies around the summit — traces into shell finance and security-contractor access patterns that Ducky and Lupin will only later recognize as **Sovereign Lattice fingerprints**.

**This is not a false flag.** It is **outsourced deniability**: real zealots, armed and aimed by someone who profits either way. NAF's two goals — no restoration, no single hegemon — are objectively aligned with Lattice interests. The Lattice does not need to control NAF; it only needs to *enable* it at chosen moments. The manipulation is invisible because the interests genuinely converge.

The cruelest realization, deferred to the campaign core: proving the link is nearly impossible, **because NAF wanted to do it anyway.**

## 4. THE CONTRACT

- Post-collapse UK intelligence is stretched thin hosting a government-in-exile half of Europe has not decided how to recognize. It needs operators who can read American extremist networks from the inside, are deniable, and are not British.
- **Ducky and Lupin** are contracted to dig into leads indicating an NAF-backed plot against the summit.

### 4.1 The handler [LOCKED: identity · backstory pass pending]

**Samuel Jackson Chiwa** — second-generation Kenyan British, from a military family. **MI6** officer; acts as the handler for Ducky and Lupin.

- **Realism note [PROPOSED]:** a plot on UK soil is primarily MI5/JTAC turf; MI6 runs foreign networks and continental operations. Cleanest framing that keeps Chiwa MI6: he is **seconded to a joint MI5/MI6 task force** stood up for the summit — and MI6 specifically owns the *foreign contractor* relationship (Ducky and Lupin operating in Hamburg is exactly his lane). This also gives Chiwa built-in interservice friction to play against.
- Recurring campaign figure; the liaison node between the contractors, UK services, and Taylor's exile apparatus.
- **Flag:** dedicated backstory/character pass (military family texture, why he champions two non-British contractors, his personal read on the exile government's presence).

### 4.2 Team composition [LOCKED]

Varies by expedition and mission context — Ducky and Lupin operate mostly within a team alongside trusted allies, local authority, or intelligence-adjacent characters; in some cases it is just the two of them.

## 5. MISSION STRUCTURE [3-mission shape LOCKED for now]

### Mission 1 — "QUIET AMERICANS" (working title) — London, investigation
- Contract briefing with Chiwa; the summit stakes and the thinness of the initial leads.
- Investigation and surveillance inside the American diaspora community in London — network mapping, a suspected facilitator, counter-surveillance pressure (the player's first taste of Thorn's signature without knowing whose it is).
- **The doubter's leak [LOCKED]:** the investigation's critical early detail originates from an **NAF-adjacent person who leaks to UK intelligence** hoping to bargain for help with a future defection. Ducky and Lupin work the product without ever learning the source's identity (see §6.1).
- Near-zero gunfire. Intelligence pressure carries the mission.
- Output: the trail points to a continental logistics node and a safe house in Hamburg.
- Team: Ducky + Lupin + UK surveillance support.

### Mission 2 — "HAMBURG BY NIGHT" [title LOCKED] — Hamburg, the cell
- Surveillance and eventual penetration of the cell's safe-house apartment in **Barmbek**, with or without German local-authority cooperation (friction texture: post-collapse German services have their own view of American exiles and American contractors).
- **The Thorn near-miss** (§2.4) happens here — unrecognized at the time.
- Enablement anomalies surface: financing and component sourcing beyond NAF's visible means. **[LOCKED]** The component route conspicuously **avoided Deluvia/Damhaven** because of the hazardous-trade rule — and the detour itself is the detectable anomaly.
- Split-up mechanic application: Ducky works the ground while Lupin holds overwatch across the street — path clearing and cover as collaborative play (transition mechanic carried forward per lock).
- Ends with partial cell roll-up — but the device, the emplacement team, and the facilitator are already in motion toward London.

### Mission 3 — "SPECIAL RELATIONSHIP" (working title) — London, summit day
- Queen Elizabeth II Centre, summit morning. Close protection, anomaly-hunting, race against the clock through the venue's back-of-house world.
- **Guardrail (hard):** all device, placement, and security-circumvention detail stays at **story level only** — real venue; no procedural or operational specificity, same discipline as the Cheyenne route rule.
- Violence, when it comes, is contained, close, and costly — Nightfall register. The bombing is **narrowly prevented**. The prevention should feel like minutes, not seconds-to-zero spectacle.

## 6. AFTERMATH — THE COST AND THE PULL

### 6.1 The doubter's long arc [LOCKED]

The leaker's identity is **never revealed in the sub-arc**. They surface much later in the story, having somehow managed to defect — **turning up in Deluvia**. Only then does the source of the original break become clear. (Deluvia sits on Spook's campaign map — this is a deliberate cross-campaign touchpoint; adjudicate later whether the recognition scene belongs to Ducky/Lupin returning or to Spook's lane, and the doubter's name/identity.)

### 6.2 The political cost of prevention [LOCKED]

The bombing fails — and the win still costs the exile government its footing:

- The government in exile **no longer feels safe** in its temporary home country.
- The British turn toward **stricter risk-mitigation policies** toward US exile officials: temporary suspension of some diplomatic privileges, downgraded security clearances for some US general staff personnel, and similar measures — the host recalculating the price of hospitality.
- International participants openly question the **UK's capability to combat national security threats**, damaging the host's standing.
- Back in America, **NAF hardliners spin the attempt — unsuccessful as it was — into a partial ideological win** (Duke Montgomery's production machine, §2.1): *they proved the exiles are safe nowhere, and that the special relationship has a price tag.*

Nightfall grammar: the victory saves lives and still loses ground. No triumph exists in this game.

### 6.3 The pull into the campaign core

- The facilitator dies or evaporates behind institutional protection before he can be taken. UK intelligence closes the case: bombing stopped, cell rolled up, politically finished.
- One recovered artifact — a credential chain, a payment corridor, a name — points somewhere nobody wants to look.
- **[LOCKED]** The artifact eventually links to the **dead-financier data** (the Dubai layer of Spook's campaign), and later helps tie the NAF bombing to the **Sovereign Lattice**.
- It is the exile government's loyalist intelligence — not the British — who quietly re-contract Ducky and Lupin to keep pulling. The campaign core begins.
- Both campaigns are now unknowingly holding ends of the same rope; they meet only at the north portal of Cheyenne Mountain (rev4 §8).
