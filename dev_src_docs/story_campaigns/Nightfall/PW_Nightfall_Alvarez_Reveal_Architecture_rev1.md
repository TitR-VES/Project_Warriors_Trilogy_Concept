## 1. THE FACT [LOCKED]

Midway through the Fragmentation Years, Pacer — original NAF leader — was killed in a loyalist missile strike on his security convoy. The loyalist forces included many former American Freedom Corps fighters. **BG Ramón Álvarez (pre-BG at the time) was part of the chain of command that authorized the strike.** He did not originate the decision; it came to him from higher up as well. In his view: a soldier engaging a legitimate enemy target under standing doctrine.

## 2. WORLD CANON — THE CALGARY ERA [LOCKED]

In the early exile period, the US federal government in exile was based in **Calgary, Alberta, Canada**. President Ainsley Taylor, his cabinet and staff relocated there with the AFC and other US military loyalist forces. The exile apparatus was well organized and highly capable of aggressively protecting its safety, with very good intelligence capabilities — including a doctrine of **preemptive strikes to neutralize emerging organized armed actors** that threatened the exile government's safety.

This doctrine is the context for the Pacer strike — and the reason the NAF never retaliated against Álvarez directly: **the NAF suspected his involvement but never had hard proof**, and in the Calgary era, moving against the loyalists was close to suicidal.

## 5. THE ARCHIVE LOCK — KNOWING ≠ PROVING [LOCKED]
