================================================================================
PURPOSE
================================================================================
This page develops the previously flagged British Boston harbor joint mission into a
source-ready mission reference that can support later story mapping, mission scripting,
operator usage, and Boston-arc progression.

The mission’s governing logic is now clear:
this is not a symbolic raid and not a generic “blow up a ship” set piece.
It is a targeted strike on a load-bearing Russian occupation resupply delivery entering
or preparing to offload through enemy-controlled Boston Harbor. The vessel is carrying
ordnance, demolitions materiel, heavy weapons ammunition, spare parts, and sustainment
stock whose arrival would materially strengthen the occupation’s ability to hold Boston.

Destroying or catastrophically denying that shipment weakens the occupation in a way the
player can later feel on land.

CORE MISSION IDENTITY
================================================================================
MISSION TYPE
Covert joint sabotage strike in occupied Boston Harbor.

CAMPAIGN HOME
Romeo Game 3 — Boston arc.

MISSION FUNCTION INSIDE THE BOSTON CAMPAIGN
This mission is the British Boston package’s clearest proof of value before the later
Vickers / Royal Marines widening. It demonstrates exactly why SAS and SBS were worth
getting through the blockade at all: they can do one precise thing the local resistance
and Romeo’s people cannot easily do alone.

PRIMARY RESULT
The occupation loses a critical ordnance / sustainment delivery before it can be fully
absorbed into the Boston port logistics chain.

SECONDARY RESULT
Boston resistance gains time, breathing room, and proof that the occupation’s port grip is
vulnerable.

TERTIARY RESULT
The strike helps justify the later widening of British support, because it proves that
small-force intervention in Boston can produce theater-relevant outcomes.

================================================================================
WHY THE BRITISH CARE
================================================================================
The British motivation should be practical, not sentimental.

Recommended combined rationale:
- the Russian Atlantic blockade and East Coast port network form one operational system
- enemy-controlled Boston is not just an occupied city but a working logistics node
- a particular inbound shipment is judged load-bearing enough that destroying it offers
  outsized effect relative to the small British footprint available
- SBS is already in theater specifically to disrupt maritime occupation capability
- SAS can support the strike by infiltrating port-side infrastructure, fixing targets,
  cutting reaction pathways, and enabling exfiltration
- helping Boston here is also helping the wider anti-blockade effort

The British are not “coming to save Boston.”
They are choosing one exact place where limited force can break something that matters.

================================================================================
WHY ROMEO / BOSTON RESISTANCE CARE
================================================================================
For Romeo and the local resistance, the target matters because the shipment directly
strengthens the occupation at street level.

Recommended cargo profile:
- mortar and artillery ammunition for harbor-side and district firebases
- heavy machine gun ammunition and replacement barrels
- demolition stores for route denial and building reduction
- vehicle spare parts for occupation motor pools and technical repair yards
- communications components and power systems needed to stabilize local command nodes

On land, this means:
- harder checkpoints
- better-supplied strongpoints
- more sustained indirect fire
- faster repair of damaged enemy vehicles and infrastructure
- a longer occupation timeline for Boston neighborhoods already barely holding on

The strike should therefore feel like a direct block-by-block campaign mission even though
much of it happens on or near the water.

================================================================================
PLACEMENT RECOMMENDATION
================================================================================
ACT / CAMPAIGN PLACEMENT
Early-to-mid Act III lane preferred.

Reason:
- early enough that British covert action still feels selective and fragile
- late enough that Romeo has already met the British teams and trust has been established
- ideally placed before the full Vickers / Royal Marines widening, so the mission reads as
  part of the covert phase that earns later expansion

BEST STRUCTURAL READ
This is not the first British mission Romeo sees.
It is the mission where British presence stops being interesting and becomes indispensable.

================================================================================
TARGET MODEL
================================================================================
TARGET TYPE
Russian ordnance / sustainment vessel moored, anchoring offshore, or entering the inner
harbor under occupation control.

PREFERRED TARGET READ
Not a capital ship. Not a dramatic duel target. Not a destroyer.
The right target is a guarded military cargo vessel or requisitioned heavy freighter whose
value lies in what it is carrying, not in its prestige.

Why this works better:
- keeps the mission grounded in occupation logistics rather than naval spectacle
- makes covert demolition and sabotage the correct solution
- fits SBS specialty far better than a stand-up naval battle
- lets the player feel the cargo as the real target

OPTIONAL COMPLICATION
Ammunition lighter or secondary security picket vessel nearby.
Use only if it adds tactical pressure without turning the mission into a fleet battle.

================================================================================
MISSION THESIS
================================================================================
The Boston Harbor Strike should feel like this:
A small multinational team moves through freezing, black, enemy-held water to stop a city
from being resupplied for its own occupation.

That is the mission.
Everything else is execution detail.

================================================================================
PRIMARY TEAM ROLES
================================================================================
ROMEO
- player-facing ground combat anchor
- local route familiarity and resistance link
- handles port-side access fights, interior clearing, and emergent adaptation when the
  plan inevitably bends under pressure
- provides the mission’s Boston ownership; this is not a British-only operation in an
  American city

MACE / SAS
- overall on-scene professional gravity
- sabotage and contingency lead on landward side
- makes the hard call when the plan starts to deform

PATCH / SAS
- precision support, overwatch, suppressed shaping kills, route security
- useful for cutting reaction forces approaching from cranes, catwalks, fuel yards,
  or customs-adjacent structures

GHOST / SAS
- close reconnaissance, infiltration support, silent interior work, fixed-site access,
  alarms / control / locking systems / human-factor exploitation

DEPTH / SBS
- load-bearing maritime specialist
- lead combat diver and underwater demolitions
- without Depth, the vessel strike does not happen

CUTTER / SBS
- demolition partner and surface-route problem solver
- charts the harbor approach window and builds the charges plan into a workable action
- can flex between surface and vessel-based breaching logic

WREN / SBS
- intercepts Russian harbor traffic, patrol changes, and reaction cues
- keeps the insertion team informed when the picture shifts
- above-water intelligence layer preventing the dive team from moving blind

MISSION FORM RECOMMENDATION
================================================================================
THREE-PHASE STRUCTURE

PHASE 1 — THE APPROACH
Black-water insertion and harbor penetration.

PHASE 2 — THE FIX
The team confirms cargo, plants denial charges, and shapes enemy response.

PHASE 3 — THE BREAK
The team escapes through a collapsing reaction window as the strike disables or destroys the
shipment and throws the harbor into violent confusion.

================================================================================
PHASE 1 — THE APPROACH
================================================================================
TONAL REGISTER
Cold, procedural, low-verbal, technically exact.
This should be one of the wettest and quietest missions in the Boston arc.

APPROACH OPTIONS
Preferred combined approach:
- initial insertion by small maritime platform or covert drop-off outside the main patrol ring
- SBS-led combat-dive approach for the actual harbor penetration / final vessel access
- Romeo and SAS elements inserted to a separate port-side landing point or industrial edge
  for synchronized landward shaping actions

Why combined approach works best:
- honors the already-seeded dive / mini-sub lane
- gives SBS something uniquely theirs
- still preserves Romeo as active mission participant rather than spectator to divers

KEY GAMEPLAY ELEMENTS
- low-visibility navigation using environmental cues, timed movement, and minimal dialogue
- patrol-light avoidance, propeller / wake danger, hull-shadow movement
- intermittent Russian comms intercepted by Wren rather than constant exposition
- sense that one mistake does not trigger instant mission failure, but does make later phases worse

KEY BEAT
The harbor should feel occupied, industrial, and half-dead rather than theatrically bustling.
Generators, sodium light, cranes, low voices, diesel, water slap, metal groan.
Boston is still there beneath it, but the harbor currently belongs to someone else.

================================================================================
PHASE 2 — THE FIX
================================================================================
OBJECTIVE
Get onto or under the vessel, confirm the cargo profile, and deny it decisively.

DENIAL METHOD RECOMMENDATION
The mission should prefer cargo-kill over ship-kill if that produces a better tactical read.

Meaning:
- the goal is not simply “sink the ship because explosions are cool”
- the goal is to make the shipment unusable, unrecoverable, or catastrophically dangerous to offload
- total sinking is acceptable if it emerges from the demolition logic, but it should not feel like the
  only valid fantasy outcome

BEST DEMOLITION READ
Layered sabotage package:
- underwater charges on critical vessel points / propulsion or mooring vulnerability
- internal or deck-level charges positioned to ignite / ruin ordnance holds or key palletized cargo
- optional secondary sabotage on harbor unloading gear, cranes, or fuel-transfer equipment to slow
  rescue and salvage effort

WHY THIS IS BETTER
It lets the strike land as logistics warfare, not generic action spectacle.

ROMEO’S JOB IN THIS PHASE
- clears port-side access route or customs / warehouse control point
- protects SAS movement to a control, alarm, or line-of-sight position
- may participate in interior vessel clearance if the plan breaks early
- keeps the mission from becoming “watch the British do maritime SOF”

SAS JOB IN THIS PHASE
- suppress or silently remove key sentries
- cut shore-based reaction and observation
- access harbor-control or security infrastructure if needed
- create the buffer that lets SBS finish the actual sabotage

SBS JOB IN THIS PHASE
- vessel access
- charge placement
- cargo confirmation
- timing and detonation architecture

KEY DRAMATIC BEAT
The team realizes the shipment is even more load-bearing than expected.
Not just ammunition, but the specific kind of material that would have made the next stage of the
occupation materially nastier.
This sharpens the necessity of finishing the strike even if the exfil window is worsening.

================================================================================
PHASE 3 — THE BREAK
================================================================================
OBJECTIVE
Escape before the harbor fully locks down.

HOW THE PLAN SHOULD BEND
One element of the plan should degrade, but not collapse into chaos porn.
Best options:
- harbor patrol timing shifts unexpectedly
- a port-side reaction element arrives earlier than predicted
- a mooring / charge timing issue forces one operator to stay committed a little too long
- an intercepted Russian call reveals the harbor is beginning to understand what is happening

The mission should then pivot from perfect stealth to compressed violent efficiency.

GAMEPLAY IDENTITY
- short, hard break-contact sequence
- split pressure between waterline and landward escape lanes
- no prolonged stand-up firefight that turns the harbor into an arena shooter
- urgency comes from enclosure, light, search, engines, and reaction, not just enemy count

DETONATION / PAYOFF RECOMMENDATION
The strike should produce a layered payoff:
1. initial proof of success — charges / fire / secondary explosions / loss of cargo control
2. a worse realization by the enemy — this is not a small incident but catastrophic denial
3. a harbor-wide confusion window the team uses to vanish

VISUAL READ
Prefer industrial violence over blockbuster fireball.
Ammunition cooking off, cranes freezing, forklifts abandoned, searchlights swinging, mooring strain,
container stacks echoing, harbor sirens, Russian radio traffic degrading in quality.
The city should feel the shock of the strike before it sees who did it.

================================================================================
MISSION OUTCOME
================================================================================
RECOMMENDED OUTCOME LOCK
The shipment is successfully denied.
The vessel is destroyed, crippled beyond practical use, or left burning and unsalvageable enough that
its intended cargo function is gone.
The team escapes, but the exfil is hard and should leave a cost impression even if no major character
is lost.

PREFERRED CONSEQUENCE MODEL
Not “the British saved Boston.”
Instead:
- several later enemy positions are visibly less well supplied
- one later operation happens because ordnance or spares failed to arrive
- resistance chatter starts treating the harbor as vulnerable rather than untouchable
- British credibility with Romeo and Boston locals rises sharply
- higher command is more willing to commit later widening support because the first intervention worked

================================================================================
RELATIONSHIP TO VICKERS / ROYAL MARINES LAYER
================================================================================
This mission should PRECEDE the fuller Vickers widening.

Why:
- it keeps Vickers from entering as unexplained support fantasy
- it gives later Royal Marines / Royal Navy involvement a proven reason to exist
- it preserves the campaign rule that British help begins as covert competence and only later widens

Best follow-on read:
TEA PARTY proves that a carefully chosen British intervention in Boston can create strategic effect.
Only after that proof does the larger support argument become materially persuasive.

Vickers may be:
- off-screen in this mission
- present only via planning approval or distant coordination
- held entirely for later reveal

Do not force her into the mission if doing so muddies the covert-phase identity.

================================================================================
RELATIONSHIP TO OTHER BOSTON MISSIONS
================================================================================
HOSTAGE / HVP EXTRACTION
This remains distinct.
The extraction mission is human, morally tangled, and about people Britain cannot leave behind.
TEA PARTY is colder: occupation logistics, denial, and pressure on the city’s port artery.
They support each other by contrast.

OPTIONAL FOREIGN-CIVILIANS / WHEELCHAIR EXFIL SIDE QUEST
Also remains distinct.
That mission expresses British presence through restraint and obligation.
TEA PARTY expresses it through precision violence.

HARBOR AND SHADOW-GOVERNMENT ARC
This strike should strengthen the harbor act materially, not replace it.
It is one operation that weakens a system, not the act’s sole answer.

================================================================================
ENEMY / ENVIRONMENT READ
================================================================================
ENEMY MIX
- Russian naval infantry / port-security personnel
- harbor labor under coercive occupation control
- technical logistics staff
- limited quick-reaction force element

Avoid turning the mission into an elite-operator theme park.
The threat should mostly come from layered security, limited reaction time, and the danger of being
caught in the wrong geometry once the strike begins.

ENVIRONMENTAL TEXTURE
- black water and rusted steel
- cranes, catwalks, loading gantries, sealed containers, wet ladders
- cold sodium light and intermittent darkness
- industrial noise floor that sometimes helps and sometimes harms stealth
- American harbor architecture wearing occupation overlays

================================================================================
CHARACTER / DIALOGUE RULES
================================================================================
- keep dialogue sparse and professional
- let SBS feel culturally and operationally distinct from SAS
- let Romeo remain mission-critical, not guest talent in a British showcase
- avoid history-class dialogue about Boston and Britain; one dry acknowledgment somewhere in the
  broader arc is enough
- Cutter and Wren can carry more of the active harbor comms texture than Depth, whose silence is part
  of his function
- Mace should only speak when it matters

================================================================================
DO NOTS
================================================================================
- do not make the target a prestige warship just to inflate spectacle
- do not make the mission a full harbor liberation
- do not make Vickers arrive with a huge force package too early
- do not let Romeo become secondary inside his own city
- do not write the British motive as sentimental rescue theater
- do not make the mission’s success abstract; the denied shipment must matter afterward

================================================================================
CLEAN LOCK RECOMMENDATION
================================================================================
TEA PARTY is a joint Romeo / SAS / SBS Boston Harbor strike in which a small British covert package
and the local resistance destroy or decisively deny a load-bearing Russian ordnance and sustainment
shipment moving through enemy-controlled Boston port infrastructure. The mission exists to weaken the
occupation materially, prove the harbor is vulnerable, and justify the later widening of British
support under Vickers rather than replacing it.
