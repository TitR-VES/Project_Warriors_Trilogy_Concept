================================================================================
PURPOSE
================================================================================
This page expands the Who Dares Wins campaign ladder into a practical mission-outline
sheet that can support later mission scripting, squad usage, Boston handoff logic,
and Vickers-layer integration.

The governing rule remains simple:
Who Dares Wins must make the later British Boston arc feel earned.
It should explain why Britain still functions, what kind of war Britain can still fight,
and how a damaged state becomes capable of one precise transatlantic intervention.

================================================================================
INHERITED LOCKS THIS PAGE MUST HONOR
================================================================================
1. Who Dares Wins covers both:
   - SAS deployment operations
   - the British home front and Britain’s response to PANDORA and the subsequent war

2. The later Boston-side British package remains:
   - SAS = covert sabotage / infiltration / hostage-HVP extraction
   - SBS = covert maritime sabotage / blockade disruption / insertion expertise
   - Royal Marines (+ Royal Navy) under Lt. Dorothy Anne Vickers = later widening,
     resistance support, CASEVAC growth, and real balance-of-power shift

3. British support in Boston must begin as covert competence and only later widen into
   meaningful support / CASEVAC capability.

4. The DLC must not consume Boston’s main payoffs. It should end by making the Boston arc
   possible, not by replacing it.

5. Working lock adopted from current thread:
   the later joint Boston harbor strike should be motivated by disrupting a key Russian
   occupation resupply delivery through the enemy-controlled port of Boston. The target is
   not just “a ship” but a load-bearing ordnance / sustainment movement whose destruction
   materially weakens occupation capacity in the city.

================================================================================
PRIMARY PLAYABLE-POV RECOMMENDATION
================================================================================
Primary playable protagonist: MACE.

Reason:
- keeps the DLC recognizably SAS-led
- supports both home-front and deployment phases cleanly
- allows PATCH and GHOST to function as the immediate SAS team
- lets DEPTH, CUTTER, and WREN grow from adjacent capability into indispensable SBS counterparts

Usage rule:
Mace remains principal POV. Short controlled perspective deviations are permissible only if
later development identifies a genuinely load-bearing SBS-specific mechanic that cannot be
felt through Mace’s observation and coordination.

================================================================================
TONE / REGISTER RULES
================================================================================
- Britain is damaged, not untouched.
- Britain survives by discipline, redundancy, triage, and grim competence.
- The DLC is not patriotic pageantry and not collapse porn.
- Most missions should feel exact, pressured, and materially grounded.
- Maritime operations should feel technical, cold, and procedural rather than swashbuckling.
- By the end, the player should feel that Boston is possible only because Britain narrowed its
  ambitions and chose one high-value intervention it could actually sustain.

================================================================================
PROLOGUE — DEAD CHANNEL
================================================================================
ROLE
Playable opening on PANDORA day.

MISSION PURPOSE
Establish the British version of the catastrophe: severe military and infrastructure shock,
command confusion, dead systems, partial continuity, and the sudden shift from routine
operation to state triage.

SETTING LANE
Strategic military or naval site in Britain. Best-fit lane: a coastal or command-adjacent
facility where dead networks and misidentification fear are immediately legible.

CORE GAMEPLAY IDENTITY
- short playable crisis opening
- urgent but simple trained tasks
- fragmentary radio traffic
- no full battlefield picture
- danger created by uncertainty, not spectacle

OBJECTIVE FLOW
1. Routine status interrupted by contradictory alarms and dead channels.
2. Player performs immediate containment tasks under incomplete information.
3. Base/system confusion produces brief friend-or-foe pressure without cheap melodrama.
4. The mission resolves not with victory but with partial restoration of a critical function.

KEY BEATS
- first realization that Britain has also been hit hard
- first proof that disciplined professionals are the only reason anything still works
- first tonal statement that normal life is already over

SQUAD / CAST USE
- Mace present in pre-crisis state, immediately forced into emergency problem-solving
- other team members can be heard or briefly seen, but do not need full dramatic introduction yet

WHAT THIS MISSION CONTRIBUTES TO BOSTON
It establishes the cost Britain pays before it can matter abroad. Boston help later will read as
something Britain fought to remain capable of, not something it could do for free.

OPEN FLAGS
- exact site selection
- whether this opens with Mace already active-duty on site or in transit to a response node

================================================================================
MISSION 01 — HOLDFAST
================================================================================
ROLE
First full mission. Britain chooses what must be preserved.

MISSION PURPOSE
Dramatize the preservation of state core functions: continuity, deterrence, strategic survivability,
base integrity, and command retention.

SETTING LANE
Sensitive military / naval / command continuity site.

CORE GAMEPLAY IDENTITY
- defensive redirection under incomplete information
- compound fighting and interior movement
- mixed security and combat tasks
- pressure from uncertainty rather than huge numbers

OBJECTIVE FLOW
1. Move to a compromised continuity-critical facility.
2. Re-establish physical control of one or more breached sections.
3. Prevent destruction or compromise of a strategic process, archive, or command function.
4. Extract or secure a person / device / facility section necessary for continuity.

KEY BEATS
- Britain cannot save everything and stops pretending otherwise
- the state preserves core capability at the expense of ordinary functionality
- Mace’s team is defined as exact, unsentimental, and already beyond peacetime assumptions

SQUAD / CAST USE
- full SAS team introduced in functional rather than expository fashion
- Patch gets first clear support-role moment
- Ghost gets first low-verbal, high-competence beat

WHAT THIS MISSION CONTRIBUTES TO BOSTON
This is the foundation of the whole British arc: Britain still has something to project later because
it first chose what not to lose.

OPEN FLAGS
- exact national-level function preserved here
- whether continuity-of-government remains off-screen or partially player-facing

================================================================================
MISSION 02 — TIDELINE
================================================================================
ROLE
First true triage-state mission.

MISSION PURPOSE
Show that Britain’s war rapidly becomes one of throughput, access, and infrastructure survival.
The enemy target is not national symbolism but ports, fuel, maritime movement, and undersea or
shoreline connectivity.

SETTING LANE
British coastal logistics node, cable-landing environment, fuel terminal, or military-adjacent port.

CORE GAMEPLAY IDENTITY
- maritime-industrial environment
- partial stealth and close interception
- civilian-proximate pressure
- environmental hazards matter

OBJECTIVE FLOW
1. Conduct reconnaissance on a threatened coastal node.
2. Identify sabotage architecture already in place.
3. Intercept multiple enemy actions with different tempos: planted charges, access compromise,
   communications disruption, and a timed exfil attempt.
4. Save enough of the node to preserve function, even if not all of it survives intact.

KEY BEATS
- Britain becomes a triage power rather than a normal state
- the war is increasingly about keeping corridors open
- ports and cable / fuel logic are now explicit, not background lore

SQUAD / CAST USE
- SAS still primary, but the mission begins to imply the future need for SBS expertise
- local uniformed defenders or security staff can briefly appear to widen the world

WHAT THIS MISSION CONTRIBUTES TO BOSTON
It teaches the campaign’s port logic early. The later Boston harbor material will feel like a natural
extension of something the DLC has already taught the player to care about.

OPEN FLAGS
- exact facility type
- whether the target is more cable-centric or fuel-centric

================================================================================
MISSION 03 — GLASSHOUSE
================================================================================
ROLE
Internal penetration mission.

MISSION PURPOSE
Reveal that Britain’s surviving institutions are functioning but compromised. The threat is not only
external assault; it is prepared access, contractor-mediated vulnerability, and penetrated systems.

SETTING LANE
Secure technical or contractor-linked site inside Britain.

CORE GAMEPLAY IDENTITY
- intelligence-led raid
- room-to-room clearing with document / device capture
- possible soft-stealth opening leading into controlled violence
- strong “exact SAS work” identity

OBJECTIVE FLOW
1. Follow fragmentary anomaly trail to a nominally British-controlled site.
2. Confirm that compromise came through access, not brute force.
3. Seize drives, communication gear, and/or liaison records before sanitization.
4. Fight or intercept exfil element attempting to erase the chain.

KEY BEATS
- Britain survives, but not cleanly
- trust narrows
- the campaign’s moral register tightens: preservation without innocence

SQUAD / CAST USE
- Patch or Ghost gets the mission’s most load-bearing specialist contribution
- Mace’s tone as leader should settle here: quiet, exact, mildly dry, no speeches

WHAT THIS MISSION CONTRIBUTES TO BOSTON
Explains why Britain’s later Boston intervention must begin very small. A penetrated state does not
commit broad force lightly.

OPEN FLAGS
- exact compromised actor type: contractor, insider, parallel intelligence access, or mixed chain

================================================================================
MISSION 04 — GREY WATER
================================================================================
ROLE
The campaign turns seaward.

MISSION PURPOSE
Transition Britain from homeland sealing to North Atlantic edge-work. This is where SBS stops
being a later add-on and becomes indispensable.

SETTING LANE
Littoral intercept or vessel-boarding operation in rough, low-visibility maritime conditions.

CORE GAMEPLAY IDENTITY
- night maritime movement
- boarding and hull / ladder / confined-vessel space
- timing, route discipline, and low-signature movement
- the ocean as an operational system, not scenery

OBJECTIVE FLOW
1. Link up with SBS counterparts and conduct approach planning.
2. Infiltrate a disguised vessel / surveillance platform / route-monitoring craft.
3. Recover route or patrol intelligence and disable the target without over-signaturing the action.
4. Exfil before the broader maritime picture tightens around the team.

KEY BEATS
- first full SBS operational legitimacy
- the Atlantic becomes a contested living space
- Britain rediscovers a kind of war it can still fight well: cold, maritime, deniable

SQUAD / CAST USE
- Depth, Cutter, and Wren properly enter the playable architecture here
- Cutter should begin reading as the practical harbor / route mind
- Wren begins to establish the comms-intercept lane that later matters in Boston

WHAT THIS MISSION CONTRIBUTES TO BOSTON
Makes later submarine transit and covert Atlantic insertion feel credible rather than hand-waved.

OPEN FLAGS
- exact target type
- how much of this mission is above-water versus submerged approach

================================================================================
MISSION 05 — LEE SHORE
================================================================================
ROLE
First outward strike with clear strategic consequence.

MISSION PURPOSE
Prove that Britain can still do more than hold ground at home. The target should be small,
technical, and load-bearing: a reconnaissance relay, route-monitoring asset, covert staging node,
or logistics-support element whose removal opens maneuver space.

SETTING LANE
North Atlantic edge-space beyond mainland Britain.

CORE GAMEPLAY IDENTITY
- exposed terrain or weather
- longer insertion and extraction discipline
- precision strike rather than attritional firefight
- isolation and distance more important than spectacle

OBJECTIVE FLOW
1. Insert onto or near a remote operational node.
2. Conduct surveillance confirmation before action.
3. Neutralize the target with minimal signature and recover any route-relevant intelligence.
4. Survive a tightening exfil window as the enemy begins to understand something has changed.

KEY BEATS
- Britain’s surviving strength is selective reach, not mass return
- the player feels risk, distance, and narrow operating margin
- the war’s geography expands without losing precision

SQUAD / CAST USE
- strongest SAS/SBS integration so far
- Ghost can carry a quiet but memorable role here, especially if the target demands patience

WHAT THIS MISSION CONTRIBUTES TO BOSTON
This is the doctrinal bridge to Boston. Britain is now demonstrated capable of reaching out and
opening one lane that matters.

OPEN FLAGS
- exact target selection
- whether this mission has a harsher weather identity or a stealthier summer-fog identity

================================================================================
MISSION 06 — NEXT OF KIN
================================================================================
ROLE
Boston becomes necessary.

MISSION PURPOSE
Converge the human, intelligence, and strategic reasons for intervening around Boston.
This mission should establish that Boston matters because several things overlap there at once:
trapped British-linked personnel, coalition leverage, blockade pressure, and a city where a small
British intervention might genuinely alter the board.

SETTING LANE
Interception, recovery, or liaison operation yielding Boston-facing intelligence and human stakes.

CORE GAMEPLAY IDENTITY
- capture / recover / extract rather than pure destruction
- time pressure and moral pressure together
- intelligence mission with emotional weight

OBJECTIVE FLOW
1. Recover a person, courier, archive, or device carrying Boston-relevant knowledge.
2. Confirm that British nationals or British intelligence-linked personnel remain exposed in or near
   the Boston battlespace.
3. Identify Boston not just as a humanitarian site but as an operational hinge on the Atlantic front.
4. End with a concrete decision point: Britain now has enough reason to consider a transatlantic move.

KEY BEATS
- Boston becomes legible as a real obligation
- the operation is not charity and not sentimentality; it is a convergence of duty and strategy
- the player feels the moment Britain’s war stops being contained to its own perimeter

SQUAD / CAST USE
- Mace carries command judgement
- Wren or Patch can own the intelligence readability of the recovered material

WHAT THIS MISSION CONTRIBUTES TO BOSTON
This is the explicit narrative hinge. It justifies why Britain risks a covert move across the Atlantic.

OPEN FLAGS
- exact source of Boston intelligence
- whether the British-linked human stake is a family case, intelligence case, or mixed case

================================================================================
MISSION 07 — LONG WAKE
================================================================================
ROLE
Prepare the lane and commit to the operation.

MISSION PURPOSE
Clear, protect, or confirm the covert route by which the SAS/SBS package can move toward New
England at all. This is the last point at which Britain can still decide not to go.

SETTING LANE
Maritime pre-insertion shaping operation in the Atlantic.

CORE GAMEPLAY IDENTITY
- covert maritime strike / denial
- route timing, patrol windows, and low-signature destruction
- tension from narrow windows rather than massive combat density

OBJECTIVE FLOW
1. Identify the route-obstructing obstacle: patrol pattern, route-monitoring node, relay, or support vessel.
2. Conduct a shaping action that clears or blinds part of the picture.
3. Confirm a viable insertion lane through the blockade environment.
4. Exit with the understanding that the decision has effectively been made.

KEY BEATS
- Britain does not “go global” again; it chooses one dangerous reach operation it can actually support
- the team crosses from possibility to commitment
- SBS route logic becomes fully load-bearing

SQUAD / CAST USE
- strongest pure maritime teamwork in the DLC
- Cutter should feel especially authoritative here
- Depth’s stillness / dive identity can land strongly if underwater work is used

WHAT THIS MISSION CONTRIBUTES TO BOSTON
This mission is the practical precondition for submarine-supported insertion and first contact on the
New England side.

OPEN FLAGS
- exact nature of the route obstacle
- whether the mission leans more sabotage or more intelligence denial

================================================================================
MISSION 08 — FIRST FOOTING
================================================================================
ROLE
Campaign finale. Britain arrives.

MISSION PURPOSE
Deliver the first clandestine British foothold on the New England side without consuming the major
Boston story beats that belong to Romeo’s campaign.

SETTING LANE
New England coastal insertion and early inland movement toward first hide-site / contact chain.

CORE GAMEPLAY IDENTITY
- stealth-heavy, tension-heavy
- minimal signature
- survival through patience, timing, and exactness
- the most important emotional effect is not conquest but arrival

OBJECTIVE FLOW
1. Execute transatlantic insertion under extreme low-signature conditions.
2. Make landfall and survive first movement off the shoreline.
3. Establish first safe hide-site / observation point / contact node.
4. Confirm that the British package has a viable foothold and that resistance capacity exists to work with.
5. End before the DLC becomes Romeo’s Boston campaign in miniature.

KEY BEATS
- the British home-front survival story becomes physical presence inside occupied America
- covert competence, not spectacle, is the reward
- the sense of scale contracts deliberately from state logic to one hidden foothold in enemy-held space

SQUAD / CAST USE
- Mace central
- SBS capability remains inseparable from success
- no Vickers arrival yet; only the knowledge that broader widening may come later if the foothold lives

WHAT THIS MISSION CONTRIBUTES TO BOSTON
Direct handoff to Romeo’s later first SAS contact and the British Boston-side architecture.

OPEN FLAGS
- exact New England insertion point
- whether first local contact is American resistance, British-linked holdout, or observation-only

================================================================================
EPILOGUE — THE OTHER SIDE OF THE OCEAN
================================================================================
ROLE
Short coda / handoff scene.

PURPOSE
Connect the DLC cleanly into the already-seeded Boston architecture without spending Boston’s own
mission payoffs too early.

RECOMMENDED CONTENT
- SAS/SBS foothold confirmed
- first task lanes identified: surveillance, sabotage, extraction, harbor shaping
- explicit recognition that the force is too small to alter Boston alone
- early mention of a later widening layer under Lt. Dorothy Anne Vickers
- confirmation that broader British support and CASEVAC capability are not yet available

WORKING BOSTON-HARBOR DIRECTION ADOPTED FROM THIS THREAD
The joint harbor strike later in Boston should target a Russian occupation resupply movement through
enemy-controlled port infrastructure. The target is best understood as a key ordnance / sustainment
shipment whose destruction would materially weaken occupation combat power in Boston rather than only
produce symbolic damage.

Why this works:
- aligns with the already-seeded “ordnance/supply vessel” concept
- fits SBS maritime sabotage and blockade-disruption identity cleanly
- gives SAS a real supporting sabotage / infiltration role onshore
- gives Romeo a clear city-level reason to participate later
- makes the harbor strike a serious operational act, not just a Tea Party allusion

BEST END NOTE
Quiet commitment, not triumph.
Britain is now on the far side of the Atlantic, but only just.
