================================================================================
PURPOSE
================================================================================
This page supplies the missing national logic that must govern both:
- Project Warriors: Who Dares Wins
- the British force package in Romeo’s occupied-Boston / liberation arc

The guiding requirement is continuity of cause and effect. Britain’s Boston presence
must feel like the later outward expression of the same wartime reality established
in the DLC, not a separately invented subplot.

================================================================================
CORE LOCK
================================================================================
Britain survives PANDORA as a strained but functioning state by preserving continuity
of government, strategic deterrence, maritime access, and select high-end military
capability. It does not emerge untouched, and it does not mirror the full state-
fracture experienced by the United States. In the war’s first phase Britain becomes
an emergency triage power rather than a sanctuary. Who Dares Wins covers the passage
from shock to selective reach. The British Boston arc is the later outward expression
of that same logic: SAS and SBS first, Royal Marines and Royal Navy widening only
after a covert foothold and strategic justification already exist.

Short thematic shorthand:
Damaged state. Preserved core. Selective reach.

================================================================================
01. PANDORA DAY — BRITAIN IS HIT HARD, NOT BROKEN
================================================================================
Britain should register PANDORA as a severe national shock, not a near-miss.
The correct tonal read is abrupt compression of normal life under military and
infrastructural stress.

Recommended immediate effects:
- severe disruption to military and governmental digital systems
- rolling power, telecom, transport, and logistics failures in selected regions
- paralysis and emergency restriction at airfields, ports, and rail nodes
- autonomous / networked-defense anomalies causing panic, misidentification, and
  localized command breakdowns
- sudden domestic fear of sabotage, infiltration, and follow-on attacks

Narrative rule:
The opening British experience is not competence porn. It is disciplined survival
under pressure.

================================================================================
02. FIRST 72 HOURS — SAVE THE STATE, NOT NORMAL LIFE
================================================================================
Britain’s first successful action after PANDORA is not restoring public normalcy.
It is preventing cascading state failure.

Immediate national priorities:
- continuity of government
- survivability of strategic deterrent / nuclear command architecture
- air-defense and early-warning continuity
- protection of key naval bases, ports, fuel nodes, and communications sites
- prevention of opportunistic internal panic, sabotage, and administrative collapse

Home-front texture guidance:
- emergency broadcasts instead of normal media rhythm
- reduced civilian movement and visible military security around critical sites
- queueing, rumor, fuel anxiety, restricted travel, and compressed public language
- sense that the country is still functioning, but in a narrowed wartime register

Comparative rule:
Britain should not read as unaffected and should not read as fully shattered. The
point is that it retains enough institutional spine to act coherently while still
paying a visible price.

================================================================================
03. FIRST MONTH — BRITAIN AS A TRIAGE POWER
================================================================================
In the first post-PANDORA month Britain becomes a North Atlantic triage state.
It cannot operate as a confident global enforcer. It can still preserve, organize,
repair, launch from, and coordinate key functions that larger broken systems no
longer can.

This phase should define the home-front identity of Who Dares Wins.

Core conditions:
- military overstretch without full institutional collapse
- economic constriction and fuel / shipping pressure
- hardened infrastructure protection posture
- counter-sabotage and counter-infiltration activity across ports, coasts, bases,
  signal sites, and sensitive civilian infrastructure
- public fatigue, professionalism, and narrowing expectation of ordinary life

Best atmospheric read:
Britain feels tired, disciplined, and increasingly wartime-practical.

================================================================================
04. WHAT BRITAIN CAN STILL DO — SELECTIVE FORCE, NOT MASS FORCE
================================================================================
The key question for both the DLC and Boston is not whether Britain remains powerful
in the abstract. It is what kind of force a damaged but functioning Britain could
still credibly project.

Recommended answer:
Britain cannot deploy large conventional force overseas freely after PANDORA. It
can still deploy select high-value capabilities where the footprint is small and
the payoff is strategic.

Capabilities that plausibly survive and matter:
- special operations forces
- submarine-enabled insertion and covert maritime access
- limited maritime special operations and interdiction
- constrained expeditionary support once a viable foothold exists

This is the national logic that makes the Boston package believable.

================================================================================
05. BOSTON LINKAGE — WHY BRITAIN GOES THERE
================================================================================
British involvement in Boston must not feel sentimental or arbitrary. It should
follow directly from British wartime logic.

Boston matters because it can credibly satisfy several British-interest conditions
at once:
- British nationals or intelligence-linked personnel require extraction
- the city offers a meaningful point of pressure against the Atlantic blockade
- a live resistance network on the ground makes limited intervention worthwhile
- a small British commitment can produce outsized coalition effect there in a way
  that broader East Coast intervention cannot

Therefore Boston is not a side adventure. It is one of the first places where a
strained Britain can turn survival capability outward with real strategic leverage.

================================================================================
06. FORCE PACKAGE FOR BOSTON — LOCKED SHARED LOGIC
================================================================================
British Boston structure should remain three-part and sequential in felt effect:

1. SAS
- covert sabotage
- infiltration
- hostage / HVP extraction
- precision raids

2. SBS
- covert maritime insertion
- blockade-disruption actions
- combat-diver approach work
- harbor and near-shore special operations support

3. Royal Marines (+ Royal Navy support)
- led by Lt. Dorothy Anne Vickers
- resistance support and widening coalition presence
- practical shift in local balance of power
- later-stage CASEVAC / reinforcement / support presence

Operational rule:
SAS and SBS establish the covert foothold first. Vickers and the Royal Marines
widen the British effect later, once the narrative has earned it.

Gameplay / campaign-progression rule:
British military CASEVAC in Boston remains a late-availability capability. The
player should feel the coalition physically thickening around Romeo’s operation
when these options arrive.

================================================================================
07. WHO DARES WINS DLC — RECOMMENDED STRUCTURE
================================================================================
The DLC should not begin as a Boston story. It should end by making Boston possible.

Recommended five-part shape:

ACT I — THE BLOW
PANDORA day and immediate British shock. Confusion, containment, interrupted
systems, and proof that the state is still alive only because exhausted people
keep it alive.

ACT II — THE SEAL
Internal security, counter-sabotage, sensitive-site defense, and the practical
reality of preserving a wounded homeland.

ACT III — THE EDGE
North Atlantic and coastal pressure environment. Britain discovers what kind of
war it can still fight: maritime, covert, exact, selective.

ACT IV — THE REACH
SAS / SBS missions that demonstrate restored outward precision, but still at small
scale. Britain is not back to normal; it is learning where it can still matter.

ACT V — THE HANDOFF
Direct narrative handoff into the Boston architecture:
- covert transatlantic insertion becomes plausible
- SAS / SBS operational lanes are established
- Vickers / Royal Marines widening is prepared
- Boston reads as consequence, not invention

================================================================================
08. HOME-FRONT DESIGN LANGUAGE FOR WHO DARES WINS
================================================================================
The home-front should not be built around speeches or broad patriotism alone. Its
strength is procedural, physical, and environmental.

Useful recurring textures:
- coastal weather, port lighting, blackout discipline, and emergency barriers
- hardened compounds and tired domestic military routines
- temporary command spaces in still-functioning but narrowed civilian Britain
- public information that becomes shorter, flatter, and more survival-oriented
- the emotional register of people continuing because the alternative is collapse

Preferred dramatic emphasis:
Britain survives not because it is invulnerable, but because enough people in key
places keep doing unglamorous jobs correctly under extreme pressure.

================================================================================
09. RELATIONSHIP TO ROMEO’S BOSTON ARC
================================================================================
This UK spine should produce the following reads in Game 3 Boston:
- British contact begins as covert competence, not triumphant arrival
- British presence is useful before it is large
- Romeo does not get “rescued by Britain”; he gains allied capability that alters
  what becomes possible
- Vickers and the Royal Marines feel materially transformative because earlier
  scarcity has already been established
- the British-in-Boston revolutionary-history inversion remains a deliberate theme,
  but never overwhelms the human and operational stakes

================================================================================
10. GUARDRAILS
================================================================================
Do not write Britain as:
- untouched sanctuary
- mirror-image US collapse
- conventionally dominant transatlantic savior force

Do write Britain as:
- damaged but functional
- maritime and strategic in its surviving strengths
- selective rather than mass-projecting
- increasingly outward-facing only after preserving its own core
