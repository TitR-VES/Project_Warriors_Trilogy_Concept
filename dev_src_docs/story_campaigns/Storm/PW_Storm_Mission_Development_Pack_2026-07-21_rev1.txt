MISSION 01 CONCEPT — OPENING ROUTE CHECK
================================================================================
Function:
Introduce Denis through competence, establish Procella's route language, and show
that “broken” and “burned” are not the same condition.

Situation:
A coastal extraction seam goes partially dark after a contact failure. Denis must
reach the route, determine whether it remains usable, identify what interrupted
the chain, recover surviving contact material, and avoid unnecessary exposure.

Environment:
- Russian Far East coastal service road
- drainage/culvert infrastructure
- rail-adjacent cuts
- broken fencing
- wet concrete, shallow runoff, dead utility boxes
- low industrial spill light, wind, mist/drizzle/sleet
- black water and distant harbor lights

Gameplay:
- environmental reading
- route validation
- hidden contact objects and power/signaling clues
- short practical contact only if necessary
- first remote shadow of Albatross
- pale-blue chalk “cold” route marking may be established here

End-state:
The route is damaged but not dead. The player learns that Denis reads systems,
not cinematic clues.

Title governance:
Do not land SOUTH CULVERT as a second canonical mission title while the Spook
Game 3 mission of that name remains active. A later title adjudication is required.

================================================================================
MISSION 02 — FALSE WEATHER
================================================================================
Function:
Establish the systems-sabotage identity of The Storm.

Situation:
Procella identifies a coastal weather/navigation node whose outputs are being
deliberately falsified. The false picture protects favored regime traffic while
making civilian and deniable movement easier to intercept or erase.

Objectives:
- infiltrate the node
- confirm the falsification
- restore, split, or corrupt output to benefit Procella routes
- preserve the illusion of normal operation if possible
- exfiltrate before the compromise becomes obvious

Environment:
- remote coastal weather/navigation station
- steel mast, service sheds, generator housing
- layered old/new equipment
- cable trenches, corroded rails, wet concrete, salt damage
- black water, poor visibility, dead/rotating signal lights

Gameplay:
- read staged neglect and false maintenance
- access damp relay/utility interiors
- manipulate output under patrol, weather, power, or remote-check pressure
- avoid generic “hack the terminal” treatment
- win by altered conditions, not by destroying the site

End-state examples:
- hidden truthful stream restored
- patrol or shipping picture delayed/misdirected
- extraction window reopened
- regime still believes the node is controlled

MISSION 03 — LAST LIGHT HARBOR
================================================================================
Working direction:
First full extraction mission with civilians/defectors through a harbor edge that
is nominally functional but decaying in practice. Emphasize escort as route
management rather than babysitting, dark service corridors, dock clutter, close
contact, and water exfiltration.

MISSION 04 — COLD BATTERY
================================================================================
Working direction:
Recover thermoelectric or comparable power units needed to restore a relay chain.
Power becomes a survival commodity. Carry burden, transport risk, installation,
and selective engagement matter as much as shooting.

================================================================================
MISSION 05 — DRIFT LINE
================================================================================
Working direction:
A known extraction line is compromised. Albatross redirects Denis to a hidden
secondary seam during worsening weather, turning him from rumor into a visible
continuity mind without overexposing him.

================================================================================
MISSION 06 — NORTHERN WINDOW
================================================================================
Working direction:
A restrained external-contact mission, likely Japan-facing. Escort a defector,
exchange technical intelligence, or receive specialized equipment through a
fragile northern maritime contact. Sanctuary is partial, political, and deniable.
This must not turn The Storm into the Home Defense campaign.

================================================================================
MISSION 07 — FURTHER EAST
================================================================================
Function:
Sakhalin's route-to-contact mission in Tomakomai, Hokkaido, synchronized with
Spook Game 3's OUTER PORT.

Design:
- different pressure and different knowledge, not a replay
- Sakhalin reaches Japan, handles surveillance risk, validates local seams, and
  determines whether the unfamiliar American is genuine or bait
- background information exchanged with Spook is taught through what Sakhalin
  has already experienced and carried
- end at the exact hinge where the scene transfers into Spook's mission

Objective progression:
- Reach the contact district.
- Confirm the American.
- Secure the handoff.

Contact:
Officer Trevor Gartner / Spook. “Gardner” is incorrect.

Developed seam:
- outer-port service edge
- deliberately killed lamp
- blue chalk route mark
- waterproof intelligence envelope
- Albatross remote guidance
- Spook supported by Tripper and Smoke
- mutual professionalism without instant warmth
- Denis exits by the lower waterline service channel

CROSS-CAMPAIGN RULE
================================================================================
OUTER PORT remains Spook's mission and owns the contact conversation.
