MISSION PURPOSE
First deliberate neighborhood counteroffensive after ROLL CALL.
Boston decides what it intends to take back and keep. The target is not a symbolic
building but a connected interior pocket built around survival infrastructure.

POCKET COMPOSITION
- protected residential interiors;
- service courtyard;
- clinic access;
- workshop/repair capacity;
- observation routes;
- water and limited power;
- multiple exits;
- three perimeter “teeth” that must be cleared.

Core distinction:
Earlier missions created breathing spaces. THE POCKET creates the first place intended
to remain theirs in the morning.

BRIEFING CORE
Elena selects ground based on sustainment, not symbolism.
The resistance must:
- walk the inside;
- clear three teeth;
- move civilians and functional locals;
- build barricades, aid, water, communications, and observation;
- survive the first night answer;
- lock the pocket for morning.

Protected conceptual dialogue:
ROMEO asks what makes the target different.
ELENA's answer is that it is not just something they can enter; it is something they can
feed, treat, repair, watch, and leave from.
The mission is a choice to keep ground, accepting that the enemy will answer.

MISSION FLOW
Phase 1 — Walk the inside
- move through candidate pocket with residents still present;
- inventory clinic, water, workshop, rooftops, blind courtyards, and escape seams;
- local knowledge determines what is viable.

Protected local logic:
- a pocket without water is a trap;
- a pocket without exits is a grave;
- a pocket without skilled people is only empty buildings.

Phase 2 — First tooth: corner post
- remove a local post controlling the most public entry;
- use interior approach rather than frontal street assault;
- preserve structures needed for later defense.

Phase 3 — Second tooth: courtyard eyes
- clear observation/fire position overlooking inner service court;
- control vertical sightlines;
- convert the courtyard into protected movement space.

Phase 4 — Third tooth: rear utility choke
- secure the rear service/utility route;
- protect water/power access and secondary exfiltration;
- prevent the enemy from folding the pocket from behind.

Phase 5 — Build the pocket
- civilians, fighters, medics, machinist/Foreman-type skilled locals, water, batteries,
  radios, optics, and tools move in;
- player helps place practical defenses;
- no flag-planting spectacle;
- the neighborhood becomes functional under pressure.

Phase 6 — First night answer
- occupiers probe from multiple teeth;
- resistance must rotate sectors rather than chase;
- use HINGE and earlier route knowledge;
- civilians and skilled locals continue working during attack;
- the point is to survive a coordinated answer, not destroy an army.

Phase 7 — Lock for night
- close false routes;
- maintain real exits;
- establish watch rotations;
- distribute water/ammunition/medical stock;
- mark sectors and casualty points;
- leave deceptive signs outside;
- preserve the ability to abandon the pocket if later necessary.

Phase 8 — First thing kept
At morning/late-night quiet:
- clinic works;
- radios charge;
- workshop repairs begin;
- residents sleep inside connected ground;
- observation is continuous;
- the pocket is still theirs.
