TITLE / PLACEMENT
================================================================================

MISSION TITLE: HOLDFAST
CAMPAIGN: Romeo — Game 3 Boston
PLACEMENT: Late Act III moving toward Act IV, immediately after LIFELINE and its
           first ambient consequence beats
MISSION FUNCTION: full screenplay / mission-scripting path for the first post-
                  LIFELINE validation mission

SHORT THESIS LINE:
LIFELINE opened the artery. HOLDFAST proves Boston can keep blood moving through it.

CORE RULE THIS PAGE PROTECTS:
HOLDFAST must not feel like a generic defensive firefight. It is the first time
Romeo and the player experience a real two-way corridor under Vickers's widened
British support logic: wounded and civilians going out, matériel and support
coming in, and the occupation adapting fast enough to try to cut the route at
its thinnest point.

================================================================================
MISSION IDENTITY
================================================================================

TONAL IDENTITY:
- fragile logistical artery inside a half-liberated city
- urgency and movement rather than heroic static last stand
- enemy adaptation arriving immediately after progress
- proof that Vickers's briefing changed material reality, not just tone

GAMEPLAY IDENTITY:
- short-range escort and handoff
- route collapse and re-opening under pressure
- strongpoint clearing with movement as the real objective
- layered defense while medics, engineers, and litter teams keep the line alive
- protection of people and throughput, not just elimination of enemies

PLAYER FEEL TARGET:
"We finally have something real, and now we have to keep it alive."

================================================================================
KEY CAST
================================================================================

ROMEO
Playable. The ground-level force that makes the route viable once the enemy cuts it.

LT. DOROTHY ANNE VICKERS
Operational lead. Exact, unsentimental, calm. She never speaks as if the route is
safer than it is.

ELENA COSTA
Local district anchor. She decides who gets moved first and what must still stay.

PAT NAPIER
Central mission-facing use. He is the practical medical conscience of the mission.
Strongly load-bearing in triage, casualty movement, and the old-vs-new standard
of care contrast.

MACE
Light use only. Best used as bridge between covert British presence and Vickers's
widened phase. One or two interventions are enough.

ROYAL MARINES DETACHMENT
Mission-facing but not over-personalized. This is the first time the player
should feel them as a functioning protective layer rather than an abstract late-
game support label.

BRITISH MEDICAL / ENGINEERING PACKAGE
A compact inbound element. Small enough to feel plausible. Important enough to
become the mission's second stake once the route is cut.

OPENING BRIEFING SCENE
================================================================================

[Interior — Temporary district-side casualty collection point, pre-dawn.
A mechanic's workshop cut into the ground floor of a brick service building.
Shutters down. Side door open to a rear alley. Work lights hooded. Compressors,
jack stands, tool cabinets, oil stains, folded stretchers, blood on concrete.
The space was meant to keep vehicles alive. Tonight it is keeping people alive.

Two wounded fighters lie on litters near an engine hoist. A teenage boy with a
shrapnel bandage around his shoulder sits upright on a tyre stack, jaw clenched,
trying not to look afraid. A middle-aged woman in a winter coat breathes through
pain in one corner while Elena kneels beside her, speaking low.

Pat is at a folding table made from a door on saw horses. Tourniquets, gauze,
chest seals, a ledger, a grease pencil. He looks like he has been awake for far
too long and has decided that this is nobody else's problem.

Romeo comes in through the alley entrance with two cell fighters. The distant city
is quiet in the wrong way.

A field radio on the workbench hisses. Then Vickers.] 

VICKERS (RADIO):
Álvarez, report.

ROMEO:
At the collection point.

VICKERS (RADIO):
Good. Your first outbound movement steps in six minutes. I want your eyes on the
order before anybody starts improvising.

[Pat slides the grease-pencil board toward Romeo. Twelve names. Three crossed out.
One circled twice.] 

PAT:
Twelve out on the first pull. Four stretcher. Three walking wounded. Two civilians
can't be treated here much longer. One priority.

ROMEO:
Which one?

PAT:
The abdominal bleed by the drill press. He stays here and he dies slow.
He moves and maybe he gets lucky.

[Elena rises from the woman in the corner and crosses over.] 

ELENA:
And if we move the wrong people first, the block remembers that too.

PAT:
I'm aware.

[He is not defensive. Just tired.] 

PAT:
Before this route, these people died where they were lying.

[Beat.] 

PAT:
Now we've got a line. Thin one. That's better than nothing and worse than safe.

[Mace steps in from the alley, wet jacket, rifle low. He looks like he has been
outside since yesterday.] 

MACE:
Middle segment's clean for now.

ELENA:
For now.

MACE:
Yes. That's what I said.

[Pat marks the circled priority patient again.] 

PAT:
He goes in first package. No argument.

ELENA:
Agreed.

ROMEO:
Who walks with him?

PAT:
I do until midpoint. British medic takes him from there if the route still exists.

[The radio crackles.] 

VICKERS (RADIO):
Listen carefully.

[Pat turns the volume up. The whole room bends toward the voice without drama.] 

VICKERS (RADIO):
Outbound package one moves from your position to Junction Blue at zero four thirty.
Inbound package crosses from shore node to Junction Blue simultaneously, not before.
You do not outrun the overlap. You do not stack at the choke point. If contact
closes the middle, you hold what you have and wait for direction.

ROMEO:
Copy.

VICKERS (RADIO):
No, not copy. Understand.

ROMEO:
Understood.

VICKERS (RADIO):
Good. Inbound package is med stores, batteries, engineer kit, one signals case,
small protection element. If the route pinches, the support package becomes part
of the problem. Keep that in mind before anyone gets brave for cosmetic reasons.

ELENA:
They're going to feel the route working.

VICKERS (RADIO):
Yes.

ELENA:
If they can feel this line working, they'll come for it every day.

ROMEO:
Then we make every day cost them.

[Small beat.
That is not a speech. It is a working assumption.] 

VICKERS (RADIO):
Exactly. Move.

[Pat starts calling names. Litter teams lift. A resistance fighter wraps a blanket
around the woman in the corner. Romeo checks his rifle, looks once at Elena, then
at the shuttered exit.

Mission title card:
HOLDFAST]

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 1 — FIRST MOVEMENT
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

MISSION START STATE:
- player begins inside casualty collection point
- first outbound package is physically present and moving
- light British radio traffic heard in background, never overbearing
- player understands immediately that this mission is about moving people through
  a live route, not simply assaulting a fixed target

PRIMARY OBJECTIVE:
Escort Outbound Package One to Junction Blue.

SECONDARY OBJECTIVES:
- keep first package intact
- protect Pat while he accompanies the priority casualty
- suppress immediate threats without over-delaying movement

GAMEPLAY FLOW:
1. Player exits via rear alley with first litter team.
2. Route begins through tight service lane, broken fence cut-through, and a half-
   cleared loading court.
3. Light enemy contact begins at distance: single rifleman, then upper-window
   spotter, then two-man harassment team falling back.
4. Romeo clears path, but the mission tempo should feel like advancing a fragile
   body of people, not running point for an assault squad.

TRIGGER 1A — EXIT ALLEY
ELENA:
Keep them moving. Don't let the lane breathe on us.

PAT:
Priority litter first. Everyone else takes their pace from that frame.

ROMEO:
Move.

CELL FIGHTER:
Window, second floor.

ROMEO:
Seen.

[Player neutralizes upper-window rifleman or forces him off the angle.] 

PAT:
Good. Keep going.

BRITISH MEDIC (RADIO):
Stretcher ready on handoff.

VICKERS (RADIO):
Hold spacing. Hold spacing. Nobody bunches at the junction.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 2 — THE CUT
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TRANSITION NOTE:
The route must fail in a way that reads as enemy adaptation, not random bad luck.
The player should understand that the occupation has identified the corridor as a
real threat to control.

PRIMARY OBJECTIVE:
Survive the route severance and secure the wounded at Junction Blue.

EVENT CHAIN:
1. As outbound and inbound elements begin to overlap near Junction Blue, enemy
   indirect fire lands on the exposed road throat.
2. Simultaneously, MG / DMR fire opens from an elevated industrial shell.
3. A side-street team shoves scrap and a light truck across the lane, splitting
   the route into pockets.
4. The inbound support package halts short of the junction.
5. Pat and two litter teams are pinned on the inner side.

TRIGGER 2A — FIRST ROUND IMPACT
[One sharp incoming whistle. Then impact. Brick dust. Shattered glass. A vehicle
alarms once and dies.] 

PAT:
Down. Litters down. Down.

ROMEO:
Middle's blown. Get them under cover.

VICKERS (RADIO):
All stations, all stations, middle segment compromised. I say again, middle
segment compromised. Hold what you have. Do not stack the lane.

TRIGGER 2B — MG / DMR OVERWATCH ESTABLISHED
CELL FIGHTER:
Gun up high. North side shell. Gantry level.

PAT:
If that angle stays open, nobody crosses.

ROMEO:
Then it closes.

TRIGGER 2C — INBOUND PACKAGE STALLS
ROYAL MARINE (RADIO):
Inbound halted short. Obstacle across the lane. We can move on foot, not with the
cases.

VICKERS (RADIO):
No, that changes the route, not the job.

[Protected line.]

VICKERS (RADIO):
Álvarez, you're reopening the spine. Mark the gun. Mark the choke. Everything else
waits.

NEW PRIMARY OBJECTIVE:
Reopen the Corridor.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 3 — ROUTE SURGERY
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

MISSION IDENTITY SHIFT:
Escort becomes violent route repair. Romeo is no longer just guarding movement;
he is restoring it.

SUB-OBJECTIVES:
1. Eliminate MG / DMR overwatch team.
2. Disable enemy observation / comms node controlling the cut.
3. Clear obstacle emplacement at the choke point.

RECOMMENDED ENVIRONMENT:
- service corridor through warehouse annex
- stairwell to upper catwalk / gantry gun position
- roof edge or overpass service deck
- small relay room or construction office used as enemy local command cell
- narrow descent back to the lane for obstacle clearance

TRIGGER 3A — PUSH INTO WAREHOUSE ANNEX
MACE (RADIO, OPTIONAL):

ROMEO:
Understood.

TRIGGER 3B — INTERIOR CLEARING / STAIRS
CELL FIGHTER:
Movement above.

ROMEO:
Stairs. Fast.

TRIGGER 3C — GANTRY GUN POSITION CLEARED
[MG team neutralized. Fire on the lane drops instantly and noticeably.] 

PAT (RADIO):
That's it. That's the angle gone.

VICKERS (RADIO):
Good. Do not admire the work. Find their eyes.

TRIGGER 3D — OBSERVATION / COMMS ROOM
[Player enters a cramped upper office with field radio, drone feed tablet, and

ROMEO:
Found their picture.

VICKERS (RADIO):
Then erase it.

[Player destroys radio / tablet / local relay node.] 

TRIGGER 3E — DESCENT TO OBSTACLE
[Back on the lane edge, a side-street team is trying to finish blocking the route

ELENA (RADIO):
They're not just cutting it. They're trying to make us afraid to reuse it.

ROMEO:
Noted.

[Player clears the team and physically secures the choke point.] 

NEW PRIMARY OBJECTIVE:
Hold the Choke While the Line Reforms.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 4 — HOLD THE THROAT
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

MISSION PURPOSE:
The route is not truly open until movement resumes. This phase proves reopening
was worth the fight.

SUB-OBJECTIVES:
1. Cover engineers removing / bypassing the lane obstacle.
2. Protect British medics and Pat during the handoff of the priority casualty.
3. Keep inbound supplies from being overrun or abandoned.

GAMEPLAY SHAPE:
- layered defense across one exposed lane and two side entries
- emphasis on protecting working people rather than chasing kills
- visible movement of litters, medics, engineer kit, and battery / signals cases

TRIGGER 4A — ENGINEERS STEP IN
ROYAL ENGINEER:
We've got three minutes if nobody shoots the wheels off us.

ROMEO:
Then work.

TRIGGER 4B — PRIORITY CASUALTY HANDOFF
[Pat and British medic meet at the restored midpoint under fire.] 

BRITISH MEDIC:
We've got him.

PAT:
No, you've got half of him. Keep the pressure there and move.

BRITISH MEDIC:
Understood.

PAT:
Good.

[Pat's hand stays on the dressing one second too long before he lets go.
That is the emotional beat. Not a speech.] 

TRIGGER 4C — ENEMY COUNTER-SURGE FROM SIDE STREET
CELL FIGHTER:
Left side. Left side. They're pushing the breach.

ROMEO:
Hold them off the frame. The lane matters more.

TRIGGER 4D — VICKERS REFRAMES STAKES
VICKERS (RADIO):
Inbound package is now the stake. If they hit those cases, LIFELINE becomes a
story people tell each other about the one time it nearly worked.

ROMEO:
It worked.

VICKERS (RADIO):
Then prove it again.

TRIGGER 4E — SIGNALS CASE / BATTERY PACKAGE ARRIVES
[Small but crucial visual beat: waterproof signals case and battery crates cross
through the choke under Marine protection while litters move the other way.] 

ELENA (RADIO):
There. That's the city breathing.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 5 — LAST LEG TO SHORE NODE
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

MISSION PURPOSE:
The route has to complete, not merely survive. The player now escorts the reduced
outbound package through the last leg toward the British-held shore node.

PRIMARY OBJECTIVE:
Escort the Remaining Casualties to the Shore Node.

SECONDARY OBJECTIVE:
Ensure the inbound support package reaches the inner transfer point.

GAMEPLAY FLOW:
1. Route is open but unstable.
2. Enemy pressure becomes more distant and harassing rather than dominant.
3. The mission breathes just enough for the player to notice the new grammar of
   support: Royal Marines covering angles, medics already working, a shore-side
   military node that exists in more than rumor form.

TRIGGER 5A — MOVEMENT RESUMES
PAT:
Move. Move now before they remember how roads work.

TRIGGER 5B — APPROACH TO SHORE NODE
[The ferry maintenance hall / waterfront node comes into view under blackout
lighting and disciplined sector coverage.] 

ROYAL MARINE:
Shore node visual. Keep them straight in.

TRIGGER 5C — FINAL HARASSMENT FIRE
CELL FIGHTER:
Rear windows. Long shot.

ROMEO:
Ignore the noise. Keep walking.

[Important: player may suppress threats, but mission emphasis is completion of
movement, not hunting final stragglers.] 

MISSION COMPLETE TRIGGER:
First full two-way movement cycle successfully completed.

END SCENE

[Interior — British shore node, dawn beginning to grey the high windows.
The first real outbound package is inside. British medics work. One walking
wounded resistance fighter is sitting on an ammo crate drinking water and looking
slightly embarrassed to still be alive.

The priority casualty is rushed through a canvas-screened med corner. The player
never gets a neat answer yet on whether he survives. That uncertainty is part of
the tone.

Pat comes in late, jacket dark with other people's blood, and stops just inside
because he is suddenly unsure what to do with the fact that the route actually
worked.

Vickers is at the map board again. Different board now. Shore node side. The lane
that nearly died is marked back open in grease pencil.

Romeo steps up beside her. Elena arrives a beat later, eyes still on the medics.] 

VICKERS:
How many did you lose?

ROMEO:
None on the line.

VICKERS:
Good.

[She means it. She does not celebrate it.] 

ELENA:
They'll hit it again.

VICKERS:
Yes.

ELENA:
Harder.

VICKERS:
Probably.

[Beat.] 

VICKERS:
Now we know where harder is.

[Pat watches the med corner. One British medic leans out.] 

BRITISH MEDIC:
Napier.

[Pat goes immediately.] 

ROMEO:
This enough for tomorrow?

[Vickers looks at the board, then at the lane, then at him.] 

VICKERS:
No.

[Beat.] 

VICKERS:
It's enough to make tomorrow worth planning.

[Mission end.]
