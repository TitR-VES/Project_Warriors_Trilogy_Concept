================================================================================
MISSION FUNCTION
================================================================================

This mission is the first hard proof that Lt. Dorothy Anne Vickers's arrival is
not rhetorical. It is the mission where British widening stops being a briefing
claim and becomes a material gameplay reality for Romeo.

The mission's primary function is not spectacular destruction. Its purpose is to
open, hold, and validate the first dependable late-game British CASEVAC corridor
inside occupied Boston. The player should come away understanding three things:

1) the British package can do something the local resistance could not yet do on
   its own: create a usable extraction artery under pressure
2) that capability is limited, risky, and hard-earned rather than magically
   omnipresent
3) once it exists, Boston feels different to play because survival options have
   actually changed

This mission should be the first point in Romeo's Boston campaign where British
military CASEVAC exists as a mission-facing reality rather than background lore.
It should not arrive too early in the arc. The player should feel the contrast
with earlier Boston missions where wounded personnel had to be hidden, dragged,
carried, or abandoned to improvised solutions.


================================================================================
WORKING MISSION TITLE
================================================================================

Primary recommendation: LIFELINE

Why it works:
- clear link to casualty movement without sounding like a pure medical mission
- speaks to logistics, survival, and fragile continuity rather than heroics
- reads well on a mission card and in briefing language

Backup options:
- CLEAR RUN
- OPEN ROUTE
- SAFE CONDUCT

Recommendation: keep LIFELINE unless a later Boston mission takes a stronger
claim to that title.


================================================================================
PLACEMENT IN BOSTON ARC
================================================================================

Recommended placement: early-to-mid Act III, after Romeo's first proper contact
and briefing with Vickers, after the player has already worked with SAS/SBS lanes,
but before British support becomes comfortable or routine.

This should be the hinge mission that converts the British presence from:
"there are British operators here"
into:
"there is now a British-backed support artery inside this city"

Suggested structural location:
- after Vickers first-contact / first-briefing scene
- before the biggest harbor or shadow-government operations widen fully
- before late-game support becomes normalized


================================================================================
CORE PREMISE
================================================================================

AFC resistance elements and local civilians have been paying a mounting price for
being unable to move badly wounded personnel out of an eastern-to-central Boston
combat pocket fast enough. Vickers has identified a narrow, temporary opportunity
to create a protected evacuation corridor from a contested inland neighborhood to
a British-held receiving position tied to a covert shore-side logistics node.

The route is not broad enough to move a battalion. It is not a liberation push.
It is a survivability artery: enough width, time, and security to move wounded,
critical personnel, and limited high-priority materiel out before the occupation
can collapse the window.

Vickers's read is simple:
- the Russians are strong in static control and response mass
- they are weaker at once their surveillance picture is fractured and multiple
  small support nodes are hit nearly simultaneously
- Boston does not need a grand breakthrough here
- it needs one route that works tonight

The mission therefore becomes a deliberately controlled late-night corridor fight
through service streets, rail-edge access lanes, underpasses, loading yards, and
warehouse-adjacent hard cover rather than a broad front-line assault.


================================================================================
THE ROUTE ITSELF
================================================================================

The corridor should feel physically plausible, urban, and unglamorous.
Not downtown postcard Boston. Not a giant battlefield boulevard.

Recommended environmental character:
- outer industrial / light-commercial service belt feeding toward the harbor side
- truck yards, fenced depots, drainage cuts, rail spurs, loading bays, utility
  alleys, small underpasses, dead-end service roads, back-lot retaining walls
- intermittent residential edges or mixed-use blocks showing that the war is
  pressing right up against ordinary city life

The route should be broken into four tactical segments:
1) the pocket — where the wounded and pinned fighters actually are
2) the breach lane — first movement out through enemy-controlled overlap zone
3) the compression zone — the dangerous middle, where the route narrows and the
   enemy can still collapse it if not actively suppressed
4) the receiving end — a British-backed strongpoint / transfer site where CASEVAC
   becomes a real system and not just a promise


================================================================================
MISSION THESIS
================================================================================

This mission is about proving that support infrastructure is a form of power.
Not every turning point in liberation is a dramatic assault on a famous place.
Sometimes it is the first night your wounded stop dying where they fell.

That is what Vickers is bringing to Boston.


================================================================================
PLAYER PACKAGE / FRIENDLY FORCES
================================================================================

Primary playable character: Romeo

Primary mission-facing allied roles:
- Lt. Dorothy Anne Vickers — on mission net, partly forward, commanding the route
  architecture and making the timing calls
- Elena Costa — local resistance center of gravity, knows the streets and the
  human cost of losing the corridor if it fails
- limited Royal Marines detachment — disciplined late-arriving widening layer,
  holding nodes and transfer points rather than replacing the resistance
- one SAS bridge element, ideally Mace or a comparable operator, to preserve
  continuity with earlier British contact and show the covert-to-supported handoff
- local resistance movers / stretcher teams / drivers / armed escorts

Optional but useful supporting presence:
- Patch or Ghost on selective overwatch / counter-overwatch tasking
- a British medic / corpsman presence at the receiving node to make the unlock
  feel operationally real

The player should never feel like the Royal Marines have arrived to take the city
away from the resistance. They are there to make a specific artery function.


================================================================================
MISSION SHAPE
================================================================================

PHASE 0 — BRIEFING / SETUP

The mission opens from the tail of Vickers's planning rhythm rather than from a
big cinematic launch. This should feel like work already in motion.

Key briefing beats:
- casualty numbers in the pocket are getting unacceptable
- the existing improvised AFC routes are too visible, too slow, or too degraded
- British receiving position is ready, but only if Romeo's side can punch and
  hold the inland half of the artery
- this is not an all-night operation; the route exists only for a short window
- if the enemy recognizes the corridor for what it is, they can seal it by weight

Vickers's tone should make the mission immediately different from earlier Boston
operations: exact, unsentimental, already thinking about throughput, timing, and
how many bodies the route can realistically carry.

Suggested Vickers line:
"We're not clearing Boston tonight. We're opening a vein and keeping it open long
 enough to matter."


PHASE 1 — GET TO THE POCKET

Romeo and his element move into a resistance-held but compressed block where
wounded fighters, civilians, and one or two load-bearing people are trapped.
This opening phase establishes the cost of not having the corridor.

Gameplay goals:
- move through tight streets and interior cut-throughs under intermittent fire
- stabilize scattered resistance holdouts
- reach a temporary casualty collection point in a school basement, loading bay,
  church hall, mechanic's shop, or similar grounded civilian space
- assess who must move first and what cannot be left behind

Key emotional effect:
The player sees the bottleneck. The city has brave people, weapons, and will, but
it does not yet have enough movement capacity.


PHASE 2 — KNOCK OUT THE FIRST LOCK

The occupation controls the exit with overlapping MG positions, spotters, and a
small command / comms node that keeps the local response picture coherent.
Romeo's first task is not to sprint casualties forward. It is to break the local
kill geometry.

Gameplay goals:
- clear or flank two route-dominating strongpoints
- destroy or disable a surveillance / relay element watching the corridor mouth
- coordinate with a small British support element already in place on one flank
- create the first actual opening so the route can begin functioning

This phase should prove that the corridor is a combined product:
local knowledge + Romeo's violence of action + British timing and support logic.


PHASE 3 — FIRST MOVEMENT

Once the first lock is broken, the route starts working immediately.
This is the moment the mission differentiates itself.

Instead of a pure advance, the player begins fighting while movement happens
behind and around him:
- stretcher teams and walking wounded begin crossing the first segment
- local vehicles or improvised carts appear where appropriate
- British and AFC net traffic starts talking in throughput terms, not just kills
- the player sees the city behaving differently because support has become real

Vickers should not sound excited. She should sound like a commander counting a
narrow success and already preparing for the counterpunch.

Suggested Vickers line:
"Good. Keep them moving. Do not admire it."


PHASE 4 — THE COMPRESSION ZONE

This is the mission's signature phase.

The corridor narrows through a rail-edge choke, depressed service road, utility
underpass, loading lane, or fenced industrial cut where casualties and escorts
are vulnerable to enfilade fire, drones, mortar fragments, or vehicle interception.
If the enemy closes this zone, the whole operation fails.

Gameplay goals:
- move ahead of the casualty flow and clear the narrowest point
- prevent a Russian response unit from sealing the route with a vehicle block,
  machine-gun team, or light armor presence
- fight through hard, ugly, practical geometry rather than a cinematic plaza
- potentially divert briefly off-route to neutralize a rooftop or bridge-line
  overwatch element the British cannot safely hit from their angle

This is where Vickers proves she is not bluffing and not improvising blindly.
Her route architecture should show itself in how many things are happening at once:
- Marines hold the far side node
- local resistance moves casualties
- one British element handles rear security
- Romeo is the knife opening the narrow throat before it shuts


PHASE 5 — FIRST BRITISH CASEVAC NODE

Romeo reaches the receiving zone: the first explicitly British-backed late-game
CASEVAC position in Boston.

Recommended form:
- a hidden but now active British military receiving site in a warehouse shell,
  ferry maintenance yard, dry service building, pierside utility annex, or dead
  industrial structure near the waterfront approach
- not a glowing high-tech medbay
- practical lighting, stretchers, triage tables, warmed fluids, ammunition crates,
  Marine sentries, route markers, engines idling, radio discipline, wet-weather kit

This is the mission's biggest payoff.
The player has earned the sight of a functioning British position that can actually
receive casualties and move them onward.

Gameplay goals:
- bring the first wave in under fire or immediately after contact
- defend the node from a final probing attack or indirect strike attempt
- maintain perimeter while triage and transfer begin
- visually and mechanically establish that this node is now available in later
  missions as a real support concept, not a one-off set dressing miracle

Suggested emotional beat:
The player watches wounded people disappear through a door and not come back out
onto the same floor bleeding. That alone changes the feel of the campaign.


PHASE 6 — HOLD UNTIL THE LAST TRUCK / LAST STRETCHER

The route does not end when the player arrives at the receiving site. It ends when
enough of the trapped people are through.

Final combat problem:
- enemy probes the route from the inland side after recognizing what is happening
- small but dangerous attempt to collapse the node with artillery fragments,
  suicide drone, vehicle push, or coordinated infantry rush
- Romeo and the British / AFC mixture must hold long enough for the last movement
  cycle to complete

This final phase should feel less like climax-as-spectacle and more like the hard,
professional end of an operation that mattered.

Suggested closing Vickers line after the last movement clears:
"Route is spent. Close it. We got enough."

That line matters because it keeps the British package grounded. The corridor is
not magically permanent after one mission. What is permanent is the fact that the
coalition now knows it can be done.


================================================================================
MISSION OUTCOME
================================================================================

Primary success condition:
- the route functions long enough to extract a meaningful number of wounded,
  trapped fighters, and one or more key personnel

Secondary outcome:
- British-backed receiving node is validated and becomes a repeatable late-game
  campaign logic element

Narrative outcome:
- Vickers's authority is proven by results, not by rank alone
- Romeo's trust in the widened British layer deepens because he has now seen what
  they can materially change
- the player feels a tangible campaign-state shift: Boston is still occupied and
  dangerous, but survival is no longer as narrow as it was yesterday


================================================================================
HOW THIS SHOULD CHANGE THE CAMPAIGN AFTERWARD
================================================================================

After this mission, later Boston missions should show evidence that the corridor
operation changed the city even if the exact same route is not always open.

Recommended persistent consequences:
- British CASEVAC becomes available in selected later missions as a late-game option
- player briefings can now reference British receiving points or transfer positions
  as real assets
- background chatter and environmental state show resistance confidence increasing
- local fighters talk about being able to move their wounded, even if cautiously
- British and AFC planning language becomes more integrated and less introductory

This mission is the unlock proof.
The player should feel it in subsequent mission design.


================================================================================
TONE / REGISTER RULES
================================================================================

- Do not write this as a triumphant liberation parade
- Do not make Vickers overly speechifying or Churchillian
- Do not make the British layer feel like a deus ex machina
- Keep the route ugly, practical, and urban-service in character
- Keep the emotional center on survivability, not flag-waving
- Show care through procedure, timing, throughput, and discipline

The win condition is not that everyone lives.
The win condition is that enough people do.


================================================================================
GAMEPLAY / SYSTEM FUNCTION
================================================================================

This mission is the natural place to tutorialize or soft-introduce late-game
British CASEVAC logic for Romeo without turning it into a systems lecture.

Potential gameplay functions:
- carrying or escorting a casualty to a British safe zone becomes mission-facing
- the player learns what a British receiving node looks and sounds like
- later missions can use the same support logic with less explanation
- the campaign progression rule from earlier design notes is preserved: Romeo's
  support options expand because the coalition has materially formed around him

This mission should feel like the narrative proof behind a mechanics unlock.


================================================================================
ROLE OF ELENA COSTA
================================================================================

Elena is strongly recommended as the local human anchor in this mission.
Why:
- she keeps the operation rooted in Boston rather than turning it into a British
  command show
- she embodies the local cost of not having the route
- her presence lets the mission dramatize coalition friction without melodrama

Useful Elena function:
- she knows which streets are dead, which ones civilians still use, which corner
  is watched by collaborators, which garage wall can be breached, which alley only
  looks open, and which holdout cell will move when ordered

This lets Vickers and Elena complement each other:
- Vickers understands corridor logic, timing, and support architecture
- Elena understands the neighborhood as lived terrain

Romeo stands in the middle and makes the route violent enough to exist.


================================================================================
ROLE OF MACE
================================================================================

Mace is useful here as the continuity bridge between the earlier covert British
presence and the widened Vickers phase.

Best use:
- small but load-bearing role at the corridor mouth or route flank
- one or two lines that make clear he trusts Vickers's read without sentimentality
- no need to dominate the mission

Suggested Mace line:
"Told you she meant it."

Short, dry, enough.


================================================================================
VISUAL / ATMOSPHERIC IDENTITY
================================================================================

Night recommended.
Cold damp air, sodium-vapor spill, emergency lights, wet concrete, distant harbor
noise, intermittent generator hum, rail clank, sirens too far away to matter.

The British receiving node should look almost shockingly functional by contrast
with the ruined improvisation the player has been living in:
- clean tourniquets laid out
- Marines on sectors
- radios working
- stretchers staged before the wounded arrive
- somebody already writing things down

The point is not luxury.
It is organized competence.

================================================================================
WHY THIS MISSION MATTERS IN THE ARC
================================================================================

Without this mission, Vickers risks reading as a persuasive briefer attached to a
late-game support package.

With this mission, she becomes what she should be:
not the British character who gives Romeo a speech,
but the British character who changes what survival looks like in Boston.
