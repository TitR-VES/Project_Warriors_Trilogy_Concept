MISSION PURPOSE
Backlash mission after DAY WINDOW.
The occupiers stop treating the district war as a nuisance and begin treating it as a
public-order and identity-control problem.

ENEMY POSTURE
- seal district;
- pull residents into screening lines;
- check identities and faces;
- map house-by-house patterns;
- mark suspected helpers;
- remove selected residents;
- rebuild control through records, cameras, aid-table theater, and exemplary punishment.

RESISTANCE OBJECTIVES
1. pull the most exposed locals off the public face;
2. infiltrate school-side processing annex/archive node;
3. destroy screening records and seize/kill any live flagged list;
4. break detainee transfer if it begins.

BRIEFING CORE
Protected dialogue:
ELENA: “Yesterday they saw us. Today they want names.”
LOCAL GUIDE: “Aid tables too. Water, maybe blankets. They’ll make it look tidy while
they count who still lives here.”
ELENA: “They’re not retaking the district. They’re reading it.”
ELENA: “Nobody disappears because they stood in the short route yesterday.”
ROMEO: “And if we just hit the line hard.”
ELENA: “No. That’s what they want.”
ELENA: “A city can live through gunfire. Lists are harder.”
LOCAL GUIDE: “They don’t need to kill the block if they can learn it.”

MISSION FLOW
Phase 1 — Read the sweep
- observe school lot, screening lanes, archive/records position, propaganda cameras,
  aid-table presentation, door-to-door teams, and transfer vehicles;
- show administrative violence before open combat.

Core point:
The occupiers are converting visibility into data.

Phase 2 — Pull the exposed
- quietly move Shopkeeper Woman, families, known route helpers, and exposed observers;
- use back rooms, shared walls, service cuts, and local cover identities;
- not everyone can leave at once.

Protected dialogue direction:
- locals resist abandoning homes/businesses;
- Romeo remains task-first;
- Elena prioritizes people most exposed by DAY WINDOW.

Phase 3 — Into the annex
- infiltrate through service, boiler, utility, or school maintenance route;
- avoid triggering the public screening line prematurely;
- encounter data technicians, records handlers, and security rather than only combat troops.

Phase 4 — Burn the lists
- destroy paper rosters, captured images, house maps, biometric/ID caches, and marked
  route associations;
- seize any list that reveals compromised locals or a deeper occupation source;
- do not romanticize data destruction: it is immediate life protection.

Protected thematic line:
ELENA/LOCAL GUIDE logic: a block is harder to break when it can return to being unknown.

Phase 5 — Break the transfer
- detainee vehicles begin moving;
- resistance ambushes transfer route rather than assaulting screening crowd;
- free selected residents before they disappear into wider system;
- keep civilian lines from becoming the firefight's center.

Phase 6 — The street chooses you
- operation becomes public despite efforts;
- residents may create noise, block sightlines, misdirect occupiers, or refuse orderly
  compliance;
- resistance must adapt without turning civilians into disposable cover.

Phase 7 — Give the district back its unknowns
- destroy remaining records;
- remove cameras or storage;
- scatter or change route markers;
- move freed detainees into multiple local paths;
- deliberately prevent a single obvious resistance exfiltration pattern.

Phase 8 — Elena debrief
Core outcome:
- ground is not newly captured;
- the enemy's district picture is damaged;
- local names and relationships regain uncertainty;
- resistance learns public momentum creates administrative retaliation.

Protected conceptual close:
The occupiers now understand Boston's district war is organized. Elena understands that
future victories must account for what the enemy learns from them.

MISSION END STATE
- flagged residents survive;
- screening archive is destroyed or compromised;
- detainees are freed;
- the district regains partial anonymity;
- the resistance chooses a deliberate kept-ground counteroffensive next: THE POCKET.
