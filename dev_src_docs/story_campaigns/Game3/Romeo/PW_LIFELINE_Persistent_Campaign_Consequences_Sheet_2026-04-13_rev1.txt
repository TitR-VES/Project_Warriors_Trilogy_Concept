================================================================================
PURPOSE
================================================================================

This page locks the persistent campaign-state consequences of LIFELINE, the first
successful corridor-establishment / British CASEVAC-route mission in Boston.

Its purpose is to make sure the mission does not behave like a one-night action
sequence that leaves no mark on the wider campaign. LIFELINE must change Boston
in ways the player can feel afterward through mission structure, support logic,
environmental state, civilian behavior, enemy posture, and coalition confidence.

The key design rule is simple:
Boston should not feel liberated after LIFELINE.
Boston should feel less alone after LIFELINE.


================================================================================
PRIMARY CONSEQUENCE LOCK
================================================================================

After LIFELINE, the player should understand that a late-game British-backed
support artery now exists inside the city.

This does NOT mean:
- open, consequence-free movement across Boston
- routine helicopter rescue on demand
- a broad conventional military corridor under coalition dominance
- the British taking over the campaign from Romeo and the local resistance

It DOES mean:
- there is now at least one validated British receiving / transfer concept inside
  Boston that can be activated in selected later missions
- wounded resistance personnel no longer automatically disappear into improvised
  cellars, basements, and hopeless delays if the story and mission conditions
  support movement to the British node
- Romeo's campaign support logic has materially advanced, and the player should
  feel that advancement without needing a systems lecture


================================================================================
CAMPAIGN-STATE SHIFT — HIGH LEVEL
================================================================================

Before LIFELINE:
- resistance movement of wounded is slow, improvised, and often local-only
- British support is credible but still partly abstract from the player's point
  of view
- Vickers is promising capability the player has not yet fully seen
- survival in Boston still feels brutally narrow

After LIFELINE:
- the coalition has proven it can create and defend a usable extraction artery
- British military competence is no longer just something Romeo is told about;
  it has become a mission-facing reality
- Vickers's authority now rests on demonstrated throughput and results
- Boston still feels occupied, but the campaign's emotional register changes from
  isolated endurance toward coordinated resistance with selective external depth


================================================================================
MECHANICAL / SUPPORT CONSEQUENCES
================================================================================

1) BRITISH CASEVAC AVAILABILITY UNLOCK

LIFELINE is the proof mission behind late-game British CASEVAC availability in
selected Boston missions.

Lock:
- British CASEVAC does not become universal after LIFELINE
- it becomes conditionally available in missions where route security, distance,
  timing, weather, and British presence make it plausible
- future mission briefings can now refer to British receiving points, transfer
  positions, or route windows without the player asking "since when?"

Design effect:
The player feels that coalition growth through newly available survivability
options rather than through a cutscene announcing a faction unlock.


2) BRITISH MILITARY POSITION AS SAFE-ZONE CONCEPT

LIFELINE permanently establishes the visual and mechanical language of a British
military position in Boston:
- disciplined perimeter
- practical triage capability
- transfer staging under pressure
- route markers, discipline, paperwork, actual continuity

Once seen here, later missions may reuse this safe-zone concept with less setup.


3) SUPPORT LANGUAGE NORMALIZATION

Before LIFELINE, British support language in Romeo's arc is introductory.
After LIFELINE, planning language can become more integrated and matter-of-fact:
- "Route window opens in twelve"
- "British node can take two more litter cases"
- "Push him to the receiving site"
- "Vickers says the lane is live"

This matters because the campaign should sound different after the mission.
The coalition is no longer theoretical.


================================================================================
MISSION-DESIGN CONSEQUENCES FOR LATER BOSTON OPERATIONS
================================================================================

1) MORE AGGRESSIVE PLAYER DECISION SPACE

Once the player knows that a real receiving node exists, later missions can ask
Romeo to take risks that would previously have been irresponsible.

Examples:
- hold a block longer because casualties can actually be moved
- commit to a harder breach knowing a rearward transfer position exists
- rescue a badly wounded NPC because there is now somewhere credible to send them
- retain a more ambitious objective package because the mission no longer collapses
  the moment someone important goes down

This should not make the campaign easy.
It should make it deeper.


2) MULTI-STAGE OPERATIONS BECOME MORE PLAUSIBLE

After LIFELINE, Boston missions can more credibly split into:
- contact phase
- transfer / stabilization phase
- renewed push

That structure was harder to justify earlier because the city lacked reliable
late-game support depth.


3) LARGER OBJECTIVE STACKS BECOME BELIEVABLE

Missions after LIFELINE can support slightly denser objective loads because the
campaign now has more back-end operational credibility.

Not because the player got stronger in a gamey sense.
Because the coalition can now sustain more complicated nights.


================================================================================
ENVIRONMENTAL CONSEQUENCES ON REVISIT
================================================================================

Revisited Boston spaces after LIFELINE should show evidence that the mission
changed the city physically.

Recommended recurring signs:
- improvised route markers, chevrons, chalk symbols, low-light tape, or coded
  corridor markings left by resistance / British coordination teams
- drag marks, stretcher wear, spent medical packaging, discarded IV bags,
  tourniquet wrappers, burned-out flares, traffic cones, sandbags, casualty tags
- a once-chaotic rear building now visibly functioning as a transfer point, or as
  the ghost of one after the window has closed
- fresh breach work in walls, cut fencing, cleared alley clutter, moved vehicles,
  broken concrete that shows a route was made and used
- civilian spaces repurposed for disciplined emergency use rather than random
  collapse alone

Important rule:
The city should not look cleaner after LIFELINE.
It should look more organized in specific scars.


================================================================================
CIVILIAN / RESISTANCE CONSEQUENCES
================================================================================

1) CONFIDENCE SHIFTS, BUT ONLY PARTWAY

Resistance morale should rise after LIFELINE, but not into triumph.
The relevant shift is not "we are winning."
It is:
"If somebody gets hit, maybe they do not just die where they fall anymore."

That is a huge emotional change and a small strategic one. Exactly right.


2) CIVILIAN BEHAVIOR BECOMES MORE PURPOSEFUL IN CERTAIN POCKETS

Selected civilian-facing spaces should show that news of the route has traveled:
- people preparing rooms, blankets, water, and bandages because they now believe
  handoff is possible
- lookouts taking route discipline more seriously
- local drivers or couriers volunteering for one dangerous run because the route
  has proven it can work
- fewer despair-coded background beats in the immediate corridor-adjacent districts
  and more exhausted purposeful ones


3) ELENA'S LOCAL AUTHORITY DEEPENS

LIFELINE should strengthen Elena not because she gains rank, but because Vickers's
system has now validated Elena's local knowledge as battle-winning.

After LIFELINE:
- more cells defer faster when Elena says a route is dead or usable
- neighborhood fighters trust mixed British/AFC timing more because they have seen
  it save lives
- Elena's relationship with Vickers shifts from cautious coalition management to
  earned professional reciprocity


================================================================================
ROMEO CONSEQUENCES
================================================================================

1) ROMEO'S TRUST THRESHOLD MOVES

Before LIFELINE, Romeo can respect Vickers without fully internalizing what her
layer means.
After LIFELINE, he has seen her change a city-level survival problem into an
operational system.

Effect on Romeo:
- he becomes more willing to plan around British support windows
- he can still question British intent, timing, or priorities, but not their value
- his campaign posture shifts slightly from local improviser forced to do
  everything alone toward coalition field leader able to exploit allied depth


2) ROMEO'S VOICE CAN QUIETLY CHANGE

Later Romeo dialogue can carry a subtle shift from:
- "Can they help?"
toward:
- "What can they hold?"
- "How long is the lane live?"
- "Can Vickers take them?"

This is a writing consequence as much as a systems consequence.


================================================================================
VICKERS CONSEQUENCES
================================================================================

1) AUTHORITY THROUGH RESULTS

After LIFELINE, Vickers no longer needs to persuade the player that she is load-
bearing. The campaign should treat that as settled.

Her later briefings can therefore become more compressed, more technical, and
less introductory.


2) HER PACKAGE IS NOW REAL TO THE PLAYER

The Royal Marines / Royal Navy layer should feel different after the mission.
Not larger in raw spectacle, but more believable.
The player now knows what this package does:
- open temporary arterial support
- hold receiving points
- provide disciplined rearward continuity under pressure
- make Boston survivable in ways the resistance alone could not yet manage


3) VICKERS / MACE HANDOFF BECOMES ORGANIC

LIFELINE should reduce the narrative seam between the earlier SAS lane and the
later Vickers phase. Mace no longer feels like Romeo's British contact while
Vickers feels like a separate plot arrival. The player has now seen the two lanes
function as parts of the same British intervention.


================================================================================
ENEMY CONSEQUENCES
================================================================================

This is critical. The occupation must learn from LIFELINE.
If the enemy does not adapt, the mission cheapens the campaign.

Recommended enemy response shifts:

1) ROUTE-HUNTING BEHAVIOR
- more active effort to identify transfer points, not just front-line cells
- greater attention to service roads, loading lanes, rail-edge cuts, and rear-area
  support traffic
- occasional punitive shelling or drone attacks against suspected evacuation nodes

2) COUNTER-CORRIDOR TACTICS
- enemy attempts to seal lanes with wrecks, burning vehicles, mines, or MG teams
- faster response to comms blackout and missing surveillance nodes in areas the
  British are likely to use again
- greater emphasis on collaborator or observer networks feeding route information

3) PROPAGANDA / PSYCHOLOGICAL RESPONSE
- occupation messaging may try to frame the British as using Boston civilians as
  cover, or claim the corridor was a staged extraction for foreign assets only
- this is useful because it shows the enemy understood the operation mattered

LIFELINE should help the player.
It should also make future route work harder.


================================================================================
MAP / DISTRICT CONSEQUENCES
================================================================================

Recommended persistent district-state changes in corridor-adjacent areas:
- one district feels marginally more connected to the harbor side than before
- another feels more threatened because the enemy is now searching for route logic
- certain interior paths, breach holes, or fenced service cuts remain usable as
  traversal options in later missions, though not always under safe conditions
- one British-backed receiving structure may remain active only intermittently,
  while another future site opens elsewhere using the same operational model

The important rule is modular continuity:
The exact same corridor need not stay open forever.
But the city now knows what a corridor is.


================================================================================
HARBOR-ARC CONSEQUENCES
================================================================================

LIFELINE should quietly strengthen the later harbor arc and the Boston Harbor
Strike logic in three ways:

1) it proves British logistics are present, not abstract
2) it proves the coalition can synchronize inland and shore-side timing
3) it gives the player a lived sense that disrupting occupation supply and opening
   extraction arteries are part of the same wider struggle over who can move what
   through Boston

This helps the harbor operations feel like escalation, not genre drift.


================================================================================
WHAT THE PLAYER SHOULD FEEL IN THE NEXT THREE MISSIONS
================================================================================

Mission immediately after LIFELINE:
- relief mixed with caution
- the coalition can now do something new, but everyone knows the enemy will react

One mission later:
- route logic begins to affect planning language, traversal choices, and casualty
  expectations

Two to three missions later:
- the player no longer thinks of British CASEVAC as a surprise feature; it has
  become part of how late Boston works

That psychological normalization is the whole point.


================================================================================
REVISIT / BARK / RADIO CONSEQUENCE GUIDANCE
================================================================================

Recommended ambient lines after LIFELINE:
- "British took three through last night. Still breathing."
- "Do not bunch at the lane. They close it, you die there."
- "Vickers's people marked the wall. Follow the second arrow, not the first."
- "If he drops, we can move him now. We could not last week."
- "They brought hot plasma. Actual hot plasma."
- "They'll hit the route next. Count on it."

Recommended radio effect:
Net traffic becomes fractionally more procedural and less desperate whenever the
British layer is in play. That tonal shift should be perceptible but understated.


================================================================================
WHAT MUST NOT HAPPEN
================================================================================

Do not let LIFELINE produce any of the following false readings:
- Boston is basically won now
- the British have solved the resistance's core problems
- casualties no longer matter
- Vickers can rescue anyone anywhere
- the occupation's rear areas are now soft
- coalition coordination is frictionless

LIFELINE is a survivability breakthrough.
It is not strategic closure.


================================================================================
CLEAN LOCK RECOMMENDATION
================================================================================

Lock the aftermath of LIFELINE as follows:

LIFELINE is the mission that proves a British-backed extraction artery can exist
inside occupied Boston. After it, selected late-game British CASEVAC options,
receiving nodes, and route-window planning become plausible and playable. The
city does not become safe; it becomes more operationally survivable. Civilian and
resistance behavior grows more purposeful in corridor-adjacent districts, enemy
forces begin actively hunting route logic, and Vickers's authority becomes rooted
in demonstrated results rather than promised support. Boston should feel changed
not by spectacle, but by the sudden fact that some of the wounded now make it out.
