TITLE / PURPOSE
================================================================================

MISSION TITLE: HOLDFAST
CAMPAIGN: Romeo — Game 3 Boston arc
PLACEMENT: Immediately after LIFELINE and its persistent-consequence shift;
           late Act III moving toward Act IV
MISSION TYPE: corridor validation / two-way movement / anti-severance action
FUNCTION: first post-LIFELINE follow-up mission that cashes out the newly opened
          British-backed CASEVAC corridor as a real campaign-changing artery

CORE RULE THIS PAGE PROTECTS:
LIFELINE must not feel like a one-off scripted miracle. HOLDFAST exists to prove
that the route Vickers opened can actually be used under pressure, in both
 directions, by real people carrying real stakes.

SHORT THESIS LINE:
LIFELINE opened the artery. HOLDFAST proves Boston can keep blood moving through it.

================================================================================
WHY THIS MISSION EXISTS
================================================================================

The Boston arc already establishes that British military CASEVAC becomes a late
campaign capability once the coalition has materially formed around Romeo's
operation. That unlock only matters if the player immediately experiences what it
changes.

HOLDFAST is the first mission where that new reality becomes practical rather than
abstract:
- wounded fighters and civilians can actually move out through a protected route
- British matériel, med support, and technical personnel can move in through the
  same route
- the enemy recognizes the corridor as strategically dangerous and adapts to cut
  it rather than merely absorb its existence

The mission therefore serves three purposes at once:
1. validates LIFELINE as real campaign progression
2. demonstrates Vickers's widening layer as materially useful, not rhetorical
3. teaches the player that a corridor is not "won" once — it must be kept open

================================================================================
RECOMMENDED NARRATIVE POSITION
================================================================================

BEST FIT:
The mission should occur soon after Vickers's first-contact / first-briefing scene
and immediately after the player has had just enough time to feel the LIFELINE
campaign consequences in ambient form.

This timing matters. The player should have had one or two quieter signs that the
route exists — new planning language, visible British matériel, changed safe-zone
behavior, more confidence around casualty handling — before being asked to defend
that change in a mission.

================================================================================
CORE MISSION IDEA
================================================================================

The first full two-way movement through the newly opened corridor is scheduled
under Vickers's authority.

Outbound:
- wounded resistance fighters
- selected wounded civilians who can no longer be treated safely inside the
  district
- one medically fragile priority patient whose survival would have been doubtful
  before LIFELINE

Inbound:
- British medical supplies
- ammunition and batteries
- compact field engineering package for route hardening / barricade improvement
- limited signals equipment to strengthen the inner handoff node
- a small Royal Marines protection element and technical personnel

The movement is intentionally modest. This is not a triumphant road march.
It is the first careful proof that the artery can be used without collapsing.

The enemy has been watching. Once the first real pattern appears, Russian local
command moves quickly to sever the corridor at its thinnest point.

================================================================================
MISSION IDENTITY
================================================================================

TONAL IDENTITY:
Not a giant British assault. Not a pure medevac set piece. Not a convoy mission in
open country.

HOLDFAST should feel like:
- a fragile logistical artery inside a half-liberated city
- urgent movement through streets that were only just made usable
- tactical proof that coalition support changes what resistance can sustain
- enemy adaptation arriving faster than comfort does

GAMEPLAY IDENTITY:
- short-movement escort and handoff
- dismounted route reopening
- strongpoint clearing under time pressure
- layered defense while the corridor is reconstituted
- protection of people and movement, not just elimination of targets

PLAYER FEEL TARGET:
"We finally have something real — and now we have to keep it alive."

================================================================================
KEY CAST / CHARACTER USE
================================================================================

ROMEO:
Playable. He is the force that keeps the route from being cut. The mission should
feel like Romeo translating Vickers's larger framework into ground truth at the
block level.

LT. DOROTHY ANNE VICKERS:
Operational lead. Calm, exact, unsentimental. She does not oversell the route or
pretend it is secure. Her authority should come through clarity, timing, and the
fact that what she said in briefing is now materially happening.

ELENA COSTA:
Local district-side anchor. She represents the part of Boston that has to trust
this new arrangement without surrendering local control. She helps choose who goes
out first and what matters enough to bring in.

PAT NAPIER:
Strong recommended use. This mission is an excellent place to cash out Pat's role
as Romeo's practical combat medic. He should be centrally involved in deciding who
must move, what can wait, and how ugly the pre-route standard of care used to be.
His interaction with British military medicine should be professional, wary, and
quietly meaningful rather than sentimental.

MACE:
Optional but recommended light use. He works best here as the dry bridge between
covert British presence and Vickers's widened phase. He does not need to dominate
screen time; a brief planning contribution or flank update is enough.

ROYAL MARINES DETACHMENT:
Mission-facing but not over-personalized. This is the first time the player should
feel them as a functioning protective layer rather than a late-game abstract.

================================================================================
RECOMMENDED ENVIRONMENT / ROUTE CHARACTER
================================================================================

GENERAL GEOGRAPHY:
Keep the route in the Boston industrial-service / rail-edge / back-street logic
already established for late Boston rather than moving into a broad boulevard war
fantasy.

BEST SPATIAL CHARACTER:
- broken service roads
- warehouse edges
- half-cleared intersections
- underpasses and retaining walls
- a narrow exposed crossing overlooked by enemy positions
- one interior handoff node that can plausibly become a repeat-use casualty
  collection point afterward

The route should feel barely sufficient rather than comfortably secure.

PROTECTED LEVEL-DESIGN RULE:
The mission should look constricted and compromised without becoming mechanically
frustrating. Visually claustrophobic, navigably honest.

================================================================================
MISSION FLOW — RECOMMENDED STRUCTURE
================================================================================

PHASE 1 — THE FIRST MOVEMENT
Romeo begins at the interior district-side collection point.

State of play:
- wounded already assembled
- Pat and local helpers triaging hard
- Elena deciding who moves first and who still has to wait
- inbound British package confirmed moving toward midpoint
- Vickers establishing windows, timings, and fallback language over comms

Player tasks:
- secure immediate departure area
- escort first outbound package through the first leg
- establish that this is a real two-way movement system, not a cutscene

Key feeling:
The route exists, but nobody trusts it yet.


PHASE 2 — THE CUT
At the route's thinnest and most exposed middle segment, the enemy makes its move.

Recommended enemy action:
- pre-sighted mortar or light indirect fire onto a known choke point
- machine-gun / DMR overwatch from an upper industrial shell or overpass edge
- rushed obstacle emplacement from side streets to split outbound and inbound
  elements
- optional drone / recon feed implication rather than futuristic swarm spectacle

Result:
- vehicles cannot continue cleanly
- the route is fragmented into pockets
- the first outbound movement risks becoming a trap
- the inbound support package stalls short of the inner node

Critical design note:
This must feel like the enemy learning, not like a random firefight.
They are responding to a newly dangerous pattern in the city.


PHASE 3 — ROMEO REOPENS THE SPINE
The mission shifts from escort to violent route surgery.

Romeo dismounts with a small element and clears the positions cutting the artery.
This should be a compact, pressurized sequence through the built fabric surrounding
 the choke point:
- stairwells / service corridors / loading-bay interiors
- rooftop or gantry gun position
- flanking rooms controlling the lane below
- one route-blocking emplacement that must be physically neutralized or bypassed

The point is not body count. The point is to restore movement.

Recommended protected line of logic:
Romeo is not "taking the objective" in the abstract. He is making it possible for
people to keep moving.


PHASE 4 — THE INBOUND PACKAGE BECOMES THE STAKE
Once the choke point is partly reopened, the inbound British element becomes exposed
rather than safely off-screen.

Recommended inbound contents:
- med crates / blood / airway kit / hard-to-source sustainment items
- radio batteries and compact relay gear
- engineer stores for route hardening
- one or two Royal Marines and a technical specialist whose survival matters

Now the player has to:
- link up with the stalled inbound package
- move it through the reopened but still vulnerable corridor segment
- protect both the outbound wounded and the inbound capacity that will make future
  use of the corridor possible

This is the phase that proves the route is valuable in both directions.


PHASE 5 — INNER NODE HARDENING
The mission's final movement is not back to calm. It is into function.

Romeo returns with the inbound package to the inner handoff node, where the player
must secure a perimeter long enough for the first real British-backed interior
casualty collection / stabilization point to come online.

Recommended tasks:
- clear remaining enemy pressure around the node
- hold perimeter while equipment is unpacked and basic routing established
- protect med staff / tech personnel / local helpers
- possibly move one critical casualty to the new treatment room under time pressure

Important tone rule:
This should not read as a pristine field hospital magically appearing.
It should read as a hard-won, improvised, militarily credible stabilization space
that is vastly better than what the district had before.


PHASE 6 — SECOND WAVE / MISSION PROOF
Before mission end, there should be one final confirming beat:
- another casualty successfully moved out
- another needed item successfully moved in
- Vickers or Pat confirming in precise language that this changes what the district
  can survive

That beat is the mission's emotional lock.
The player must not leave wondering whether all that effort changed anything.

================================================================================
RECOMMENDED ENEMY LOGIC
================================================================================

ENEMY OBJECTIVE:
Not to retake the whole district. Not to wipe out every resistance fighter.
To cut the corridor and prove it cannot be used reliably.

BEST ENEMY COMPOSITION:
- disciplined local occupation infantry / motorized troops
- one stronger internal-security or special-purpose cell coordinating the cut
- competent overwatch rather than endless waves

The enemy should feel tactically coherent and unpleasantly fast at adapting.
That makes the British widening feel contested and therefore real.

================================================================================
MISSION-SPECIFIC CHARACTER BEATS
================================================================================

PAT / BRITISH MEDICAL LOGIC:
Pat should recognize immediately what is different about real military casualty
movement, but he should not romanticize it. His response should be practical.
For example: less about gratitude, more about finally having options he can trust.

ELENA / CONTROL OF THE DISTRICT:
Elena should not read as surrendering control to Britain. The mission should show
her deciding what the district sends, what it accepts, and what it still refuses.
The coalition works because it is negotiated, not absorbed.

VICKERS / CREDIBILITY:
This mission is where Vickers becomes unquestionably real to the player.
Not through a speech. Through competence under interruption.
Her comms in this mission should be exact, dry, and useful — the kind of command
voice that narrows panic rather than amplifying it.

MACE / QUIET HUMOR UNDER PRESSURE:
Optional but useful small beat. A single line after the cut is reopened can keep
continuity with the earlier covert British tone without stealing the mission.
Something dry, clipped, and instantly finished.

================================================================================
SUPPORT / SYSTEM CONSEQUENCES THIS MISSION MUST DELIVER
================================================================================

After HOLDFAST, the following should be treated as materially more believable in
campaign logic:
- British interior casualty collection is now a functioning reality, not just a
  distant receiving point
- later missions can route wounded through this infrastructure with less bespoke
  explanation
- British supplies and limited technical reach now extend further into the city
- Vickers has earned both player trust and local resistance confidence
- the enemy is now actively hunting corridor logic, meaning future route use
  carries tension even when available

This mission should therefore unlock or justify:
- more confident late-Boston CASEVAC use
- stronger interior safe-zone feel at the handoff node
- follow-on missions with slightly higher operational ambition because sustainment
  has improved
- increased enemy adaptation in any later mission touching the same corridor system

================================================================================
POST-MISSION WORLDSTATE CHANGES
================================================================================

ENVIRONMENTAL:
- more obvious casualty-routing preparation at the inner node
- visible British crates, medical packaging, batteries, comms gear, stretcher
  traffic, route markings, and better barricade discipline
- civilians react differently: not relaxed, but less purely trapped

MISSION BRIEFING LANGUAGE:
After HOLDFAST, later briefings can talk about casualty movement, resupply windows,
 and route timing as established facts rather than hopeful experiments.

PLAYER EXPECTATION:
The player now understands that Boston's liberation is not only about taking
 ground. It is about making districts survivable long enough to keep fighting.

================================================================================
TIES TO LARGER BOSTON ARC
================================================================================

HOLDFAST should strengthen, not replace, the wider British mission lanes.

It supports:
- Vickers / Royal Marines late-Boston widening
- the harbor strike logic by showing Britain can now help sustain the city, not
  only raid for it
- any later hostage/HVP or evacuation logic by proving movement infrastructure can
  exist under pressure
- Romeo's larger campaign thesis that liberation is about what kind of order can
  actually replace occupation

This is especially important for Boston's late-campaign identity:
not "the British arrived and fixed it," but "their arrival helped make holding and
surviving liberated ground actually possible."
