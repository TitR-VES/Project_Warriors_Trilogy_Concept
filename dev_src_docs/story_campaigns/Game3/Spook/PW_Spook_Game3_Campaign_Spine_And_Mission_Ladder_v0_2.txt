Campaign spine v0.2

Core thesis:
Dark Skies is the trilogy’s deep-route, strategic-unmasking campaign, but it should no longer open inside the Far East access lane. It should open in a humanitarian-maritime / South Atlantic / private-power transition block that proves the world after Godkiller is already wider, dirtier, and more globally opportunistic than the domestic conspiracy frame alone can explain. The campaign then shifts eastward into the older Yunnan / Wei / engineer line, where those wider patterns become legible.

Act I — Bad Omens
Function: restore the real opening act.
Required mission block:
- Dawn of Mercy
- Mercy Line
- Order and Progress
- Outer Port
Normalization: Outer Port is the Japan contact mission.
End-state: the campaign has widened beyond the domestic-coup frame.

Act II — Private Route
Function: transition from the Mercy/Brazil opening into the Far East access architecture.
Best-fit movements:
- Cephaly Splice
- La Avispa re-entry
- route tightening toward Laos / Yunnan
- first sense that the China-facing story is not what the public record says it is
End-state: Spook is committed to the access corridor that leads into the wrong-signature phase.

Act III — Wrong Signature
Function: turn access into hard pattern truth.
Core movements:
- Laos -> Yunnan crossing
- participatory low-input border sequence
- Tripper’s route/timing crack
- the China silence / wrong-signature interpretation
- first meaningful Wei pressure
End-state: the public story is broken. The campaign is ready for architecture and strategic truth.

Act IV — Dark Skies
Function: resolve the larger architecture without pretending the world gets clean closure.
Core movements:
- Bastion / Atlas / GenCorp overlap resolves
- Wei fully arrives
- Lin becomes active rather than theoretical
- Goldberg’s larger-war articulation lands
- the player understands the domestic false flag was only one layer inside a much bigger opportunistic storm
End-state: no public closure, no neat restoration, but a radically widened and actionable strategic picture.
