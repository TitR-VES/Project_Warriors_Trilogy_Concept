1. PROJECT / DLC IDENTITY
===============================================================================

Title:
- Project Warriors: The Collapse
- Treated as a DLC / standalone campaign thread within the Project Warriors universe.

High-level identity:
- A post-PANDORA / collapse-era St. Louis campaign centered on Christopher "Chris" Carl Haywood, Dr. Carrie Nowitzki, Rivergate University Hospital, and a hardline FFSM campaign to undermine public trust in aid, medicine, hospital systems, and federal/state relief.
- The DLC begins in grounded local survival and medical-logistics crisis, then escalates into a hidden radiological plot, false remediation fronts, public aid targeting, and a catastrophic Act IV endgame.

Core theme:
- "Systems do not fail all at once; they rot link by link."
- The campaign is not only about violent attacks. It is about how paperwork, routing, fuel, refrigeration, transfer authority, public-health language, and aid-site visibility become weapons.
- Chris and Carrie repeatedly achieve partial but meaningful survivals, not clean victories.

Primary protagonist:
- Christopher "Chris" Carl Haywood.
- Former military / practical mechanical background.
- Former Deadline Engineering employee; leaves after falling out with Philip Brand.
- Later joins Phalanx Secure Transit Services through Frank Manola.
- Gameplay role: practical logistics, movement, secure transit, route reading, evidence preservation, physical intervention, combat under duress.
- He is not a doctor, CBRN specialist, or omniscient investigator.

Primary medical anchor:
- Dr. Carrie Nowitzki.
- Chris's search for Carrie drives the early campaign.
- Carrie reads the medical truth; Chris reads the movement/logistics truth.
- Their partnership is strongest when each solves a different half of the same problem.

Primary hospital anchor:
- In-game fictionalized hospital: Rivergate University Hospital.
- Real-world geographic/functional model: Saint Louis University Hospital / SLU medical campus around South Grand / Vista.
- Function: central trauma / academic medical anchor for the mid-campaign medical spine.

Primary geographic spine:
- St. Louis, Missouri.
- South Grand / Vista functions as the hospital-side throat of a mid-campaign medical corridor.
- Later Act III locations widen to Forest Park / Wilson Ave hotel model, Lambert-adjacent / Berkeley freight-industrial areas, and north-county service-yard logistics spaces.
- Act IV ultimately foreshadows and later pays off the Gateway Arch underground visitor-center / former Museum of Westward Expansion complex.

Names and spellings:
- Free Frontier State of Missouri, not Misery.
- FFSM, not FFSN or FSA unless an earlier typo is being quoted.
- Jacobs & Reed Sanitation Services, not Jacobson Reed.
- Hazzard, with two z's.
- Former Staywell Suites Forest Park, no en dash between Suites and Forest Park.

- Glennford Miles Brunner, title Marshal.

- "Superintendent officer" is the working rank for Turner's key subordinates below lieutenant marshal.
- Skye Watkins / Sky Watkins spelling remains to be verified; "Skye" is the current cleaner form.

Radiological / weapon content:
- Keep all technical radiological and weapon material abstract.
- The story may show sealed rooms, warning signage, contaminated-site anxiety, cleanup/disposal paperwork, protective behavior, and offscreen technical involvement.
- Do not show or explain construction steps, materials specifications, operational assembly, detonation mechanics, or technical optimization.

Timeline caution:
- Use broad timing unless the current source package has been refreshed.
- Current broader Project Warriors canon supersedes older PANDORA dates where conflicts exist.
- Do not inject exact dates into The Collapse unless deliberately anchored.

Act boundaries:
- Act III is Invisible Fire.
- Act IV is They All Fall Down.
- HOT ZONE / QUARANTINE LINE / DECAY CHAIN / BLACKOUT MEDICINE are Act III escalation and foreshadowing missions.
- Gateway Arch final van confrontation does not happen until the end of Act IV.
- Act III should seed the Gateway Arch remediation clue but not resolve it.

Gameplay tone:
- The Collapse should not become a pure action shooter escalation.
- Many missions should be investigations, stealth, logistics, or infrastructure survival first, with combat arriving as a consequence or late pressure.
- Chris often wins by keeping evidence visible, preserving one support link, or buying time.

3.1 CHRISTOPHER "CHRIS" CARL HAYWOOD
Status: WORKING LOCK

Role:
- Sole playable protagonist for The Collapse.
- Former military / practical mechanic/logistics skill set.
- Former Deadline Engineering employee.
- Leaves Deadline after a falling out with Philip Brand.
- Receives a call from Frank Manola, an old military contact from the same or adjacent unit/base, who is now working for Phalanx Secure Transit Services.
- Chris joins Phalanx as an armored van driver / secure transit specialist during the company's St. Louis metro expansion.

Early-game Phalanx identity:
- Black or Multicam Black light tactical outfit.
- Plate carrier / tactical vest with Phalanx logo on back.
- Front admin panel may carry SECURITY / TRANSIT / similar role patch.
- Baseball cap with Phalanx logo.
- One-ear headset with boom mic.
- Work weapons: SIG P226 and MCX Rattler.
- Phalanx-issued radio later turned in; Chris has his own personal radio in his vehicle lockbox.

Post-Phalanx transition:
- After the armored run goes bad and Frank dies, Chris transitions from company security identity into the collapse-war identity.
- He may retain partial Phalanx carryover gear, but removes logos/patches to avoid misidentification.
- Mixed civilian clothing from truck stash + practical armor layer.
- Weapons are acquired progressively, not all handed to him immediately.
- Gradual transition into main-game loadout with LAST RESORT and SAVING GRACE is preferred.

Core dramatic identity:
- Chris is not a superhero or specialist in everything.
- He is good at movement, route logic, chain of custody, mechanical spaces, vehicle/cargo interpretation, and keeping things moving.
- He reads when paperwork and physical reality do not match.
- He is brave but must often choose stealth, evidence, and survival over direct combat.

3.2 DR. CARRIE NOWITZKI
Status: WORKING LOCK / spelling should be preserved unless later source corrects it

Role:
- Medical anchor and Chris's key relational/emotional thread.
- Chris searches for her in early Act I.
- She begins as a person Chris is trying to find, then becomes the medical truth-reader of the campaign.
- Carrie's medical authority opens doors Chris cannot open; Chris's logistics/movement instincts reveal lies Carrie cannot see from charts alone.

Functional dynamic:
- Chris sees movement lies: trucks, manifests, access states, routes, physical custody.
- Carrie sees medical lies: impossible symptoms, wrong classifications, bad transfers, clinical impossibilities.
- Their strongest scenes are not speeches but compressed decisions under pressure.

Important line / concept:
- At the end of TRANSFER AUTHORITY, Carrie sees the pediatric anomaly and says: "This doesn't happen in a house."
- In ADVERSE EVENT, after confirming the pattern: "This is radiation exposure."
- Tone should be quiet, devastating, not melodramatic.

Status: LOCKED core faction

- Main local antagonist faction in The Collapse.
- Built around Missouri territorial power, post-collapse authority struggle, anti-federal ideology, and hardline violence.

Rationale for targeting aid:
- FFSM hardliners see federal/state aid as a tool of control.

8. ACT II — CRITICAL PATH
===============================================================================

Act II identity:
- Hospital corridor and medical logistics act.
- The story is about routes, transfers, supply corridors, cold chain, and administrative authority.
- Escalates from physical blockage to paperwork manipulation to an impossible medical anomaly.

8.1 BOTTLENECK
Status: PARTIAL / needs dedicated writeup from earlier thread

Function:
- Establish the medical corridor as a vital but fragile route.
- Identity shift: Chris is no longer just looking for Carrie; he is becoming someone who keeps medical movement alive.
- Should involve route obstruction, corridor narrowing, convoy pressure, or hospital transfer choke point.

Known design principle:
- Route opening matters because it creates a usable corridor not only for Carrie/Chris but for other factions and state/federal-adjacent actors.
- This draws attention and later conflict.

8.2 LIFELINE
Status: WORKING LOCK

Function:
- Proves the corridor matters enough that different actors start fighting over it.

Mission logic:
- Chris and convoy encounter a state authority roadblock / prep point.
- They negotiate their way around it or gain passage.
- State personnel warn about a local armed group / FFSM-adjacent threat.
- Convoy is intercepted by local armed group hostile to the corridor because they see it as collaboration or as a resource route they cannot control.
- Firefight occurs.
- Convoy continues with light but meaningful damage: delays, hit tire, shot-out window, slowed movement.
- The most meaningful damage is time lost for patients and medical supply flow.

8.3 COLD CHAIN
Status: LOCKED / detailed working mission sheet exists in thread

Setting:
- Hospital cold-storage annex, likely in or near Rivergate campus.
- Visually contrasts with the previous louder corridor mission.
- Cold rooms, storage areas, utility spaces, basement/mechanical areas, loading/service exterior.

Core problem:
- The cold-chain support system is failing due to sabotage/theft attempt, not random failure.
- FFSM-adjacent team entered to steal critical refrigerated medical supplies/equipment and possibly lift cold-chain components for their own use.
- Their tampering causes or worsens the power/cooling problem.

Sabotage signs:
- Old disused office before basement access.
- Corporate false ceiling tiles fallen or smashed.
- Service/roof access hatch recently used.
- No obvious ladder.
- Fire door emergency alarm disabled.
- Fire door wedged open or latch jammed so it does not secure.
- Refrigeration panel / support equipment tampered with.
- These details show deliberate access manipulation, not random outage.

Enemy presence:
- Around six FFSM-adjacent sabotage/theft actors.
- Not all at once; staged in narrow utility spaces, basement corners, maintenance cages, loading/service areas, emergency-lit corridors.

Gameplay:
- Chris follows pipes/signs/service logic, locates power/cooling issue, tries to stabilize.
- Mission is part environmental navigation, part stealth/combat.
- Chris is isolated and must fight in constrained hospital infrastructure spaces.

Final beat:
- Final pair realizes full equipment theft plan is blown.
- They retreat with minimal stolen cold-chain/medical items toward a truck.
- Objective: STOP THE EXTRACTION.
- Chris pursues through service/loading exterior.
- If they reach the cab, Chris has a narrow last-shot opportunity with LAST RESORT through the rear cab window at the driver.
- Frame as urgent interdiction of critical stolen medical supplies/equipment, not a stylish kill-shot.
- End state: stock recovered; priority cold chain preserved; losses remain unavoidable.

8.4 TRANSFER AUTHORITY
Status: LOCKED / detailed working mission sheet exists in thread

Immediate handoff:
- Follows almost immediately after COLD CHAIN.
- Same broader hospital campus, not a separate city location.
- Chris returns with recovered stock.
- Staff realize the stolen/targeted items were too specific.
- Carrie compares stock against transfer needs and routing board.
- Key line: "They didn't guess."
- Chris follows Carrie to Transfer Authority / patient transfer-control / bed-control / movement-records wing.

Setting:
- Same Rivergate/SLU-modeled hospital campus.
- Older admin or records wing.
- Emergency transfer-control room, bed-control office, ambulance dispatch, paper backup records, pharmacy allocation desk, state/federal liaison table.
- Visually opposite COLD CHAIN: whiteboards, phones, printers, paper, failing screens, arguments, office fluorescence.

Gameplay structure:
- Chris and Carrie enter together.
- Carrie uses medical authority to access the main transfer-control room.
- They split.
- Carrie stays in the main medical/admin space, questions staff, reads medical logic.
- Chris searches back corridors, paper records, dispatch binders, physical logs.
- They communicate by radio/phone.

Core discovery:
- Transfer authority layer is compromised:
  - forged or misused authorizations;
  - bad routing inserts;
  - manipulated paper backups;
  - old credentials;
  - supplies redirected;
  - patients marked as moved when they were not;
  - cold-storage releases linked to pending transfers.

Action beat:
- Chris sees suspicious person pulling selected hard-copy backups from archive stacks.
- Person may be fake maintenance / staff / security.
- Player prompt: CONFRONT.
- Suspect shoves Chris in scripted beat and runs down a long aisle between tall, deep archive shelves.
- Chris chases, catches him before he exits, tackles/slams him into end wall or shelf end-cap.
- Suspect reveals limited information or none; files are the real proof.
- Avoid major gunfight; this is physical investigative tension.

Ending:
- Recovered packet leads Carrie to two anomalous pediatric cases.
- Girl, age 8; boy, age 13.
- Ordinary suburban home origin.
- Symptoms/classification wrong.
- Carrie: "This doesn't happen in a house."
- Directly hands to ADVERSE EVENT.

The Jacobs & Reed chain expands through a false quarantine line at a Lambert/Berkeley warehouse, where Chris tags a suspicious cargo vehicle with a MAT-77 tracker. He follows it in DECAY CHAIN, documents a larger logistics-collapse network, saves critical medications, but loses the chance to fix the generator-fuel shortage. BLACKOUT MEDICINE closes Act III with Rivergate in blackout; Chris stabilizes improvised cold-chain measures and fights limited looter incursions, keeping the hospital alive for one more night.

Act IV, They All Fall Down, begins with AID AND COMFORT: relief and generator-fuel support are finally moving, but the convoy is targeted, revealing the hardliner pattern that aid itself is now the target. MARKED SAFE follows Chris hunting the people who marked the aid infrastructure, while radiological cleanup/disposal traces and Jacobs & Reed movements begin converging toward the endgame. The final act should eventually pay off the earlier Gateway Arch remediation clue: a Jacobs & Reed van at the underground visitor-center/concrete pad, Turner's radiological device plot, and a separate standoff-weapon strike against a mobile hospital/aid site initiated by Skye Watkins. Chris may stop one catastrophe, but not all of them.
