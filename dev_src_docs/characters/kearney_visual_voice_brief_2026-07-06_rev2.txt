CHARACTER (canon, unchanged)
Eamonn Kearney, 58. Irish-born, Boston-based gunsmith / machinist / repair
specialist; hidden armorer and small-arms sustainment node for the Boston
resistance in Romeo Game 3. Buried history of quiet clandestine armorer work
around republican underground networks in an earlier conflict. Trade-first; not
operator-coded, not gangster-coded, not ideological in voice; does not
romanticize insurgency.

VISUAL ARCHETYPE
- Build: lean, wiry, work-hardened rather than gym-built; strong forearms and
  hands that read as the most capable part of him; slight forward stoop from
  decades over a bench.
- Face: weathered late-50s; deep-set eyes that do the appraising before the
  mouth says anything; grey-shot stubble or a short, unfussy beard; hair short,
  practical, self-cut-looking, more grey than not.
- Hands: the signature detail. Scarred knuckles, one flattened fingernail,
  permanent graphite-and-oil shading in the creases, a faded burn mark along one
  thumb. First-person interactions should feature the hands.
- Wardrobe: shop apron over a plain flannel or chambray work shirt; sleeves
  rolled exactly twice; canvas work trousers; scuffed leather boots resoled at
  least once; reading glasses pushed up on the forehead and forgotten there; a
  worn watch with a scratched acrylic crystal on a leather strap.
- Anti-direction: no tactical clothing, no gun-culture merchandise, no leather
  vest / biker coding, no crucifix-and-rosary Irish shorthand, no cigarette prop
  dependence.

VOICE ARCHETYPE
- Register: low, dry, economical; gravel from age and shop dust, not from
  affectation; unhurried even when the city outside is not.
- Accent: Irish-born, four decades in Boston — a softened Irish underlay with
  Boston vowels sanded in. Rule: the accent is seasoning, never performance.
  Under irritation the Irish rhythm surfaces slightly; it never becomes a bit.
- Delivery: bench-authoritative. He talks to the work as much as to the person.
  Sentences end early. Praise is rationed ("There. Now it's honest."). Contempt
  is procedural, not theatrical ("That mag's done." / "Captured doesn't mean
  usable.").
- VO anti-direction: no imitation of any recognizable actor or public figure;
  no whiskey-poet lyricism; no drill-sergeant volume. If a read sounds like a
  known performer, redirect the take.
