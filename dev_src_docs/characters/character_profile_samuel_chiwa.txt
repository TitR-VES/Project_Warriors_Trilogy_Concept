2. FAMILY LINE — FOUR GENERATIONS, ONE QUESTION [LOCKED]
---------------------------------------------------------------
Who deserves your rifle? Each generation answered differently.

GREAT-GRANDFATHER — b. ~1940s, from a line of tribal warriors.
Fought 1964–1979 for ZANLA in the Zimbabwe war of independence.
His answer: the liberation.

GRANDFATHER — b. ~1958, Rhodesia. Emigrated to Britain within
months of independence (1980), infant son in arms, and joined THE
PARACHUTE REGIMENT — the army of the empire his father fought —
to his anti-imperialist father's lasting disapproval. Fought in
the Falklands, 1982. The outsider in the Paras who had to be
twice as good. His answer: the professional army, even the old
enemy's. Why he left the liberation he was born into, within a
year of its victory, is the family wound — disillusionment,
economics, or refusal to live inside his father's war. Never
fully settled, never fully discussed.

FATHER — b. ~1977, Rhodesia; carried to Britain as a small child,
1980. Royal Marines: Operation Iraqi Freedom (2003, age 26) and
multiple coalition missions in Afghanistan. His answer: the
Western order.

SAMUEL — b. ~2000, UK. His answer: nobody's rifle — the war you
can't photograph.

4. WHY HE READS THE NIGHTFALL WORLD BETTER THAN HIS COLLEAGUES
---------------------------------------------------------------
His family history is a compressed study in state legitimacy: an
unrecognized settler state, insurgents who became a government,
exile politics, a liberation that curdled. When the American
exile government arrives in Britain, Chiwa sees it through his
great-grandfather's war. He knows governments die and the world
adjusts. He knows what insurgent movements become.

This is why he takes the NAF seriously when Whitehall files them
as militia residue: HIS GREAT-GRANDFATHER WAS MILITIA RESIDUE TO
SALISBURY. He recognizes an armed organization with a genuine
theory of legitimacy, and he neither romanticizes nor dismisses
it. That professional clarity is why the Ducky/Lupin contract
gets stood up at all.

5. WHY HE CHAMPIONS TWO NON-BRITISH CONTRACTORS
---------------------------------------------------------------
- Family precedent: his grandfather was the outsider who had to
  be twice as good. Chiwa's default respect runs toward
  capability, not tribe.
- Prior form: Ukraine taught him to work with deniable,
  mostly-ex-military irregulars. The contractor model isn't a
  compromise to him; it's a proven instrument.
- Cold logic: deniable, capable, American-literate, not British.

6. POST-SUMMIT SEAM [ACCEPTED DIRECTION]

After the foiled bombing, the political-cost consequences (rev2

about being useful to Britain right up until Britain
recalculates.
