1. IDENTITY (LOCKED, rev1)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Troy "Stone" Barry.
- Former military; friend of Frank Manola from that world; Frank's
  shooting-range buddy.
- Skilled gunsmith.
- Operator of Stone Cold Shooter's — fictional firing range and gun shop,
  1100 Antire Rd, Eureka, MO 63025, United States (real-address model per
  established series practice; business is fictional).

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. NARRATIVE FUNCTION (LOCKED, rev1)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Seeded BEFORE Frank's death via the handwriting-seed note: Frank hands
  Chris (preferred variant) a hand-written note with Troy's name, the
  shop address, and phone number, inviting Chris to call Troy and maybe
  drop by sometime for some range time. (Full mechanics:
  HASTA_LA_VISTA_Build_Spec_rev1.txt §2.1 / §4.5.)
- Payoff: some time after Frank's death, Troy contacts Chris and hands
  over Frank's personal DynaCal 870 Tac-14 TACS II Custom ("¡HASTA LA
  VISTA!") to honor Frank's wishes.
- Design payoff: because the note named Troy, the player may already
  half-know the name when a stranger calls Chris months later. Felt, not
  stated.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. RECURRING PROCUREMENT ROLE (WORKING LOCK, Stefan 2026-07-18)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Troy becomes a recurring contact through whom the player can acquire more
advanced, highly effective weaponry and ammunition — items ranging from
borderline to fully illegal in most US states — earned as rewards for
achievements or completion of optional objectives in the preceding or
upcoming mission.

Design guardrails (adjudicated in thread, 2026-07-18):
3.1  CURATED, NOT A SHOP. No scrolling inventory, no prices, no loot
     economy. Each qualifying optional objective / achievement unlocks
     ONE specific named item at Troy's bench. He built it; he hands it
     only to someone Frank vouched for. Trust as currency, not scrip.
     Frank's endorsement extending posthumously IS the characterization.
3.2  LEGALITY AS QUIET WORLDBUILDING. Troy knows exactly which authority
     would object to each item and no longer believes that authority
     exists. At most ONE dry beat of dialogue on this — never a speech.
     He is not an arms dealer; he is a craftsman whose customer base
     collapsed along with the state.
3.3  FOREGRIP UPGRADE PATH: the ¡HASTA LA VISTA! foregrip
     upgrade/removal path (base spec) is serviced at Troy's bench —
     mechanic-as-storytelling; no menu abstraction. (Direction agreed;
     fold into build spec at v2.2 pass.)
3.4  GEOGRAPHY (OPEN — see 5.2): Eureka is 30+ minutes SW of the
     campaign's STL corridor spine, and travel plausibility degrades as
     acts escalate.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. SPECIALTY AMMO — 12GA DRAGON'S BREATH (LOCKED concept, Stefan
   2026-07-18)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Among the specialty ammunition Troy can at some point provide: 12ga
dragon's breath shells (incendiary), for ¡HASTA LA VISTA! — the only
12ga platform in Chris's roster; the ammo deepens the named build's
identity rather than diluting the loadout.

Gameplay identity:
- Devastating at close quarters, especially in tight indoor spaces.
- HAZARDOUS BY DESIGN: risks igniting flammable or explosive materials
  and containers downrange from the shooter.
- This is "combat as consequence" made literal: in collapse-era St.
  Louis, the spaces Chris fights through are often the spaces he is
  trying to protect. The most devastating CQB option carries the risk of
  destroying the supplies, fuel, or structures the mission exists to
  save. Tactical decision, not power fantasy.

Real-world register note (non-instructional): dragon's breath is the
type specimen of Troy's procurement lane — federally available but
banned in multiple US states. Story-facing only; no technical detail.

Naming conventions (LOCKED, Stefan 2026-07-18):
- Diegetic / spoken: Troy refers to them as "incendiary shells" —
  craftsman's plain register, no theatrical branding.
- Weapon inspect UI overlay ammo-type identifier: 12GA DB
- "Dragon's breath" remains the design-document term; whether it ever
  appears diegetically (e.g., stenciled box label) is left open.

Implementation dependency (FLAG): the risk mechanic requires ignitable
environmental objects with fire-propagation logic in the relevant
levels. This is a level-design/system commitment, not just an ammo
entry. Scope which missions support it before locking availability
timing.
