16A. ANIMAL CALM-STATE INTERACTION

STATUS: Locked as flavor / world-authenticity feature. Use with restraint.

OVERVIEW

Select non-hostile animals may be briefly interacted with in first person when
they are calm, the surrounding situation is safe, and the interaction is
fictionally appropriate. The mechanic exists to add lived-in environmental
texture, warmth, and authenticity to the world. It does not exist to provide
mechanical advantage, progression, or collectible-style completion pressure.

CORE RULE

This feature only works through moderation. It should be rare, quiet, optional,
and context-sensitive. The player should feel as though they encountered a small
human moment in the world, not as though they triggered a system asking to be used.

NON-NEGOTIABLE DESIGN RULES

- No rewards, buffs, score, achievements, or progression hooks.
- No pressure to use the interaction for completion.
- No interaction prompt when the fiction makes it absurd or tactically reckless.
- No broad assumption that every visible animal is interactable.
- Animal behavior and agency must remain believable.

VALID CONDITIONS

The interaction may appear only when all of the following are true:
- The animal is non-hostile and in a normal behavioral state
- The animal is not panicked, fleeing, injured, restrained in distress, or
  otherwise behaving as though human contact is unwelcome
- The player is not in active combat, immediate pursuit, urgent stealth tension,
  or another situation where stopping to touch an animal would break tone
- The local story context supports the animal's presence and the player's
  ability to approach it without compromising the scene

INVALID CONDITIONS

Do not present the interaction when:
- Gunfire, alarms, pursuit, or immediate danger are active
- The player is in a high-urgency dramatic beat
- The animal is stressed, working, guarding in a way that makes contact unsafe,
  or otherwise behaviorally unavailable
- The animal's presence is meant to communicate neglect, fear, contamination,
  or other negative environmental information that petting would trivialize

ANIMAL-SPECIFIC GROUNDING

Dogs:
- Most readable and reliable version of the mechanic
- Preferred actions: offered hand, brief neck rub, ear scratch, shoulder pat

Cats:
- Must remain more selective and less predictable
- May accept a brief chin or head stroke, ignore the player, or walk away
- Refusal is valid behavior and should remain in the system

Horses / Donkeys:
- Do not frame like domestic-pet cuddling
- Preferred actions: muzzle touch, forehead stroke, short neck pat
- Read as familiarity and calm handling, not cuteness-first staging

CHARACTER DIFFERENTIATION

Minor character-specific animation flavor is encouraged if kept subtle:
- Romeo: direct, warm, unceremonious
- Ducky: careful, steady, quietly familiar
- Spook: restrained, slightly awkward, economical
- Other protagonists / DLC leads may receive equally small handling differences
  where useful

Do not turn these differences into overt personality showcase moments. The
interaction should still read first as a natural environmental beat.

DIALOGUE / AUDIO RULE

Dialogue should be absent by default. At most, use occasional soft
under-the-breath lines when the moment genuinely supports it. The primary tools
should be hand animation, animal response, cloth/gear movement, breath, and
small diegetic sound.

PLACEMENT PHILOSOPHY

Use in curated, memorable spaces rather than as a frequent open-world filler:
- Quiet back rooms
- Resistance hideouts
- Farm or service spaces
- Brief downtime windows between high-pressure beats

A single remembered animal in the right space is more valuable than repeated
interactions across many areas.

EXAMPLE REGISTER

- A cat in the Calloway pharmacy back room during downtime
- A calm resistance dog present at a Boston hideout or meeting point
- A horse or donkey in a rural logistics / farm-adjacent location where its
  presence is natural to the setting

PROMPT / PRESENTATION RULE

The interaction prompt should be plain and quiet. Avoid cute phrasing and avoid
framing the mechanic as a joke. The action should read as a small human choice,
not a novelty button.

SUCCESS CONDITION

The player should come away feeling:
- this place is inhabited
- these animals belong to the world
- these people still have ordinary human reflexes in wartime

Not:
- I found the petting feature again
