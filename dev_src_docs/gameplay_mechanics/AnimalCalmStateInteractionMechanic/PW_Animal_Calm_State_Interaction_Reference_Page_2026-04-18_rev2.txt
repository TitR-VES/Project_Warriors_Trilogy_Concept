PURPOSE
This page refreshes the locked direction for optional animal interaction moments
with the rev4 implementation clarifications.

LOCK SUMMARY
Select non-hostile animals may be briefly interacted with in first person when
calm, safe, and fictionally appropriate. These interactions are optional,
non-reward-bearing, context-sensitive, and sparse. Animal agency and grounded
behavior must be preserved.

IMPLEMENTATION RULES
- Only calm, non-hostile animals in believable states and locations
- No rewards, buffs, achievements, or progression hooks
- No prompt during combat, pursuit, urgent stealth, or tonal high-pressure moments
- Not every animal should be interactable
- Dialogue minimal or absent
- The animal may accept, ignore, or decline the interaction
- Do not present the interaction for stressed, fleeing, working, or tactically
  compromising animals

GROUNDING BY ANIMAL TYPE
Dogs:
- brief hand-offer, neck rub, ear scratch, shoulder pat
- best fit: quiet resistance dog in a Boston hideout

Cats:
- brief chin or head touch when accepted
- refusal / walking away remains valid behavior
- best fit: Calloway pharmacy back-room cat during downtime

Horses / Donkeys:
- muzzle touch, forehead stroke, short neck pat
- should read as grounded handling, not pet-video staging

CHARACTER FLAVOR
- Romeo: direct warmth
- Ducky: careful steadiness
- Spook: restrained economy

TONE WARNING
This feature gains value from rarity. One well-placed interaction can be
memorable. If it starts reading like a "pet the animal" checklist, the tone is
wrong.
