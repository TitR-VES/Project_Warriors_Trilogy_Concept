PURPOSE
This page records the locked direction for optional animal interaction moments in
Project Warriors. The feature is intentionally small. Its purpose is not mechanical
advantage but human texture, environmental authenticity, and tonal contrast.

LOCK SUMMARY
Select non-hostile animals may be briefly interacted with in first person when calm,
safe, and fictionally appropriate. These interactions are optional, non-reward-
bearing, context-sensitive, and used sparingly. Animal agency and behavioral
plausibility must be preserved.

DESIGN INTENT
The mechanic exists to create occasional quiet human beats inside a war story
without softening the project into sentimentality. It should make spaces feel
inhabited and worth protecting. It should not become a collectible behavior, a
completion target, or a novelty that appears so often it starts announcing itself.

RULES
- Only calm, non-hostile animals in believable states and locations
- No rewards, buffs, achievements, or progression hooks
- No prompt during combat, pursuit, urgent stealth, or tonal high-pressure moments
- Not every animal should be interactable
- Dialogue minimal or absent
- The animal may accept, ignore, or decline the interaction

GROUNDING BY ANIMAL TYPE
Dogs:
- brief hand-offer, neck rub, ear scratch, shoulder pat

Cats:
- brief chin or head touch when accepted
- refusal / walking away remains valid behavior

Horses / Donkeys:
- muzzle touch, forehead stroke, short neck pat
- should read as grounded handling, not pet-video staging

CHARACTER FLAVOR
Small protagonist-specific handling differences are encouraged but should remain
subtle:
- Romeo: direct warmth
- Ducky: careful steadiness
- Spook: restrained economy

PLACEMENT EXAMPLES
- Calloway pharmacy back-room cat during downtime
- Calm resistance dog in Boston hideout / meetup space
- Rural horse or donkey in a farm / logistics environment where it naturally belongs

IMPLEMENTATION WARNING
This feature gains value from rarity. One well-placed interaction can be memorable.
Frequent reuse, broad map coverage, or exaggerated cute framing will damage tone.
