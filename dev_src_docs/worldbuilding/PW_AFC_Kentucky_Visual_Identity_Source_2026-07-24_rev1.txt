SCOPE
This page governs the Kentucky-born visual language of the American Freedom Corps during Romeo Game 2.
It supplements, and does not replace, the later wider-AFC / Boston emblem hierarchy:
- Republic Shield — national/coordinating AFC emblem;
- Breach Star — rough field/propaganda mark;
- Union Mark — inherited Boston underground emblem.

DESIGN THESIS
The Kentucky movement grows from occupied local life: pharmacy back rooms, feed stores, school gyms, church basements, barns, road signs, survey paint, stitched fabric, marker labels, and scavenged stencil plates.
It is civilian-born, practical before ideological, handmade before standardized, and emotionally American without becoming generic flag merchandise or polished insurgent branding.

SIGNATURE PALETTE
Goldenrod field:
- primary/base identity color;
- muted, dusty, sun-faded yellow rather than bright lemon;
- evokes Kentucky goldenrod, field grass, old roadside paint, feed-sack ink, shelter, support, wayfinding, persistence, and home soil.

Cardinal red:
- sharp accent/action color;
- barn/cardinal/flare/sign-enamel red rather than glossy fire-engine red;
- evokes defiance, warning, sacrifice, retaliation, rallying, and memory.

Supporting neutrals:
weathered off-white, charcoal, rust brown, oxidized steel gray, and ordinary faded denim in clothing only.

CORE COLOR RULE
YELLOW OWNS SPACE. RED INTERRUPTS IT.

Working meanings:
- yellow cloth/mark = support, shelter, route, supply, friendly contact;
- red interruption = danger, attack, compromise, reprisal, or active cell;
- red over yellow = watched, burned, or no longer safe;
- red and yellow together = active AFC presence, jointly held ground, rally point, or organized operation.

MATERIAL RULE
Use house paint, spray paint, fabric dye, thread, marker, chalk, old sign enamel, cardboard, plywood, reused labels, and field-cut stencils.
Avoid clean vector perfection, glossy esports/PMC identity, neon, skull-heavy cliché, giant eagles, pristine uniforms, or corporate-patriot branding.

SYMBOL FAMILY

1. CARDINAL SLASH
Fast cardinal-red diagonal brushstroke with a slight hooked wing gesture.
Not a literal bird.
Meaning: presence, refusal, “we are still here.”
Uses: walls, underpasses, culverts, fuel pumps, barn doors, crates, magazines, propaganda overstrike.

2. GOLDENROD HOME
Minimal yellow goldenrod stem/spray.
Meaning: shelter, medicine, food, mutual aid, memory, civilian support.
Uses: pharmacies, churches, pantries, aid bags, cache boxes, cloth tabs, memorial sites.

3. AFC BLOCK MARK
Rough goldenrod AFC letters crossed or backed by a cardinal-red slash.
Meaning: the network has a shared name and visible movement identity.
Uses: plywood, rally walls, truck doors, banners, ammo crates, reclaimed civic spaces.
Variation between locally copied versions is intentional.

4. REDBUD ARROW
Narrow yellow route arrow with red notch/dot/slash modifiers.
Meaning: courier/logistics code.
Uses: fence posts, culverts, mailboxes, grain structures, gate hinges, tractors, road-sign backs.
Red modifiers can mark danger, checkpoint, watched route, or burned route.

5. BROKEN COUNTY RING
Rough yellow ring broken by a gap and interrupted by red.
Meaning: contested, not conquered; occupation has failed to close control.
Uses: larger walls, silos, barricades, vehicle hoods, rally points.

6. BOONE TAB
Subdued workshop-made combat tab/shield with dull yellow field/stripe and red diagonal wing motif; tiny AFC letters optional.
Meaning: disciplined Boone-linked / National Guard-adjacent organized element.
Uses: Priya’s unit, selected armbands, chest rigs, helmets, vehicles, command/security elements.

7. PHARMACY CROSS-OUT
Faded neutral medical cross crossed by goldenrod stem and small red wing mark.
Meaning: medicine, triage, and civic care as resistance.
Uses: Dale-side clinics, aid bags, volunteer jackets, manifests, supply boxes.

8. NAMES WALL MARK
Goldenrod sprig plus short red wing stroke beside names, dates, initials, or missing-person notices.
Meaning: remembered, not erased, belonged to this place.
Use without skulls or macho memorial language.

CAMPAIGN EVOLUTION
Phase 1 — deniable local network:
yellow chalk, thread, label reuse, fence/gate marks; no clean central logo.

Phase 2 — named AFC network:
rough AFC initials and shared red/yellow grammar appear across counties; imperfect copies remain authentic.

Phase 3 — open identity under pressure:
reclaimed vehicles, armbands, tabs, banners, memorial walls, and overpainted occupation signage become more consistent.

LATER-AFC MIGRATION
Kentucky’s red/yellow grammar is the lived regional origin layer, not the permanent totality of the national mark system.
As the AFC widens:
- the Republic Shield becomes the national/coordinating emblem;
- the Breach Star inherits the rough field-tag / direct-action function;
- Kentucky goldenrod/cardinal colors remain a respected regional lineage and can persist in local tabs, memorials, unit accents, route marks, and heritage variants.
Do not flatten Kentucky marks, Republic Shield, Breach Star, and Boston Union Mark into interchangeable logos.

CHARACTER / NETWORK FIT
- Dale Pruett: goldenrod, aid, ledger, pharmacy, civic reliability.
- Cody Beaumont: rougher red, fast arrows, angry field marks, memorial spillover.
- Gene Marlow: discreet route codes hidden in agricultural clutter.
- Priya Okonkwo: cleanest Boone-tab / proto-military standardization.
- Romeo: increasing visibility of a movement that begins locally and becomes larger than one leader.

PROPAGANDA / PUBLIC-LANGUAGE RULE
Messaging should sound local, useful, and earned rather than like a professional campaign agency.
Strong approved directions include:
STILL HERE.
NOT CONQUERED.
THEY LEFT. WE STAYED.
YOU CAN HOLD THE ROAD, NOT THE HOME.
IF YOU CAN CARRY, CARRY.
MEDICINE / WATER / SHELTER.
NO ONE WALKS ALONE.
THEY WERE OURS.

Tone reference:
AFC should look like the American countryside decided it would rather bleed than submit.

OPEN PRODUCTION FLAGS
Exact final vector construction, embroidery specifications, color values, stencil plates, typeface choices, and asset-library implementation remain production work.
