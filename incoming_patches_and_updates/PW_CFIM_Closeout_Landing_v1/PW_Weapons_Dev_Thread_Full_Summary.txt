PART 1 — DESIGN PARAMETERS (established at thread open)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WEAPON SYSTEM APPROACH: Mostly realistic — real-world guns accurate to 2028
military/paramilitary use, with a small number of near-future prototype weapons.

RARITY SYSTEM: Both — some weapons hidden as collectibles requiring exploration
or side objectives; some dropped by defeating specific named/elite enemies.

FACTION COVERAGE: All factions developed simultaneously.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PART 2 — WEAPON ROSTER BY FACTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

NOTE: Full faction tables with tiers (Standard / Uncommon / Rare) are
contained in the thread itself. Below is a summary of key decisions and
faction-specific design principles.

US MILITARY / NATO (Player-side):
  - Rangers (Romeo): M4A1 DD MK18, SIG MCX Spear (NGSW late game),
    M249 SAW, M320 GLM, AT4, MAAWS Carl Gustaf M4
  - AEGIS (Ducky): HK416 A5 as standard, FN SCAR-H, FN P90, B&T APC9K
  - Ground Branch (Spook): Suppressed compact platforms, HK MP5SD,
    SIG MPX-K
  - Standard sidearms: Glock 19 Gen5, SIG P320-M17, SIG P226 MK25
  - Key design note: AEGIS carries European platforms deliberately —
    multinational unit with European procurement character

RUSSIAN FORCES (VDV / GRU / FSB Vympel):
  - Line infantry: AK-74M, AKM, SVD Dragunov, PKM/PKP Pecheneg, RPG-7
  - VDV elite: AK-12, AK-15, ORSIS T-5000
  - FSB Spetsnaz: AS Val, VSS Vintorez, SR-3M Vikhr, 9A-91
  - Vympel naval: OTs-14 Groza, PSS-2 silent pistol, SPP-1M, APS
  - KEY DESIGN NOTE: When player starts seeing AS Vals and VSS instead
    of AKMs — they know they are fighting better-trained enemies
  - 7.62x39mm thread: AKM/AK-15 drops feed CMMG Mk47 ammo pool
    throughout Romeo's late campaign — deliberate cross-faction mechanic

NORTH KOREAN FORCES (KPA):
  - Standard: Type 58/Type 68 (AKM clones — FEEDS CMMG Mk47), Type 88
  - Elite SOF: Type 98, indigenous bullpup (unsettling, unlike anything else)
  - Support: Type 73 LMG, F-7 RPG clone, Bulsae-3 ATGM
  - KEY DESIGN NOTE: DPRK infantry look deliberately wrong — older,
    indigenous designs that signal "different from Russian kit"

LOS SIETES CARTEL:
  - Standard: AKM/WASR-10, AR-15 variants (stolen LE), Glock 17/19,
    Mossberg 500, Saiga-12
  - Elite sicarios: FN SCAR-L, HK MP5, FN Five-seveN, Vector KRISS V
  - KEY DESIGN NOTE: Elite Cartel kit (K Lounge, Bisutri's team) should
    feel professional, not gangster — purchased military-grade hardware

ATLAS PMC / WAR WOLVES:
  - Standard: HK416 A5 suppressed, Daniel Defense MK18 suppressed
  - Elite: MCX Rattler (300BLK), AXSR, CheyTac M200
  - KEY DESIGN NOTE: Atlas carries same platforms as AEGIS and Ground
    Branch — deliberate uncomfortable parallel about shared procurement.
    War Wolves are the most individually dangerous human enemies in Game 1.

FSB VYMPEL (Shipboard / Hull Breach):
  - 9A-91 suppressed, AS Val, SR-3M Vikhr, OTs-14 Groza
  - PSS-2 silent pistol, SPP-1M underwater pistol, APS underwater rifle
  - KEY DESIGN NOTE: All Vympel kit optimized for confined steel spaces —
    subsonic, suppressed, compact. They are mechanically different enemies.

AMMO ECONOMY DESIGN THREADS:
  7.62x39mm — richest scavenge caliber. DPRK Type 58, Russian AKM/AK-15,
    Los Sietes AKM all feed this pool. CMMG Mk47 unlock in Down Rodeo is
    a gameplay infrastructure decision paying off through San Diego finale.
  9x39mm — creates its own niche economy when Vympel appear. AS Val/VSS/
    SR-3M/9A-91 drops sustain the caliber on scavenge.
  300 BLK — deliberately scarce. No enemy uses it. GRAY GHOST is a luxury
    weapon; Atlas MCX Rattler drops provide limited scavenge.
  5.56 NATO — baseline. Most universally available across all campaigns.

PART 5 — INSPECT WEAPON MECHANIC (finalized)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

BEHAVIOR BY CLASS:
  Rifles/carbines/DMRs/LMGs: Arms-length, rotated slowly right, 3-4 seconds,
    support hand repositions naturally.
  Pistols/compact SMGs: Hand-profile, strong-side. Hammer-check motion for
    weapons with external hammers (CZ P-09, HK45CT, P-99) — varies per weapon.
  Shotguns: Rack-and-check, forend half-inch back and released.

SIGNATURE WEAPON ANIMATIONS: Slightly longer/more detailed.
  Build name in overlay bottom frame for:
  GRAY GHOST / NOTHING / FREYA / TRG M10 / ARGINT / DON'T TREAD
  WREN: NOT in overlay — bolt-open empty-chamber animation ONLY.

OVERLAY (field modification mode):
  Semi-transparent, non-intrusive. Labels near attachment points.
  Empty rails with nothing available: no label shown.
  Field-removable (interactable):
    Suppressors / QD-mount optics / weapon lights / MAWL-DA / MAWL-C1+ /
    foregrips / ammunition type (where multiple types loaded)
  Non-removable (no prompt):
    Fixed barrels / integrated handguards / permanently mounted hardware

AMMO SWITCHING:
  Most weapons: single caliber shown.
  GRAY GHOST: 300BLK SUBSONIC / 300BLK SUPERSONIC — switching requires
    magazine change animation ~4 seconds, NOT skippable. Tactical risk.

PHYSICAL REMOVAL ANIMATION: ~3 seconds. Not skippable.
  Removing a suppressor mid-contact is a deliberate tactical cost.

GRAY GHOST DUST COVER: Faint worn factory marking "300 BLK" visible in
  inspect. Worn from years of handling, not deliberate removal.

MAWL-DA INSPECT (once unlocked on GRAY GHOST): Thumb clicks activation
  dial one position forward and back. IR beam briefly pulses visible.

CLEANING ANIMATIONS (gunsmith between missions):
  Romeo:  Fast, functional, muscle memory. No ceremony.
  Ducky:  Methodical — checks each component before reassembly.
  Spook:  Slow, almost meditative. The cleaning is how he thinks.
  Others: Not yet specified — flag for development.

GUNSMITH SYSTEM (between missions only):
  Laid flat on workbench. All attachment points visible including non-field-
  removable. Categories: Barrel / Muzzle / Optics / Underbarrel /
  Laser/IR / Stock / Grip / Internal (trigger, BCG, buffer — affects
  performance, not visible on model).
  Signature weapons CAN be modified but core identity cannot be changed
  (GRAY GHOST cannot become another color; FREYA color discipline locked).

KEY NOTES FROM MISSION TABLES:

DOWN RODEO:
  DPRK + Los Sietes. Romeo starts M4A1 + P320-M17.
  7.62x39mm abundant from DPRK Type 58 + Los Sietes AKM.
  Rooftop cache above K Lounge: Saiga-12 + drum mags + cash (uncollectable).
  DPRK elite squad carries Type 98 (5.56 — NATO ammo bonus) +
    one indigenous bullpup (rare, one mag only, no resupply).

CORONADO:
  DPRK amphibious + Russian Naval Infantry + Iranian IRGC.
  KH-2002 Khara (Iranian 5.56 bullpup) — no dialogue explains why IRGC
    carries NATO caliber. Implies deliberate re-equipping. Just there.
  PKP Pecheneg can be unbolted and carried — movement penalty.
  ORSIS T-5000 on control tower — only G1 appearance.
  Locked cage in secondary hangar: M1014 + nameplate "GUNNERY SGT. R. HARLOW"
    — not a named character; nameplate just a nameplate.

WIRECUTTER:
  FREYA available and primary (first Ducky mission).
  Dive prep station: APS + SPP-1M both present as finds.
  Captain's cabin: Benelli M4 in wall mount + unburned burn bag papers
    (readable intel collectible — routing history of Sea Lion 7).
  Vympel Dive Team Commander: OTs-14 Groza + PSS-2 — emerges partially
    suited from dive station. If player too slow, he may reach the water.
    APS underwater rifle required to follow. Time pressure design.

HULL BREACH:
  Stealth-favored: alarm raised = Spetsnaz spawn = SR-3M available.
    Stealth run = Spetsnaz never appear = SR-3M not obtainable.
  SIGINT room hidden: VSS Vintorez in padded case under console.

VIENNA CHASE:
  Stribog SP10 A3 (10mm AUTO — CONFIRMED CALIBER) + HK45CT only.
  Mission-specific — Stribog cannot be kept. 10mm unusual caliber
    reinforces why it cannot be sustained in the field.
  Chase scenario — scavenging possible but disrupts momentum.

BURNING DAYLIGHT:
  GRAY GHOST + HK45CT. MAWL-DA NOT yet available (pre-unlock mission).
  Supersonic 300BLK mag from Tripper = "break glass" option if mission
    goes loud — acoustic change signals mission character shift.
  Walther P-99 in locked drawer, coat check area — belongs to gala guest.
  PSS-2 second opportunity (if missed in Wirecutter) — tunnel FSB officer.
  MAWL-DA unlock: Pelican case, equipment room, GenCorp sticker.

PANDORA'S BOX:
  Cartel general + elite sicarios + KYSC (5 members, conditioned behavior).
  KYSC fight differently — irregular movement, strange vocalizations, don't
    respond to suppressing fire normally. One already dead on arrival.
  Vector KRISS V (.45 ACP) shares ammo with HK45CT — only exotic pickup
    with sustained utility in this mission.
  Desert Eagle (.50 AE) — 7 rounds total, non-replenishable, Max Payne easter egg.
  Hidden finds: MP5SD in guitar case mount behind bar (pre-cartel occupant);
    Mossberg 590M behind junction box in maintenance corridor (labeled "PETE").

ROTTERDAM FINALE:
  FREYA suppressor-off for first time. Tonal shift deliberate.
  OTs-14 Groza second appearance (Spetsnaz demolitions operator).
  KAPITAN (named kill-feed only) drops SR-3M Vikhr.
  RPG operator: fires from drain hole. Adjacent NATO forces respond
    ~4 seconds after — heard off-screen, not shown. Player who shoots
    him before he fires: RPG goes into harbor, tip caused by shockwave.
    Identical outcome. No dialogue acknowledges player was fast enough.
  Dutch Marine cache in secondary building: Walther P-99 + field notebook
    (readable intel, reveals additional patrol routes on minimap).

LOW GROUND:
  Most resource-constrained mission in Game 2. Romeo's M4A1 running low
    from G1 carry. No 5.56 scavenge available (no enemy uses it).
    Ammo scarcity IS the narrative before any dialogue.
  Glushkin introduction: player observes him interviewing Gene Marlow
    from concealment. Cannot engage. Watching Glushkin operate calmly
    while being unable to do anything = Christoph Waltz archetype landing.
  SR-1 Vektor dropped by close protection IF Romeo breaks cover
