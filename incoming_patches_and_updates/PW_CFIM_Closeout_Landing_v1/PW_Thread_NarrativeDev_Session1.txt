SECTION 1: THREE-CAMPAIGN STORY OVERVIEW (GAME 1)

THE OVERARCHING CONSPIRACY
--------------------------
All three campaigns converge on a deep-state plot codenamed RIPTIDE RED.
Architects: Edward Gregory Goldberg (CIA codename: KINGMAKER) and Gillian
Stockbridge (codename: CHAPERONE), with complicity of President Barry James Crodyn.

Their instrument: TETHERBREAK — a covert CIA program weaponizing America's own
autonomous military drone systems. Operational plan: PANDORA — deploying TETHERBREAK
as a false-flag attack on US soil to destabilize the government and install Crodyn
in an unconstitutional third term.

Goldberg also operates the Shadow Underground (Forever Pact / Eclipse Society /
KINGMAKER) — a global human trafficking, blackmail, and dark-money empire centered
on the underground complex beneath Montoya Farm, New Mexico.

Goldberg sold intelligence about Riptide Red to multiple hostile foreign governments,
triggering a coordinated Russian, North Korean, and Iranian amphibious invasion of
the US West Coast, a Russian push into Western Europe, and a Chinese move on Taiwan.
Effectively: World War Three used as cover for the conspirators' escape.

PANDORA canonical attack date: September 3, 2028.
US Presidential Election: November 3, 2028.


CAMPAIGN 1 — CRITTER SQUAD
---------------------------
Playable character: LT Ramón "Romeo" Álvarez, Fire Team Delta, 75th Ranger Regiment
Squad: Dingo, Howler, Beaver

Arc: Begins in eastern Poland (NATO eastern flank reinforcement). PANDORA activates,
autonomous systems turn against US forces. Squad fights west through Czech Republic
and Austria to Ramstein Air Base, Germany. Assigned to protect classified geospatial
intelligence sites across Europe alongside AEGIS Squad:
- Faroe Islands
- French Alps
- Greek island of Lemnos (Aegean)
- Hinderloopen, Netherlands

With all flights grounded, squads travel overland through Brittany to French naval
base at Île Longue. Defend French nuclear submarines Le Terrible and Le Vigilant
from enemy sabotage. AEGIS diverted to Rotterdam. Romeo's squad boards nuclear
submarine USS District of Columbia, surfaces off Ventura, California.

US campaign:
- Links up with Army/Marine forces
- Fights North Korean forces out of North Hollywood (mission: Down Rodeo)
- Pushes through South Beverly Hills toward coast
- Secures Port of Los Angeles
- Defends Long Beach Airport at Battle of Signal Hill
- Convoy south toward San Diego
- Climax: Battle of Coronado Island — defending North Island Naval Air Station
  against massive joint amphibious assault (shared climax across all three campaigns)

Key character: André Nathaniel Bridges, USMC SSgt, New Orleans. Meets Romeo at Port
of LA. Signal Hill disagreement (Rangers push vs Marines consolidate) — outcome TBD.
[FLAG: Signal Hill resolution and Coronado climax detail]

   Mission: Sins of Our Fathers. Fight through War Wolves — three former Tier 1

   CLIFFHANGER ENDING. Goldberg monologue gives mission its name.

CONVERGENCE POINT: All three campaigns meet at Battle of Coronado Island.
Romeo fighting amphibious assault. Ducky's campaign ends at Rotterdam as invasion

REGENT AS WHISTLEBLOWER [LOCKED]
---------------------------------
Mullen (Regent) confirmed as major NPC in Game 2 — a Riptide Red whistleblower.

THREE SUBTLE GAME 1 SEEDS [LOCKED]:

(1) CUT RADIO TRANSMISSION — Frankfurt warehouse mission (Above My Pay Grade):
As team clears Atlas personnel from Warehouse 46, Spook's earpiece briefly catches
a fragmented transmission on an unassigned frequency. Regent's voice, mid-sentence:
"...copies of everything, they don't know I—" then cuts to static. Easy to miss
entirely during firefight. Game never acknowledges it happened.
SEQUEL REVEAL: Video recording found in East McFarlane cache reveals this was a
live transmission Regent was making during the exact moment of the warehouse raid.
He was trying to reach Spook directly and was cut off — or silenced.

(2) MANUALLY REDACTED FILE — Montoya Farm server room:
Among archived documents Tripper extracts, one file stands out: a PANDORA-adjacent
intelligence report whose author's name has been manually redacted — crudely blacked
out, not algorithmically purged like the others. As if done in a hurry by someone
who knew what they were doing. File creation timestamp: 3 days before Regent was
"urgently reassigned." Tripper notes the anomaly but has nothing to go on.

(3) MACMILLAN'S SLIP — Helipad stand-down (Sins of Our Fathers):
When Macmillan radios Spook to order stand-down, he says: "Stand down, Spook.
This is bigger than Mullen told you." He clearly meant to say "bigger than you know."
Catching himself mid-sentence implies he knows exactly what Regent told the team,
and when — suggesting active FSB monitoring of Regent, not just reassignment.

EAST McLARLANE HIDDEN CACHE [LOCKED]
-------------------------------------
Off the main objective path in the final mission — looks like scenery, easy to miss.
Contents: weatherproof cache, single encrypted USB drive, handwritten note.
Note contains: a specific symbol + a set of coordinates. No signature.
Coordinates don't appear on any map accessible in Game 1.
The symbol is also seeded in Montoya Farm tunnels, Game 1 (graffiti in cell or
hidden crawlspace or film set — appears purely atmospheric on first playthrough).

CROSS-GAME QUEST MECHANIC [LOCKED]
------------------------------------
In Game 2: A location corresponds to the coordinates from the cache note.
Access requires knowing the symbol from the note (cannot be obtained elsewhere
in Game 1 without finding the cache). Symbol is embedded in Game 2 environment
looking like scenery — player who knows it recognizes it and interacts with it.
Players who don't have the note walk past forever.
Inside: Unredacted Riptide Red analyst report authored by Regent.
This triggers the "Find Regent" storyline in Game 2.

SEQUEL CACHE CONTENTS — UNREDACTED REPORT [LOCKED]

impossible — no uncorrupted institution left to receive it.
Regent documented everything and hid it rather than publishing — the player must
wrestle with whether that was cowardice or wisdom.

CAMPAIGN THEME [LOCKED]
------------------------
Ducky's campaign is about collective will — fragmented, imperfect, argumentative
people and nations choosing to be stronger together than the forces trying to pull
them apart.

Political soul: Uniting a politically disjointed European leadership against:
- Russian covert sabotage campaign targeting European civil and military infrastructure
- Compromised personnel within Europe's own political and military command structure

AEGIS (the system) as the "Seeing Eye of Europe":
Years before the events of Game 1, European NATO leaders recognized the threat of
continuous Russian subversion and escalating infrastructure attacks. Built AEGIS —
a classified, independent, distributed terrestrial and orbital infrastructure system
with enough fault tolerance, fail-safety, and redundancy to remain online even when
US infrastructure is taken down. Secures independent geospatial capability for
European NATO command.
AEGIS has proven highly effective at unmasking Russian covert sabotage operations
and clandestine media campaign funding of manufactured dissent within European civil
and political society.
Russian response: Step up subversive activity, escalate to attacks on civil and
military infrastructure, targeting strategic components of AEGIS itself.
AEGIS Squad's task: Protect AEGIS. Protect the Seeing Eye of Europe.

THEMATIC MIRROR [LOCKED]:
AEGIS Squad's diversity (Ducky=American-Irish expat, Frost=Finnish, Lupin=Romanian,
Switchblade=Australian) mirrors the EU unity theme. They are all different, don't
always get along, but willing to set differences aside when it matters most — just
like EU countries united to build AEGIS, and DAOG came together from different
backgrounds to protect a common asset that in turn protects each of them individually.

ANOMALY THREADS IN DUCKY'S CAMPAIGN [LOCKED STRUCTURAL ASSET]
---------------------------------------------------------------
Three anomaly threads to plant across the campaign — never explained in Game 1,
retroactively meaningful in Game 2:

(1) Wirecutter: Vympel diving equipment was already staged and partially prepped
when Nereid found them — not being unpacked, already staged. Means someone told
them the window. Official attribution: good Russian operational planning. Ducky
mentions to Halliday afterward. Neither pushes it. Never referenced again in Game 1.

(2) Hinderloopen: Intercepted operative carries communications equipment / encryption
signature consistent with private contractor rather than state intelligence apparatus.
Halliday flags it in her report. Walker notes it. No further action.

(3) Torrejón Air Base: How did Russian operators get deep enough inside a NATO air
base to attempt satellite deorbit command sequence? Someone inside was compromised.
Nobody officially held accountable for the infiltration. Ducky notices. Files it away.

All three point toward same pattern: the enemy has inside information. Collectively
they form a thread Halliday has partially documented. In Game 2, this becomes
evidence of deeper penetration of DAOG or NATO command structure.

---------------------
Official designation: Cold Frontier 07 (winter military exercise).
Jussi Mustonen's death was officially reported as drowning after falling through
ice while crossing the frozen surface of Lake Inari.
Cover story rationale: A Russian Spetsnaz team operating inside Finnish sovereign

escalation of conflict with Russia at the time.
The cover story is mundane enough to generate no questions — the kind of death
that happens in Arctic conditions.

What this means for Frost:
Jussi doesn't just die — he dies officially as a clumsy accident. No record of
