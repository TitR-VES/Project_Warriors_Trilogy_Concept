================================================================================
01. ROLE IN THE BOSTON ARC — WHAT THIS PACKAGE IS
================================================================================

Vickers and the Royal Marines package are not a full-scale liberation force and not a second campaign taking over Romeo's story. They are the late-arriving widening layer of an already established British presence.

The simplest defining line is:

SAS and SBS prove Britain can still reach Boston.
Vickers proves Britain can help Boston hold.

That distinction is load-bearing.

SAS and SBS belong to the covert-competence phase:
- insertion through the blockade
- sabotage
- hostage/HVP extraction
- harbor infiltration
- deniable disruption

Vickers and the Royal Marines belong to the support-and-retention phase:
- create a usable allied foothold
- widen maritime access on a limited scale
- stabilize casualties and movement
- support resistance operations with disciplined military competence
- make later liberation feel materially sustainable rather than episodic

This package should change the campaign's feel from isolated underground competence to the first credible sense that an external allied military structure is now physically participating inside Boston.

================================================================================
02. WHAT THIS PACKAGE IS NOT
================================================================================

To keep the Boston arc honest and proportionate, this package is explicitly not:

- a British invasion of New England
- a large conventional landing force
- a substitute for the AFC / local Boston underground
- an excuse to skip Romeo's block-by-block struggle
- the point where the city is effectively won
- a pretext for early unlimited air support, armored columns, or broad blue-water dominance

Britain remains a strained post-PANDORA state with selective reach. The Boston package must feel hard-won, finite, and carefully risk-managed.

================================================================================
03. ENTRY LOGIC — WHY VICKERS ARRIVES WHEN SHE DOES
================================================================================

Vickers does not arrive at the start of the Boston arc. Her package becomes viable only after three conditions are met:

1. SAS / SBS have already established that Boston contains a resistance partner worth reinforcing.
2. The harbor strike and related disruption have proven that Russian occupation logistics in Boston can be damaged meaningfully.
3. Britain has a plausible temporary maritime access window and a local receiving structure that will not simply waste the risk of widening support.

This preserves the existing progression rule that British support begins as covert competence and only later widens into meaningful support and CASEVAC.

Operationally, Vickers arrives not because London suddenly has spare force, but because the earlier covert phase has justified the risk of committing a slightly larger, still limited, expeditionary package.

04. LT. DOROTHY ANNE VICKERS — CORE PROFILE LOCK
================================================================================

NAME
Lt. Dorothy Anne Vickers

FUNCTION
Mission-facing British officer and visible commander of the later Royal Marines / Royal Navy support package in Boston.

RANK / POSITIONING RULE
Keep her at lieutenant level as a deliberately lean, forward, mission-leading figure rather than inflating her into a strategic-theater commander. The package should feel like something driven by competence, personal authority, and exact operational control — not by rank spectacle.

BRANCH LOGIC
Royal Navy officer working closely with a Royal Marines package and maritime support element. In practical game-facing use, the player reads her as the person who makes the later British layer function.

AGE / BEARING
Early to mid-thirties. Young enough to still be physically and psychologically credible in the field-facing role the campaign needs. Senior enough that nobody doubts why she has command.

VOICE / TEMPERAMENT
Exact, unsentimental, fast-reading, low-drama. She should not sound theatrical, aristocratic, or slogan-heavy. Her confidence comes from velocity of understanding, not performative hardness.

Her best mode is:
- clipped without sounding robotic
- composed without sounding cold
- authoritative without sounding pompous
- dry enough to feel British, but never written as a parody of Britishness

She is not Mace's personality in officer form. Mace carries veteran stillness. Vickers carries calibrated forward pressure.

CHARACTER CORE
Vickers is the officer who understands that in Boston the decisive thing is not planting a flag. It is creating a support structure that lets someone else keep moving and survive the counterblow.

That makes her ideal for Romeo's campaign. Romeo is fighting to define what liberation means. Vickers arrives as an allied officer who understands that helping without owning the story is part of her job.

INTERPERSONAL RULE
She should respect local competence quickly and visibly. She does not waste time making Romeo or Elena prove themselves twice. Once she has the picture, she moves.

KEY CHARACTER LINE (GUIDING ONLY)
"We're not here to occupy your liberation. We're here to make it harder for them to stop it."

Use sparingly if used at all. Treat as tonal compass more than protected dialogue.

06. WHAT THE ROYAL MARINES SPECIFICALLY ADD
================================================================================

The Royal Marines package must contribute something meaningfully different from both SAS/SBS and the AFC.

They add five things.

1. HOLDING POWER
SAS and SBS are ideal for getting in, breaking something important, and leaving or disappearing. Royal Marines are the British layer that can seize a position, secure it, and keep it usable long enough for the resistance to benefit.

2. DISCIPLINED TRANSITION SPACE
The Boston resistance is brave, adaptable, and local. It is not a full formal military support system. Royal Marines create temporary bridges between insurgent action and structured military sustainment.

3. CASUALTY STABILITY
This is the gameplay and narrative hinge. Once Vickers's package is active, a badly hit operation does not feel automatically terminal in the same way it did earlier. The coalition can now catch people who would previously have been lost.

4. SHORE-SIDE MARITIME VIABILITY
SBS opens the maritime lane covertly. Royal Marines help keep pieces of that lane open long enough to matter.

5. LIBERATION AS INFRASTRUCTURE
This is the biggest thematic contribution. The Royal Marines package should make Boston feel less like a series of brave attacks and more like the beginning of recoverable civic ground.

07. OPERATIONAL POSTURE INSIDE BOSTON
================================================================================

Vickers's package should be used in four recurring modes.

MODE 1 — HIDDEN RECEIVING NODE
A concealed dockside / warehouse / industrial-edge foothold where people, matériel, and casualties can move through in carefully controlled windows.

MODE 2 — TEMPORARY STRONGPOINT SUPPORT
A building, quay, terminal edge, workshop belt, or reclaimed service space held just long enough to enable the next move.

MODE 3 — MOBILE REINFORCEMENT
A small Royal Marines element appears when the campaign needs a disciplined allied shock of support rather than a full separate front.

MODE 4 — EXFIL / RECOVERY FRAMEWORK
The British layer gives late-Boston missions real extraction logic: boats, hidden coastal pickup, secure transfer point, treated casualties, or a defended onward route.

08. GAMEPLAY / CAMPAIGN CONSEQUENCE LOCKS
================================================================================

The Vickers package should unlock support in stages, not all at once.

STAGE 1 — PRESENCE FELT, NOT YET FULLY USABLE
- rumors of British movement become confirmed
- evidence of organized foreign military competence appears in mission spaces
- player sees effects before broad access

STAGE 2 — LIMITED ACCESS
- one or two missions gain British support options
- a temporary casualty collection point becomes available
- a small amount of ammunition / specialist support or route assistance becomes real

STAGE 3 — LATE-GAME SUPPORT THRESHOLD
- British military CASEVAC becomes available in a way the player can feel
- one or two stronger combined operations become possible
- the campaign's momentum changes because survival odds and operational imagination both expand

STAGE 4 — COALITION WEIGHT WITHOUT FULL CONVENTIONAL DOMINANCE
- British presence is now openly shaping the battle space in selected sectors
- still finite, still vulnerable, still not citywide control
- enough to make the player believe liberation can persist after a mission ends
