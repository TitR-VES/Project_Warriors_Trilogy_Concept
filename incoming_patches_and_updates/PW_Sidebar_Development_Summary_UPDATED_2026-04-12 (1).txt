AFC / BOSTON RESISTANCE VISUAL IDENTITY:
  Republic Shield — Primary AFC emblem. Upright shield silhouette with a central
    five-point star and a rising wedge / chevron from the lower portion. Meaning:
    protection of civilians, defense of American soil, and active resistance against
    occupation. Must read cleanly in one color and be sprayable fast. [THREAD LOCK]
  Breach Star — AFC field tag / propaganda-wall mark. Bold five-point star breaking
    through a horizontal barricade or block shape. Meaning: breach, defiance, violent
    disruption of occupation control. Used for fast spray tags, sabotage claims, rough
    morale markings, and improvised wall messaging rather than polished formal branding.
    [THREAD LOCK]
  Union Mark — Pre-existing Boston underground emblem. Angular industrial bands creating
    a hidden / implied star in negative space. Feels local, civic, industrial,
    neighborhood-rooted, and suitable for workshops, reclaimed vehicles, barricades,
    safehouses, and district-level resistance spaces. [THREAD LOCK]
  Relationship / progression:
    — Early Boston: Union Mark appears first as local resistance identity.
    — Mid Boston: Union Mark and Republic Shield begin appearing together as AFC
      coordination and Romeo's integration deepen.
    — Late Boston: Republic Shield becomes the dominant umbrella emblem while Union Mark
      remains on local vehicles, workshops, barricades, and veteran neighborhood
      strongholds. [THREAD LOCK]
  Visual design rules:
    — Avoid skulls, rifle silhouettes, eagle marks, decorative heraldry, or tactical-brand
      aesthetics.
    — Favor heavy geometry, stencil-readability, one-color survivability, and fast
      spray-paint execution.
    — Preferred palette for polished applications: muted red, off-white, dark navy.
      Real field use should still work in plain white or off-white spray paint.
    — Union Mark should not read as a simplified Republic Shield; it must feel like an
      older, local Boston resistance tradition later aligned with the AFC. [THREAD LOCK]
