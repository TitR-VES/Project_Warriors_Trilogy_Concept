PROJECT WARRIORS
ROMEO / KEARNEY / BOSTON WEAPONS REFERENCE
Compendium-ready archival pass
Status: LOCKED WORKING CANON

SCOPE
This page consolidates the rifle continuity and authorship logic spanning Romeo’s late Kentucky phase, Boston transition, Eamonn Kearney’s armorer role, and Romeo’s Boston signature primary.

LOCKED CORE RULE
These rifles must not blur together.
- DON’T TREAD = where Romeo comes from
- Unnamed Boston-entry rifle = how Romeo crosses into Boston without arriving empty-handed
- CIVIL DISOBEDIENCE = what Boston decides he should carry once he has earned it
- WORK ORDER = Kearney’s personal benchmark rifle and supportability standard

If preserved, the progression reads as place-identity and authorship. If collapsed, it reads as generic upgrade logic.

Kearney anchor
Eamonn Kearney is Boston’s hidden armorer / repair specialist / small-arms sustainment node. He is not a vendor-first character. He is the recurring local infrastructure figure who proves Boston had hidden professional continuity before Romeo arrived and who gives the city’s liberation material credibility through repair, triage, magazine discipline, optics / mount honesty, and practical small-arms continuity.

Locked profile notes
- Name: Eamonn Kearney
- Age: 58
- Irish-born Boston-based gunsmith / machinist / repair specialist
- Buried history of quiet clandestine armorer work around republican underground networks in an earlier conflict
- Trade-first, dry, exact, anti-romantic, bench-authoritative
- Main node: hidden upper storage/service-floor workshop in a partially powered dead light-industrial/commercial building reached by back-lot route and freight elevator
- Permanent shop remains separate from Van Hult’s reclaimed body shop, though occasional overflow collaboration is acceptable

WEAPON 01 — DON’T TREAD
Status: Locked earlier-war / Kentucky-era Romeo signature primary
Campaign ownership: Game 2 / Kentucky / early-war American resistance phase
Meaning:
- Kentucky-place identity
- inherited / local insurgent-war texture
- emotionally tied to earlier American resistance phase
Use rule:
- DON’T TREAD should dominate the Kentucky phase strongly enough to earn its place-identity
- It should not be dragged too deep into Boston
Exit rule:
- Romeo parts with DON’T TREAD intentionally before or at the Boston threshold
- Best logic remains returning / leaving it with Dale or Dale’s people, or otherwise within the Kentucky resistance ecosystem it belongs to

PROGRESSION LOGIC
Phase A — Kentucky
- Active rifle: DON’T TREAD
- Strong earlier-war / Kentucky identity phase

Phase B — Kentucky to Boston bridge
- DON’T TREAD remains with Romeo at first, then is intentionally left behind in the Kentucky resistance ecosystem

Phase C — Boston arrival and foothold
- Active rifle: unnamed Boston-entry rifle
- Romeo reaches Boston armed but not yet city-authored

Phase D — Early Kearney access
- Kearney judges and maintains the Boston-entry rifle while increasingly reading it as too costly / too compromised to remain the answer

Phase E — THE RIGHT RIFLE
- Active rifle changes: Boston-entry rifle -> CIVIL DISOBEDIENCE
- This is the decisive handoff; not an upgrade, a judgment

Phase F — Act III onward
- Active rifle: CIVIL DISOBEDIENCE
- Romeo now carries Boston’s rifle through widened battlespace, harbor phase, late retained-ground fighting, and finale

Across all Boston phases
- Kearney personal rifle remains WORK ORDER

MISSION SNAPSHOT
DON’T TREAD
- Primary through Kentucky / early-war Game 2 phase
- Exits intentionally before Boston identity takes hold

Boston-entry rifle
- Primary through Boston arrival, foothold, fractured local contact, A-10 suburban aftermath, first Kearney meeting, sustainment revisit, early retention phase
- Retired in THE RIGHT RIFLE

CIVIL DISOBEDIENCE
- Primary from late Act II onward
- Mission 08 first blooding, harbor / widened battlespace, post-contact revisit, Act III normalization, Act IV retained-ground and finale

WORK ORDER
- Present as Kearney’s constant benchmark weapon across his scenes

CHARACTER AUTHORSHIP RULES
- Romeo does not author WORK ORDER
- Kearney does not author DON’T TREAD
- Kearney does not author the Boston-entry rifle
- Kearney does author CIVIL DISOBEDIENCE

PRESENTATION RULES
Do:
- keep each rifle in its own place-identity lane
- treat rifle transitions as authorship shifts, not just gameplay swaps
- preserve Kearney’s dry standards-driven voice
- let CIVIL DISOBEDIENCE feel like a judgment
- let WORK ORDER remain Kearney’s stable benchmark

Do not:
- blur the Boston-entry rifle into a signature build
- let DON’T TREAD own Boston
- let CIVIL DISOBEDIENCE feel like a loot reward
- let WORK ORDER become Romeo-adjacent enough to lose its personal Kearney identity
- over-name or over-explain rifles once their role is established

COMPRESSED READ
Romeo’s rifle progression moves from DON’T TREAD as Kentucky’s earlier-war insurgent identity rifle, through an unnamed Boston-entry rifle that bridges him into the city without arming him from zero, and finally into CIVIL DISOBEDIENCE, the Boston signature rifle that Eamonn Kearney builds / provides once Romeo has earned the city’s trust. WORK ORDER remains Kearney’s personal benchmark rifle throughout, giving the arc a stable internal standard and preventing CIVIL DISOBEDIENCE from collapsing into generic upgrade logic.

FINAL WORKING RULE
If this packet is functioning correctly, the player should feel:
- DON’T TREAD = where Romeo came from
- Unnamed Boston-entry rifle = what carried him across
- CIVIL DISOBEDIENCE = what Boston placed in his hands
- WORK ORDER = the standard that judged him worthy of it
