BUILD 01 — GRAY GHOST
Character: CPT Trevor "Spook" Gartner — Signature Primary
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Platform:       Noveske Rifleworks N-4 Gen3
Caliber:        300 Blackout (subsonic primary / supersonic available)
Barrel:         10.5" select fire
Finish:         Solid dark gray Cerakote overall — no markings

ATTACHMENTS:
  Handguard:    NWS-9 M-LOK
  Optic 1:      Aimpoint Micro T-2 1x22 red dot on 0.8" riser (primary)
  Optic 2:      Aimpoint 3x magnifier, swing-to-side mount, behind red dot
  Canted sights:45-degree offset MBUS Pro Offset iron sights (black)
  Laser module: BE Meyers MAWL-DA — player-unlocked after Burning Daylight
  Weapon light: SureFire Scout, M-LOK, extended momentary pressure switch
  Foregrip:     Milled aluminum low-profile angled skeleton grip with
                integrated barrier stop — front-mid underside of handguard
  Suppressor:   SureFire SOCOM556-RC2 (standard)
                [Supersonic mag available from Tripper as break-glass option]

AMMUNITION TYPES (field-swappable via inspect menu):
  300 BLK SUBSONIC   — default; near-silent through suppressor
  300 BLK SUPERSONIC — louder; signals mission has changed character

INSPECT ANIMATION NOTE:
  Dust cover bears a faint, nearly worn-away factory caliber marking
  reading "300 BLK" — worn from years of handling rather than deliberate
  removal. Visible only during the inspect animation.
  MAWL-DA, once unlocked: thumb clicks activation dial one position
  forward and back; IR beam briefly pulses visible in inspect view.

BUILD NAME displayed in inspect overlay: GRAY GHOST

BUILD 05 — DON'T TREAD
Character: LT Ramón "Romeo" Álvarez — Early Game 2 Primary
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Platform:       Ruger AC-556 (military selective-fire variant)
Caliber:        5.56x45mm NATO
Config:         Folding stock, compact tactical variant
Magazines:      20-round factory magazines

PROVENANCE:
  Given to Romeo by Dale Pruett at the end of mission Low Ground.
  Dale retrieved it from a locked gun safe in his barn.
  Handoff animation: unhurried — Dale checks action, wipes receiver
  with a cloth, passes it stock-first to Romeo.

ENGRAVING:
  Left side of receiver — small eagle clutching a banner reading:
  "DON'T TREAD"
  Spelled exactly as it appears on the Gadsden flag.
  Appears to have been done by hand, possibly with a Dremel,
  at some point in the weapon's past.

ENVIRONMENTAL ECHO:
  A detail in Dale's house — framed photograph or jacket patch —
  echoes the same truncated motto, tying Dale to the weapon's origin
  without any dialogue drawing attention to it.

NARRATIVE FUNCTION:
  Early-campaign primary only. 20-round magazine limit and moderate
  accuracy ceiling mean the player naturally phases it out as better
  options become available — making the moment of setting it aside
  a quiet story beat in itself.

STORY FLAG:
  In Act Two, a member of the Three Percenter / Boogaloo-adjacent
  militia group recognizes the engraving when Romeo is carrying
  DON'T TREAD — creating a moment of dangerous mistaken assumption
  Romeo must navigate carefully.
  [Full development reserved for main narrative thread]

INSPECT ANIMATION NOTE:
  The "DON'T TREAD" engraving is clearly visible during the inspect
  animation. No character draws attention to it.

BUILD NAME displayed in inspect overlay: DON'T TREAD

BUILD 06 — TRG M10
Character: SSGT Aki "Frost" Peltola — Signature Primary
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Platform:       Sako TRG M10
Caliber:        .338 Lapua Magnum
Barrel:         27" fluted match-grade, threaded muzzle
Finish:         Chassis — olive drab
                Barrel and suppressor — matte black
                Scope body — matte black
                Scope caps — tan (always closed when not in use)

ATTACHMENTS:
  Suppressor:   Sako / Vaime SAR .338 (Finnish-made; run as permanent
                fixture in field conditions)
  Optic:        Schmidt & Bender PM II 5-25x56
                (reticle worn at center from years of use; tracking exact)
  Bipod:        Atlas BT10 V8 on ARCA rail at forend
  Magazine:     5-round detachable box; 6 spare magazines in dedicated
                chest pouch
  Ammunition:   Lapua Scenar 250gr BTHP
                (hand-loaded in garrison; factory Lapua in field)
  Stock:        Adjustable LOP and cheek piece height; both set to Frost's
                exact measurements; marked with small white ink lines for
                return-to-zero after transport

NAME NOTE:
  The TRG M10 is not a name Frost gave the weapon. It is simply what
  the weapon is — the platform name and the build name are the same
  because the weapon and the man are so closely identified that the
  distinction ceased to matter. Frost does not name his weapons.
  The TRG M10 is just what it is, and everyone who knows Frost
  knows what that means.

BUILD NAME displayed in inspect overlay: TRG M10

INSPECT ANIMATION NOTE:
  Frost rotates rifle slowly; runs right thumb along bolt handle
  (habitual checking motion); checks suppressor lockup with two fingers
  (quarter-turn test); does not open bolt; scope caps remain closed
  throughout. The rifle is always ready but never presumptuous about it.

INSPECT WEAPON MECHANIC — DESIGN REFERENCE
================================================================================

BEHAVIOR BY WEAPON CLASS:

  Rifles / carbines / DMRs / LMGs:
    Arms-length presentation; weapon pulled slightly back and rotated
    slowly to the right; three-quarter view of receiver, optics,
    handguard; approximately 3-4 seconds full inspect; support hand
    repositions naturally to allow the view

  Pistols / compact SMGs:
    Weapon raised slightly and turned to strong-side profile;
    for weapons with visible hammers (CZ P-09, HK45CT, P-99)
    animation includes a brief hammer or safety check motion —
    one mechanical gesture that varies per weapon

  Shotguns:
    Short rack-and-check motion; forend pulled back a half-inch
    and released; functional rather than aesthetic

SIGNATURE WEAPON EXTENDED INSPECT:
  Signature builds receive slightly longer or more detailed animations
  Build name appears in small text at bottom of inspect frame for:
  GRAY GHOST / NOTHING / FREYA / TRG M10 / ARGINT / DON'T TREAD
  WREN does not appear in overlay — discoverable only via
  bolt-open empty-chamber animation

OVERLAY SYSTEM (field modification mode):
  Semi-transparent non-intrusive overlay during inspect
  Labels appear near attachment points with available interactions
  Empty rails with nothing available show no label
  Field-removable attachments interactable during inspect:
    — Suppressors (threaded barrels)
    — Optics on quick-detach mounts
    — Weapon lights (M-LOK / Picatinny)
    — MAWL-DA and MAWL-C1+ laser modules
    — Foregrips (M-LOK / Picatinny)
    — Ammunition type where multiple types carried
  Non-removable in field (no prompt appears):
    — Fixed barrels
    — Integrated handguards
    — Permanently mounted hardware

AMMUNITION SWITCHING (inspect menu):
  Most weapons: single caliber option shown
  GRAY GHOST: 300 BLK SUBSONIC / 300 BLK SUPERSONIC
    Switching requires magazine change animation — approximately
    4 seconds; not skippable; tactical risk if done mid-contact

REMOVAL ANIMATIONS:
  Physical attachment removal: approximately 3 seconds
  Not skippable — removing a suppressor mid-firefight is a
  deliberate tactical decision with real time cost

CHARACTER-SPECIFIC CLEANING ANIMATIONS (gunsmith):
  Romeo:  Fast and functional; muscle memory; no ceremony
  Ducky:  Methodical; checks each component before reassembly
  Spook:  Slow and almost meditative — the cleaning is something
          he does when he needs to think
