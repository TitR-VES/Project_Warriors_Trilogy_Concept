Reference: Roy Batty's monologue, Blade Runner (1982). Rationale: the game gives the player a chance to witness things they'd never believe — the scale and weight of a world at war glimpsed through the eyes of individuals caught inside it. The studio name reflects this thematic core.

- To be displayed on a CRT-style screen in the studio splash sequence (see below)
- Design direction confirmed; final visual execution TBD

STUDIO DESCRIPTOR [LOCKED]

— displayed beneath the TITR logo on the splash screen

STUDIO SPLASH SEQUENCE [DEVELOPMENT]

- Camera: slightly off-axis from screen — not fully head-on. Physical environment visible around the device (bezel, chassis, ambient surroundings)

SECTION 6 — THEMATIC & NARRATIVE FLAVOR
========================================================

GAME CONCEPT BLURB [LOCKED — draft, subject to refinement]
"This is a tactical shooter. It aspires to be a good one — and hopefully succeeds. It will recommend realism. But for the players who follow the story, the game isn't really about the shooting.

It's about people. Extraordinary people, mostly — but still human. They're capable. They know how to fight. They are warriors.

This is a story of people who are good at surviving, caught in a war that each of them, in their own way, chose to fight. A war they didn't start. A war they most likely cannot end. They can only hope to make the right choices, to do their best, and to live to fight another day."

NOTE: "Warriors" is deliberately chosen to encompass both military and civilian protagonists — the game's cast includes non-military characters (Ebonn, resistance cell members, civilians caught in the war) as well as operators.

THE NORTH STAR [LOCKED — internal creative reference]
The goal for trailers and promotional material: the player sees it on their screen and thinks "holy shit, when is this out?" This feeling — generated by the Good Morning America teaser, by the aircraft scenes, by the paratroopers over the Smoky Mountains — is the benchmark.

SECTION 1: THREE-CAMPAIGN STORY OVERVIEW (GAME 1)
================================================================

THE OVERARCHING CONSPIRACY
--------------------------
All three campaigns converge on a deep-state plot codenamed RIPTIDE RED.
Architects: Edward Gregory Goldberg (CIA codename: KINGMAKER) and Gillian
Stockbridge (codename: CHAPERONE), with complicity of President Barry James Crodyn.

Their instrument: TETHERBREAK — a covert CIA program weaponizing America's own
autonomous military drone systems. Operational plan: PANDORA — deploying TETHERBREAK
as a false-flag attack on US soil to destabilize the government and install Crodyn
in an unconstitutional third term.

Goldberg also operates the Shadow Underground (Forever Pact / Eclipse Society /
KINGMAKER) — a global human trafficking, blackmail, and dark-money empire centered
on the underground complex beneath Montoya Farm, New Mexico.

Goldberg sold intelligence about Riptide Red to multiple hostile foreign governments,
triggering a coordinated Russian, North Korean, and Iranian amphibious invasion of
the US West Coast, a Russian push into Western Europe, and a Chinese move on Taiwan.
Effectively: World War Three used as cover for the conspirators' escape.

PANDORA canonical attack date: September 3, 2028.
US Presidential Election: November 3, 2028.


CAMPAIGN 1 — CRITTER SQUAD
---------------------------
Playable character: LT Ramón "Romeo" Álvarez, Fire Team Delta, 75th Ranger Regiment
Squad: Dingo, Howler, Beaver

Arc: Begins in eastern Poland (NATO eastern flank reinforcement). PANDORA activates,
autonomous systems turn against US forces. Squad fights west through Czech Republic
and Austria to Ramstein Air Base, Germany. Assigned to protect classified geospatial
intelligence sites across Europe alongside AEGIS Squad:
- Faroe Islands
- French Alps
- Greek island of Lemnos (Aegean)
- Hinderloopen, Netherlands

With all flights grounded, squads travel overland through Brittany to French naval
base at Île Longue. Defend French nuclear submarines Le Terrible and Le Vigilant
from enemy sabotage. AEGIS diverted to Rotterdam. Romeo's squad boards nuclear
submarine USS District of Columbia, surfaces off Ventura, California.

US campaign:
- Links up with Army/Marine forces
- Fights North Korean forces out of North Hollywood (mission: Down Rodeo)
- Pushes through South Beverly Hills toward coast
- Secures Port of Los Angeles
- Defends Long Beach Airport at Battle of Signal Hill
- Convoy south toward San Diego
- Climax: Battle of Coronado Island — defending North Island Naval Air Station
  against massive joint amphibious assault (shared climax across all three campaigns)

Key character: André Nathaniel Bridges, USMC SSgt, New Orleans. Meets Romeo at Port
of LA. Signal Hill disagreement (Rangers push vs Marines consolidate) — outcome TBD.
[FLAG: Signal Hill resolution and Coronado climax detail]

CAMPAIGN 3 — RIPTIDE RED
------------------------
Playable character: CPT Trevor "Spook" Gartner,
Victor Two Bravo "The Tourists" — CIA Directorate of Operations, Ground Branch
Team: Tripper (Bartlett), Smoke (Oakenfold)

Handler: Senior Case Officer Matthew Mullen (mission callsign: Regent)
Frankfurt CIA officer: Cameron Steele (Emperor)
CIA Director of Operations: Robert Macmillan (Bishop) — COMPROMISED

Mission sequence:
1. AMSTERDAM (Prologue) — Arrive undercover at Schipol. Meet Emperor at Eastern
   Docklands warehouse. Briefed on PANDORA — suspicious restricted tech shipment
   from Turkmenistan headed to Atlanta, currently in transit to Vienna.

2. VIENNA — Report to CIA officer Kathy Hughes (Archduke) at US Embassy.
   Mission: Raid Lebanese terror cell safehouse, Hernals district (17th). Find it
   empty. Interrupted by armed men. Rooftop parkour chase across Hernals, motorcycle
   escape onto city beltway, high-speed firefight (GP SP10A3 Stribog PDW — Vienna
   mission only, cannot be kept). Pursuers' van destroyed. Exfil to Port of Vienna.
   Recovery: tablet from safehouse. Intel points to Betta Logistics GmbH Frankfurt
   and owner Simenon Christophe Navarre (French-Canadian-German), and his partner
   Karyna Budanova (Austrian, executive at GenCorp).
   NOTE: Navarre → now Simon-Pierre Aubert [LOCKED]. Budanova → now Katja Reimann
   [LOCKED]. Both relocated to Évora, Alentejo, Portugal under new CIA identities.

3. FRANKFURT — Mission: Above My Pay Grade. Night infiltration of Navarre's house.
   Navarre gives access to laptop with shipping manifests → Warehouse 46, Frankfurt
   Airport. Player can give Navarre/Budanova new identities (optional — canonical
   for sequel). Team takes Navarre's keys/van, heads to airport.
   Warehouse swarming with Atlas Solutions personnel. Shipment already moved —
   tipped off (anomaly: someone warned Atlas). Team clears warehouse, extracts
   hard drive, destroys site. Hard drive contains partial PANDORA/TETHERBREAK
   references. Partial flight schedule recovered. DAOG contact Karl Alexander
   "Jaeger" Stanzler (PYTHIA) helps match to privately chartered flights.

4. ABU DHABI — Mission: Infrastructure Day. GenCorp regional HQ raid — rappel
   down skyscraper. Data obtained links shipment to Agarwal Berkhan (Indonesian-
   Emirati billionaire, majority GenCorp shareholder). Optional target: Leandro
   Blommaert (GenCorp Head of Data Security).

5. MEDAN CITY, INDONESIA — Operation DRAGONSLAYER / Mission: Man From Medan.
   Ordered to find and apprehend Berkhan. Fail to capture him — Berkhan escapes
   by helicopter, shot down mid-flight by apparently sabotaged Indonesian air
   defense. Silenced before team could reach him.
   Regent "urgently reassigned." Team placed under Macmillan (Bishop).

6. MIAMI BEACH — Mission: Pandora's Box. NSA contacts Keith Simmons and Jessica
   Lynch brief team: restricted components tracked to far-right cult Kali Yuga
   Surf Club (KYSC), now holed up in abandoned Desautels Resort Hotel, Miami Beach
   (based on real-life Deauville Beach Resort, 6701 Collins Ave, demolished 2022).
   Hotel occupied jointly by KYSC and Los Sietes cartel as muscle.
   KYSC: brainwashed MKUltra-style conditioning at Montoya Farm, CogniConnect neural
   implants that detonate on death — identities permanently destroyed.
   Team neutralizes PANDORA device and all 5 KYSC members. All bear identical symbol
   (old MKUltra era marking associated with Montoya Farm).
   Ambushed by cartel on exit — setup by Macmillan. Escape through collapsing hotel
   via elevator shaft and parking garage. Media reports as foiled lone-wolf attack.
   Goldberg later reveals: Miami was only one of many PANDORA cells.

7. NEW MEXICO — Mission: Burning Daylight. Night infiltration of Montoya Farm
   compound near San Rafael. Cover: stolen U-HAUL truck and disguises from meth
   producer "Uncle" Jack Pruitt's men (New Mexico white supremacist biker gang).
   Gala event at mansion provides stealth cover. Spook uses directional mic to
   tail Stockbridge, eavesdrop on phone conversations.
   Security room accessed via second-floor concealed entrance (keycard from
   Ballroom Annex cloak room desk). CCTV reveals vast underground complex:
   - Hidden prison
   - Inpatient medical facility / operating theater
   - Dormitories for long-term living
   - Underground plaza with simulated sky ceiling
   - "Movie sets" contaminated with bodily fluids / snuff film production evidence
   - Same KYSC symbols on tunnel walls
   - Server room: Tripper plants hardware implant, extracts encrypted archive
   Exfil: narrow vertical shaft with ladder, hidden door behind filing cabinet
   in basement laundry room. Data decrypted at safehouse reveals Shadow Underground
   architecture — FSB, Mossad, IRGC, Chinese MSS, North Korean intelligence, entire

SECTION 5: FROST BACKSTORY — COLD FRONTIER 07
================================================================

THE INCIDENT [LOCKED]
----------------------
Location: Near Inarijärvi (Lake Inari), East Lapland, Finland —
near the Finno-Russian national border.
Operation: Finnish Special Border Jaeger Company border security mission.
Frost's role at the time: Young NCO, serving with his spotter and close comrade
Jussi Mustonen.

What happened:
Frost and Jussi's position was ambushed by two Chechen members of a Russian
Spetsnaz Arctic operations team. One Spetsnaz attacker charged directly at Frost.
Jussi, in an immediate instinct to protect Frost, threw himself at the attacker —
taking a hatchet to the neck. Jussi was fatally wounded.

Meanwhile Frost engaged the other attacker in the cramped elevated fortified lookout
position. A close-quarters struggle ensued as Frost tried to grab the attacker's
pistol. He failed to secure it. He was shot point-blank in the face and thrown from
the elevated position, managing to drag the attacker down with him as they both
plunged several dozen feet off a clifftop into the icy lake water below.

Underwater, the struggle continued until Frost — severely wounded, hypothermic,
barely conscious — managed to get his bearings, grasp the back of the Spetsnaz
operator's helmet as the enemy briefly faced away, and drive his head face-first
into a jagged rock outcropping underwater.

Frost's unit found him unconscious on the lake shore, half in the water. The dead
Spetsnaz operator was floating face down in a crimson-tinted patch of lake water
a few yards out.

Frost's first words upon regaining consciousness (in Finnish):
"Naamani? Sinun pitäisi nähdä, miten se toinen kusipää päätyi. Unohda koko juttu,
meidän on löydettävä Jussi! Se toinen venäläinen pani hänet pahasti pieleen."

English caption translation:
"My face? You should see how that other asshole ended up. Forget the whole thing,
we have to find Jussi! That other Russian screwed him badly."

This moment is designed as a playable flashback sequence.
[FLAG: Confirm placement and integration of flashback within Ducky campaign]

THE COVER-UP [LOCKED]
---------------------
Official designation: Cold Frontier 07 (winter military exercise).
Jussi Mustonen's death was officially reported as drowning after falling through
ice while crossing the frozen surface of Lake Inari.
Cover story rationale: A Russian Spetsnaz team operating inside Finnish sovereign
territory is an act of war. Finnish and NATO leadership chose to bury it to avoid
escalation of conflict with Russia at the time.
The cover story is mundane enough to generate no questions — the kind of death
that happens in Arctic conditions.

What this means for Frost:
Jussi doesn't just die — he dies officially as a clumsy accident. No record of
his sacrifice. No acknowledgment that he threw himself at an armed Spetsnaz operator
to save his spotter's life. In the official version, he just fell through ice.
The real wound beneath the physical scarring: not just that Frost couldn't save him,
but that the institution they both served decided Jussi's actual death was
inconvenient and replaced it with a lesser one.
Somewhere in the Kremlin, someone signed off on the operation and was never held
accountable.

FROST'S RELATIONSHIP WITH HIS SCARRING [LOCKED]
-----------------------------------------------
Frost is not ashamed of his scarring. He sees it as a reminder of sacrifice and
perseverance in the face of adversity — a badge of courage in his own mind.
He never talks about it that way to others, preferring not to discuss how he got
the injury.
He wears the mask primarily because the scarring makes him recognizable —
a significant operational OPSEC liability.
He is also aware that the disfigurement can disturb people, and hides his face
when it makes his day easier. This is pragmatic, not emotional.

-------------------------------------------------

western viewers especially. The time has changed, but the implications of human loss. hardship, and suffering that this imagery evokes are the same
regardless of time and place. As part of this mission, 2 hospital ships, the Dawn of Mercy and the Beacon of Hope are dispatched to UN controlled
ports on the US’s eastern seaboard.
