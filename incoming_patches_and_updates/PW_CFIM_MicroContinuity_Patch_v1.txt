PROJECT WARRIORS — MICRO-CONTINUITY PATCH
CFIM / COGNICONNECT DISTINCTION
Status: Approved canon refinement

INSERTION BLOCK:

COGNICONNECT / CFIM CLARIFICATION:
CogniConnect is the broader implant architecture present across multiple hostile
programs and covert applications in the Project Warriors setting. CFIM
(Counter-Forensic Identification Module) is a separate specialized add-on
package integrated only into selected War Wolves implant stacks. CFIM is not a
default feature of CogniConnect.

WAR WOLVES — IDENTITY MYSTERY:
The War Wolves carry no identification. Their CogniConnect implant stacks
include CFIM (Counter-Forensic Identification Module) packages that trigger a
terminal anti-exploitation detonation on death, catastrophic implant breach, or
designated fail-dead conditions. The effect is intended to destroy or severely
degrade the face and adjacent cranial structures, corrupt implant-resident data,
and deny rapid postmortem forensic identification. CFIM is designed to defeat
rapid battlefield and near-term forensic identification, not to guarantee the
perfect erasure of every possible biometric trace under laboratory conditions.

KYSC / MONTOYA FARM DISTINCTION:
KYSC-conditioned Montoya Farm victims may carry CogniConnect implants as part of
the broader conditioning / control architecture, but they are not fitted with
CFIM packages. Standard CogniConnect implantation does not imply terminal
counter-forensic detonation. The War Wolves' postmortem facial-destruction /
rapid-identification-denial effect is specific to CFIM-equipped operators.

RETCON CLARIFICATION:
Earlier broad wording may imply that CogniConnect implantation itself causes the
War Wolves' postmortem anti-identification detonation. Current canon narrows
that effect: the terminal facial-destruction / rapid-forensic-denial behavior is
specific to CFIM-equipped War Wolves, not to all CogniConnect users.

REPLACEMENT GUIDANCE:
- Replace broad phrasing such as "CogniConnect detonates on death" with
  "CFIM-equipped CogniConnect stacks trigger a terminal anti-exploitation
  detonation on death" when referring to War Wolves.
- Replace broad phrasing such as "no ID possible" with
  "prevents rapid forensic identification" or
  "denies rapid postmortem exploitation" unless a more absolute phrasing is
  explicitly desired for in-world imperfect reporting.
- Do not apply CFIM language to KYSC unless a future source explicitly changes
  the canon split above.

SHORT IN-WORLD EXPLANATION:
"CogniConnect is the base hardware. CFIM is the black add-on. KYSC had the
first. The War Wolves had both."
