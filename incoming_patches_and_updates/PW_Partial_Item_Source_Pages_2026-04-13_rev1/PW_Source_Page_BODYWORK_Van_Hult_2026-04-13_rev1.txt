================================================================================
01. PURPOSE
================================================================================

BODYWORK is Romeo Game 3's optional Boston neighborhood-reclamation branch centered on Mitchell Van Hult, a former USAF technician and body-shop owner whose seized garage has been turned into an occupation motor pool.

The branch is not revenge fantasy. Its narrative point is that liberation becomes real when ordinary skilled people recover civic function, practical infrastructure, and neighborhood confidence.

Guiding line:
It breaches as a weapon, then is reclaimed as infrastructure.

================================================================================
02. VAN HULT — LOCKED CHARACTER FUNCTION
================================================================================

Name: Mitchell Van Hult
Age band: mid-50s
Background: former USAF technician; later Boston body-shop owner / tradesman
Role in branch: local civilian resistance anchor for vehicle reclamation / fabrication
Core read:
- proud
- exact
- community-minded
- not crank-coded
- not revenge-maniac-coded
- grievance is specific: the occupation stole his life’s work, his role in the neighborhood, and turned his shop into something crude and parasitic

Writing rule:
Van Hult should read as a tradesman whose dignity has been violated, not as a cartoon avenger.

================================================================================
03. PLACEMENT / STRUCTURE
================================================================================

Campaign placement:
- Romeo Game 3
- Act II / Neighborhood War
- Recommended as a four-mission optional branch plus one epilogue beat

Recommended district flavor:
- Newmarket / Widett Circle / South Bay industrial belt edge
- broad roads, back-lot access, service corridors, parking structure volume, construction plausibility, proximity to dense neighborhoods

Thematic function inside Boston campaign:
- proves liberation is not only about raids and firefights
- shows skilled civilians as infrastructure-makers rather than victims/background dressing
- turns reclaimed ground into something materially sustainable

================================================================================
04. BRANCH SHAPE
================================================================================

Mission 01 — THE SHOP
Function:
- meet Van Hult
- establish that his seized body shop now functions as a Russian motor pool / checkpoint support node
- show neighborhood cost
- seed the possibility of a tracked breaching vehicle built from construction equipment and community labor

Mission 02 — CONCRETE
Function:
- secure a half-finished construction site / parking-garage workspace
- restore limited power and usable shelter
- move salvage, tools, fuel, armor plate, welding gear, and heavy components
- establish hidden neighborhood build cell: welders, mechanics, electricians, operators, laborers, lookouts

Mission 03 — OPEN THE ROUTE
Function:
- Romeo-led shaping mission
- blind surveillance / comms picture
- remove likely overwatch and machine-gun positions
- isolate the checkpoint / motor-pool area
- shape the avenue so the breach can work without becoming a suicide charge

Mission 04 — BODYWORK
Function:
- moving assault built around Van Hult driving the improvised armored dozer through the checkpoint line
- Romeo does not ride the dozer
- Romeo advances on foot with the breach, clearing flank rooms, MG nests, rooftops, and route-blocking hardpoints
- fight should feel like a civic breach held together by timing, nerve, and practical planning rather than superhero machinery

Mission 05 — EPILOGUE BEAT
Function:
- ordinary life returns in partial form
- reclaimed block / street life / local music / neighborhood gathering
- emotional point is not triumphalism but proof that reclaimed ground can become lived space again

================================================================================
05. THE DOZER / BREACH VEHICLE
================================================================================

Design rule:
The vehicle is a one-purpose improvised armored bulldozer / breaching ram.
It is not a homemade supertank.

Threat envelope:
- barricades
- concrete obstacles
- light fortified checkpoints
- small arms
- fragments
- light occupation control nodes

Not for:
- tank duels
- reusable campaign-wide armor asset fantasy
- theatrical invulnerability

Construction logic:
- hidden build in parking garage / industrial cover space
- neighborhood labor cell assembles it from available industrial equipment and protection layers
- reads as ugly, plausible, welded, functional, and singular

Gameplay rule:
The dozer supports the breach. Romeo remains the playable assault element on foot.

================================================================================
06. MISSION 04 END-STATE — LOCKED
================================================================================

The final-state logic is locked as follows:
- the dozer completes its intended breaching role
- after smashing the avenue roadblock and helping collapse the motor-pool / checkpoint pocket, it suffers a final mobility kill
- preferred presentation: lodged off the avenue in rubble, rebar, debris, or broken street furniture
- acceptable cause set: thrown track, shattered drive component, engine failure, jammed undercarriage, cumulative battlefield damage, terrain entrapment
- it must read as worn out and dead, not martyred in a theatrical sacrifice shot

Temporary end-of-fight function:
- rear-mounted gun serves as a short-duration expedient strongpoint during the final stabilization push
- this is not a permanent hero emplacement

Post-fight diegetic rule:
- resistance strips recoverable weaponry and useful heavy components from it after the fight
- later revisit should show the hull repurposed into barricade / lookout / reclaimed defensive-civic infrastructure

Guiding revisit rule:
The neighborhood keeps the shell, not the weapon fantasy.

================================================================================
07. CAMPAIGN CONSEQUENCES
================================================================================

Completion consequences:
- Van Hult’s shop becomes a reclaimed vehicle repair / fabrication node
- wider Boston should show improved allied vehicle condition and improvised reinforcement capacity
- branch completion should quietly strengthen the sense that the resistance can hold and maintain space

Relationship to Kearney:
- Van Hult is not a substitute for Eamonn Kearney
- Kearney remains the hidden armorer / small-arms sustainment figure
- Van Hult handles heavier fabrication / vehicle-space continuity
- occasional overflow collaboration is acceptable; permanent identity collapse is not

================================================================================
08. WRITING / TONE RULES
================================================================================

Do:
- keep the branch grounded, practical, and civic-minded
- make the breach feel planned by workers and fighters together
- show cost, preparation, and physical labor
- preserve Boston’s layered geography and local continuity

Do not:
- frame Van Hult as a meme-killdozer reference in-text
- turn the dozer into a recurring magical combat asset
- make Romeo passenger to spectacle rather than active assault lead
- write the epilogue as a clean victory lap with no scars or material aftermath
