================================================================================
01. PURPOSE
================================================================================

This page governs Boston-era weapon identity for Romeo Game 3 and the associated hidden armorer / sustainment logic centered on Eamonn Kearney.

It exists to do three things:
- keep Romeo’s rifle progression legible and place-bound
- protect Kearney from sliding into generic vendor territory
- keep the Boston weapon language supportable, practical, and materially grounded

================================================================================
02. EAMONN KEARNEY — LOCKED FUNCTION
================================================================================

Name: Eamonn Kearney
Age: 58
Identity: Irish-born, Boston-based gunsmith / machinist / repair specialist with a buried history of quiet clandestine armorer work around earlier underground networks
Narrative function:
- hidden armorer / repair specialist
- sustainment node
- proof Boston had professional continuity before Romeo arrived
- recurring infrastructure figure, not shopkeeper-first content

Voice / behavioral rules:
- exact
- dry
- bench-authoritative
- unsentimental
- mildly territorial about workmanship
- contemptuous of preventable sloppiness and fashion-gun nonsense

Representative lines / logic:
- “That mag’s done.”
- “Captured doesn’t mean usable.”
- “There. Now it’s honest.”
- “Trust is for people. This is supportability.”
- “You’re getting less expensive.”

================================================================================
03. Kearney’s Permanent Node
================================================================================

Shop logic:
- hidden storage / service-floor workshop in a mostly dead light-industrial or service-belt building
- accessed by rear lot / service route / freight path logic
- not a glossy storefront
- not a loot room
- not a conventional RPG merchant space

Visual rule:
The space should read as professional continuity under occupation pressure: selective power, machine tools, repair benches, racks, crates, spare optics and mounts, magazines sorted by condition, honest wear, and supportability-first triage.

================================================================================
04. ROMEO BOSTON RIFLE PROGRESSION — LOCKED
================================================================================

A. EARLIER RIFLE — DON’T TREAD
- Place-bound to Kentucky / Game 2 identity
- Should be intentionally parted with before or at the Boston transition
- Do not drag deep into Boston as if nothing changed

B. BOSTON-ENTRY RIFLE — UNNAMED TRANSITIONAL CARBINE
- competent but narratively subordinate
- AR-pattern, practical, unsuppressed by default
- useful, credible, increasingly costly / compromised
- exists partly so Kearney has something real to judge and maintain

C. BOSTON SIGNATURE — BUILD 12: CIVIL DISOBEDIENCE
- Romeo’s Boston rifle from late Act II onward
- provided / built through Kearney
- this is Boston’s rifle in Romeo’s hands

Narrative rule:
Romeo should feel like he carries different places differently. Boston is not a continuation skin for Kentucky.

================================================================================
05. BUILD 10 — WORK ORDER
================================================================================

Owner: Eamonn Kearney
Function: personal benchmark rifle / shop standard
Configuration direction:
- old-service-pattern Colt M4A1 lane
- 14.5-inch old-service carbine
- KAC RAS
- Aimpoint PRO
- SureFire Scout
- plain two-point sling
- A2 flash hider
- no laser by default

Meaning:
WORK ORDER is not styled for glamour. It is the rifle that expresses Kearney’s standard of honesty, maintainability, and supportability.

Presentation rule:
This should read like the rifle of a man who fixes problems for a living, not a collector flex.

================================================================================
06. BUILD 12 — CIVIL DISOBEDIENCE
================================================================================

Owner / user: Romeo
Function: late-Act-II onward Boston signature rifle
Configuration direction:
- 14.5-inch midlength AR-pattern carbine
- free-float M-LOK rail
- Aimpoint T-2
- SureFire Scout
- Blue Force Gear Vickers sling
- practical furniture
- unsuppressed by default
- no laser by default
- domestic-parts / supportable logic

Meaning:
CIVIL DISOBEDIENCE is not just Romeo’s best rifle. It is the moment Boston itself starts investing in him as an enduring instrument rather than a passing outsider.

Key identity line to protect:
“That one was a rifle you carried in Boston. This one’s a Boston rifle.”

================================================================================
07. BUILD 08 — HELL OR HIGH WATER
================================================================================

Owner / user: Elena Costa
Function: Boston local armed center-of-gravity weapon
Configuration direction:
- old-service-pattern Colt M4A1
- detachable carry handle retained
- iron-sight / carry-handle-forward identity
- visible wear
- tan rubber bands on stock
- green tape on top of stock
- handguard text reading “…HELL OR HIGH WATER…”

Meaning:
HELL OR HIGH WATER should read as a city-kept rifle: older, worn, stubborn, completely trustworthy to the person who knows it.

It should not be prettified into boutique retro aesthetics.

================================================================================
08. THE RIGHT RIFLE — LOCKED HANDOFF SCENE
================================================================================

Placement:
- late Act II
- between Mission 07 and Mission 08
- hinge before harbor / widened shadow-government phase

Function:
- Kearney judges Romeo’s transitional rifle too full of small apologies
- Elena has quietly been part of the decision to build for him
- CIVIL DISOBEDIENCE is handed off only when Romeo is no longer too expensive to build for

Thematic point:
The scene is about trust, supportability, and belonging.
Not a hero-upgrade vending moment.

================================================================================
09. SUPPORTABILITY RULES
================================================================================

Global Boston small-arms rules:
- practical parts logic
- maintainable domestic / common-source component choices where possible
- magazines matter
- optics and mounts should read honest and zeroed, not decorative
- captured weapons are suspect until proven supportable
- older service-pattern weapons remain credible if maintained properly

Write against:
- social-media operator builds
- gratuitous rare-part fetishism
- naming every weapon in ways characters would not
- suppressor / laser creep without mission justification

================================================================================
10. ROLE SEPARATION
================================================================================

Kearney is not:
- a generic vendor
- Van Hult with smaller tools
- a universal procurement switch

Van Hult is not:
- an armorer substitute
- the small-arms continuity node

Elena is not:
- a mascot for Boston armed struggle
- only a cutscene face

Romeo is not:
- carrying a single campaign-agnostic hero gun forever
