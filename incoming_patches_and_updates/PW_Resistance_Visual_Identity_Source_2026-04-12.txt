PROJECT WARRIORS — RESISTANCE VISUAL IDENTITY SOURCE NOTE
2026-04-12

STATUS
------
Thread-derived visual identity note intended as a clean source page for
future narrative, art, UI, environmental storytelling, and promotional use.

CORE DECISION
-------------
The resistance-symbol system is now split across three related marks rather
than one all-purpose emblem.

1) REPUBLIC SHIELD — AFC PRIMARY EMBLEM
---------------------------------------
Definition:
- Main American Freedom Corps emblem

Shape:
- Upright shield silhouette
- Central five-point star
- Rising wedge / chevron from the lower portion

Meaning:
- Protection of civilians
- Defense of American soil / territory
- Active resistance against foreign occupation
- Civic legitimacy rather than generic militia identity

Usage:
- Main AFC symbol
- Organized markings
- Printed materials
- Mission brief references
- Formal resistance surfaces
- Later-stage coordinated liberation visuals

Rules:
- Must survive in one color
- Must be easy to spray quickly
- Avoid ornate detail

2) BREACH STAR — AFC FIELD TAG
------------------------------
Definition:
- Rough field / propaganda-wall variant for fast spray use

Shape:
- Bold five-point star breaking through a horizontal barricade / block shape
- Visible split / wedge showing forced breach

Meaning:
- Aggressive defiance
- Occupation disruption
- Resistance action
- “We were here” / sabotage-claim energy

Usage:
- Propaganda walls
- Sabotage claims
- Fast morale tags
- Improvised field markings
- Quick stenciled surfaces

Rules:
- Simpler and rougher than Republic Shield
- Not the polished formal AFC emblem
- Should read instantly at distance

3) UNION MARK — BOSTON UNDERGROUND LOCAL EMBLEM
-----------------------------------------------
Definition:
- Pre-existing Boston underground symbol that predates Romeo’s deeper AFC integration

Shape:
- Angular industrial bands
- Hidden / implied five-point star in negative space
- Structural / civic / shop-floor geometry rather than heraldic form

Meaning:
- Local urban resistance
- Civic / neighborhood authorship
- Workshop / dockyard / transit / utility-space identity
- Organized Boston underground before full AFC umbrella consolidation

Usage:
- Reclaimed utility vehicles
- Workshop walls
- Barricades
- Safehouses
- Leaflets
- Armor plates
- District-level Boston resistance spaces

Rules:
- Must not look like a simplified Republic Shield
- Should feel older, local, and city-grown
- Not sleek-tech or corporate-futurist

RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN THE MARKS
------------------------------
- Early Boston: Union Mark appears first
- Mid Boston: Union Mark and Republic Shield begin appearing together
- Late Boston: Republic Shield becomes the dominant umbrella emblem while
  Union Mark remains on local vehicles, workshops, barricades, and veteran
  neighborhood strongholds
- Breach Star remains the AFC’s rough quick-action field mark throughout

VISUAL LANGUAGE RULES
---------------------
Do:
- Favor heavy geometry
- Favor stencil readability
- Favor one-color survivability
- Favor quick spray-paint execution
- Use muted red, off-white, and dark navy for polished applications
- Preserve functionality in plain white or off-white spray paint

Do not:
- Use skulls
- Use rifle silhouettes
- Use eagle marks
- Use over-detailed heraldry
- Use mall-ninja / tactical-brand aesthetics
- Overcomplicate the interior geometry

ENVIRONMENTAL / NARRATIVE GUIDANCE
----------------------------------
- Republic Shield is the emblem of a broader American resistance body
- Breach Star is the quick violent visual language of active resistance
- Union Mark is the language of Boston’s own local authorship and survival
- Co-presence of Union Mark and Republic Shield should signal growing
  coordination between local resistance and the wider AFC project
